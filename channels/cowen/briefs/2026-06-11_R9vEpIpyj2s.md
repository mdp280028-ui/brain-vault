## Key points (the distilled video)
1. The realized price is calculated as the realized cap (aggregate value of all coins at the price they were last moved) divided by circulating supply — it represents the average cost basis of the entire Bitcoin network.
2. In every prior bear market — 2011, 2014–15, 2018, and 2020 — Bitcoin dropped below the realized price before the cycle bottom was confirmed; this dip below realized price is framed as a near-necessary condition for a true cycle low.
3. The current realized price sits at approximately $53,000–$54,000. Bitcoin has fallen significantly but has not yet broken below that level, meaning the historical pattern has not yet been satisfied.
4. Cowen acknowledges this would "break some models" if it happened, but invokes the maxim "all models are wrong, some are useful" — he is presenting it as a real possibility, not a certainty.
5. He shows price/realized-price ratio (normalized to 1.0): Bitcoin is currently just above 1.0, meaning it is trading just above its network cost basis but has not breached it.
6. The realized-price framework is consistent with the supply-in-profit/loss crossover discussed in prior videos: both metrics point to a cycle low occurring within roughly 1–4 months of the crossover, not immediately.
7. Historically, when Bitcoin does fall below the realized price, it does not spend much time there — suggesting any dip below ~$53–54K would likely be brief.
8. Multiple models currently conflict: some argue $60K was the cycle low; others (including the terminal price) argue it was not. Cowen leans toward the data that says the low is not yet in.
9. Cowen frames this as consistent with historical midterm-year patterns, where Bitcoin typically bottoms in the second half of the midterm year — mid-2026 timing aligns with that template.
10. He closes by emphasizing his role is to present the data as he sees it, even when the audience wants him to declare a bottom — the data does not yet confirm one.

## Predictions made
- If historical patterns hold, Bitcoin is likely to dip below the realized price (~$53–54K) before the cycle bottom is confirmed
- Cycle bottom likely in the second half of 2026, consistent with midterm-year historical patterns
- Any move below the realized price expected to be brief, not a prolonged stay
