## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen references his prior video on Bitcoin's percentage of supply in profit vs. loss — when those two lines cross, Bitcoin historically bottoms within 1–4 months. That crossover has now occurred in the current cycle.
2. The core new content is a composite on-chain risk metric built by taking ~9 individual on-chain indicators (MVRV Z-score, Puell Multiple, MVRV score, miner cap to thermocap ratio, transaction fees, market cap to thermocap ratio, terminal price, RHODL ratio, supply in profit/loss), normalizing each between 0 and 1, and combining them into a single risk reading.
3. The current composite on-chain risk reading is approximately 0.18–0.198, well below the 0.2 threshold. Historically, Bitcoin spends very little time below 0.2 — this is a rare, low-risk accumulation zone.
4. Historically, sub-0.2 on-chain risk readings occur most commonly during midterm years, which is exactly the current position in the 4-year cycle (2026 is a midterm year).
5. The pattern in every prior bear market: the composite risk metric carves out a multi-month low before recovering. Cowen expects the same to happen this cycle, with the low forming before end of 2026.
6. October 2026 is cited as the most likely month for the cycle bottom, though Cowen acknowledges it could happen at any moment.
7. Cowen draws an extended analogy to the 2019 cycle: Bitcoin topped before QT ended in both 2019 and 2025, there was no rotation into altcoins in either case, and there were three rate cuts in both 2019 and 2025. He frames the current period as a "post-apathetic top digestion phase" analogous to post-2019.
8. He distinguishes between "time-based capitulation" (his current operating thesis — the bear market resolves through grinding sideways time) vs. "price-based capitulation" (a sharp drop below key cost-basis levels). He is open to pivoting to the latter if conditions warrant.
9. The on-chain risk framework deliberately ignores news, geopolitics, macro data, and presidential statements — its value is that the signal emerges from chain behavior alone.
10. Looking beyond the current bear market: if this cycle is the 2019 analogue, the subsequent bull market (post-bottom) could be more euphoric than the last one, since the 2019-equivalent top was relatively apathetic.

## Predictions made
- Bitcoin cycle bottom most likely in October 2026, with the low forming before end of 2026
