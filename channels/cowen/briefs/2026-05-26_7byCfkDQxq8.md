## Key points (the distilled video)
1. The four-year cycle predicts the timing of BTC bottoms, not price levels: lows occurred at end of 2014, 2018, and 2022, and Q4 2026 is the projected next. Cowen's single best guess is October, with a possible earlier local low in June.
2. BTC topped on cycle day 1,162 — within one week of the prior two cycles' tops (days 1,059 and 1,168). The precision of this timing is Cowen's strongest structural argument that the cycle is intact despite "it's dead" narratives.
3. The "topped on apathy not euphoria" criticism is rebutted with S&P 500 history from 1965–1974: the index put in new highs only 12–15% above prior highs (apathetic tops) yet still produced 1–2 year bear markets with up to 50% drawdowns. An investor who bought in 1965 was up only 28% eight years later, yet still lived through a 50% bear market — apathy at the top does not prevent a bear market.
4. The current bear market rally (~35–36% off the low) is weaker, not stronger, than the 2022 midterm bear market rally (~46% off the low), undermining claims that this rally is uniquely impressive or cycle-breaking.
5. Structural parallels to 2018 are precise at every stage: February 2026 low = February 2018 low; higher low in late March/April 2026 = April 2018; early-May 2026 peak = early-May 2018 peak. Dollar support levels mirror each other exactly one order of magnitude apart — ~$6,000 in 2018 vs. ~$60,000 now; higher low ~$6,500 vs. ~$65,000.
6. The 16-week duration of the current rally without a new low is well within historical norms: prior cycles saw 15–25 weeks between lows (21 weeks from June 2022 low, 19–21 weeks across 2018 instances, ~25 weeks from April to October in 2014). Duration alone is not evidence the cycle broke.
7. In every prior cycle (2014, 2018, 2022), Bitcoin rallied to or above the 200-day MA during the bear market and bulls declared the cycle over — the four-year cycle prevailed each time. Cowen sees no basis for treating the current 200-day MA touch differently.
8. Stablecoin dominance (USDT + USDC) just had a weekly close below the 21-week EMA before bouncing back — an exact replay of the 2022 pattern, where false breakdowns gave bulls false confidence before the bear market resumed.
9. Best-case bull scenario still implies a $60K revisit: even if the cycle doesn't fully play out, Cowen says it is "very likely" Bitcoin at minimum returns to $60K to confirm it as support this year — analogous to how $6K held for months in mid-2018 before the final decline.
10. ROI from cycle peak is tracking slightly above the 2014 trajectory (0.62x vs. 0.54x from yearly open at a comparable stage), but the direction and shape remain consistent. The cycle's argument rests on time, not price, and the time required has not yet elapsed.
