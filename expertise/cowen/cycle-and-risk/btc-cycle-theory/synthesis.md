# BTC Cycle Theory

Cowen's framework that Bitcoin and broader risk assets follow an approximately four-year cycle of bull-top, bear-bottom, and accumulation phases. He frames the cycle as macro-driven rather than halving-driven, citing parallel equity-market cycle precedents going back to the 1950s.

## Current Thesis

BTC remains in the late stage of the 2026 bear market, with Cowen's framework unchanged (year outlook still ↓2): the day-count models (day 1333 from-low, day 283 from-peak-to-low vs. prior cycles' 1432/1436 and 364/377/406) continue to point to a bottom roughly 100 days out, around October, and the 2018/2022 seasonal pattern-match (February low, spring higher low, May lower high, June/July low, then a July relief rally given back in August/September) remains his base scaffolding. He now applies that same seasonal logic to altcoins, arguing BTC's midterm-year second-half strategy (DCA, don't try to time the bottom) works reliably but the 2018-vs-2022 fork matters much more for alts like ETH, since low current social participation (~0.25, matching July 2018 rather than the more bullish ~0.5 of July 2022) raises the odds of a deeper, more 2018-like drawdown into the August–October window rather than a gentler 2022-style higher low. He continues to reject macro-indicator-driven forecasting and top-timing in favor of the four-year cycle and its seasonal sub-patterns.

## Key Evidence

- ROI-from-low day-count: cycle on day 1333 vs. prior bottoms at day 1432/1436 (~100 days out, pointing to October, December if the bear runs as long as 2014's); complemented by the ROI-from-peak-to-low chart (day 283 vs. 364/377/406 for prior post-halving-year bears), suggesting more time may still be needed. (oj2n8nkTTTE, sy6AxBbgico, XQN1M-sIuGs)
- 2026's price action pattern-matches 2018/2022 at scale (February low, higher low in March/April, May lower high at the 200-day MA, June/early-July lower low, July relief rally historically given back in August/September); Cowen now extends this same 2018-vs-2022 fork to altcoin behavior, since alts don't always bottom exactly when BTC does. (sy6AxBbgico, 8TmOvGK235I, oj2n8nkTTTE, rxekpAAHwRs, XQN1M-sIuGs)
- Social participation reading of ~0.25 currently matches July 2018 rather than July 2022's ~0.5, reinforcing the more bearish analog across both BTC's cycle framing and Cowen's newly detailed ETH downside case for the August–October window. (XQN1M-sIuGs, OZAQDVkVuG8)
- Every prior bear market bottom (2014, 2018, 2022) featured a volume capitulation spike that reset on-chain metrics; that spike has not yet occurred for BTC, which remains Cowen's primary reason for rejecting any current bottom call (the June price-based-capitulation window passed without one, consistent with time-based capitulation as his base case). (psz9FwqWGns, g4FiHz14W14, 1LI9wfqxOmc, 8TmOvGK235I, XQN1M-sIuGs)
- Cowen's distilled rule — ignore BTC in the first half of midterm years, DCA in the second half, sell at the end of post-halving years — remains undefeated across three cycles; he reiterates that this BTC-specific rule is reliable while altcoin timing in the same window is comparatively "hit or miss." (sy6AxBbgico, psz9FwqWGns, g4FiHz14W14, OYcGIifaO4Q, 1LI9wfqxOmc, 8TmOvGK235I, oj2n8nkTTTE, XQN1M-sIuGs, OZAQDVkVuG8)
