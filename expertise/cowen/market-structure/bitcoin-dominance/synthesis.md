# Bitcoin Dominance

BTC's market-cap share of the total crypto market. Cowen tracks dominance as the primary lens for altcoin-season timing — falling dominance signals capital rotation into alts, rising dominance signals BTC-favoring regime.

## Current Thesis

BTC dominance excluding stablecoins continues making higher highs, and the headline figure is only suppressed by the parallel surge in stablecoin dominance — itself a bear-market signal, not a sign that alts are gaining ground. The same A-B-C-D pattern that drove BTC dominance far higher and longer than expected in prior cycles is now playing out in stablecoin dominance, with both charts structurally confirming a BTC-favoring bear regime. When the next bull market arrives, Cowen expects capital to rotate out of stablecoins into BTC first, causing headline BTC dominance to surge further. The macro backdrop reinforces this: with zero cuts priced through 2027 and a hike more probable than a cut, the liquidity conditions required for sustained altcoin-season flows remain structurally absent.

## Key Evidence

- BTC dominance excluding stablecoins is still making higher highs — the suppression in the headline figure is stablecoin dominance rising, not alts outperforming (mIjviu7Q4ag)
- The A-B-C-D pattern on the BTC dominance chart from prior cycles drove dominance "far longer than most people thought" — Cowen treats this as the direct precedent for where stablecoin dominance (and by extension the BTC-favoring regime) is headed (mIjviu7Q4ag)
- In the 2022 bear market, stablecoin dominance dipped below the 21-week EMA in March, August, and October before bouncing sharply higher each time — current dip-and-recovery mirrors that behavior exactly (mIjviu7Q4ag)
- Total 2 (minus USDT) / BTC ratio shows relentless BTC dominance gains as rate-cut hopes have fully evaporated (Rf-gAQJk5-0)
- Markets have repriced to zero cuts in 2026–2027 with a hike now more likely than a cut in 2027 — eliminating the liquidity catalyst altcoins require (Rf-gAQJk5-0)
- Supply-driven CPI at 3.78% with core reversing higher creates a late-cycle "Fed checkmate" dynamic that structurally delays any return to the liquidity regime that preceded prior altcoin seasons (Rf-gAQJk5-0)
