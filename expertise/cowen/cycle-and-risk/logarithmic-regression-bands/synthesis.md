# Logarithmic Regression Bands

Log-regression bands fitted to Bitcoin's all-time price history that Cowen uses to define long-term support, midline, and resistance trajectories. He treats deviation from the bands as a long-horizon valuation signal and a complement to the risk metric.

## Current Thesis

Cowen uses logarithmic regression bands to frame ETH's long-term valuation across a lower support band, a fair-value midline, and an upper bear market resistance band. Having been rejected at the resistance band in May 2026, he now expects ETH to stall at or below that band for the rest of the year and not durably break above it until 2027, drawing on how the 2022 rejection persisted until January of the following pre-halving/pre-election year. His near-term target path runs toward the lower band / April 2025 low area (~$2,000-2,100), consistent with both the 2019 cycle-analog thesis (fall to lower band, then rally) and his newer framing that a ~40% drop from a July high would land ETH in the same zone. The fair-value midline crossing $2,000 in 2026 continues to validate his long-standing "ETH goes home before a new all-time high" call.

## Key Evidence

- ETH rejected at the bear market resistance band (upper band) in May 2026, confirming the band as meaningful overhead resistance in the current cycle (prior)
- The lower regression band aligns precisely with the April 2025 lows, providing a concrete price target for the anticipated decline; a ~40% drop from a July 2026 high would likewise put ETH around/slightly below that same April 2025 low area (~$2,000-2,100) (prior; OZAQDVkVuG8)
- In 2021, Cowen predicted the regression model's fair value for ETH would not cross $2,000 until 2026; ETH is now trading near that midline level, validating the model's long-range accuracy (prior)
- The 2019 cycle analog: ETH previously fell to the lower regression band before staging a ~240% rally, supporting the lower band as a meaningful cyclical floor (prior)
- Cowen expects ETH to stall at or before the bear market resistance band and not durably break above it until next year (2027), citing the 2022 precedent where rejection persisted until January of the following year (OZAQDVkVuG8)
- A touch of the lower band without a recession materializing remains his explicit trigger for expecting a "sizable counter-trend move" upward (prior)
