# BTC Cycle Theory

Cowen's framework that Bitcoin and broader risk assets follow an approximately four-year cycle of bull-top, bear-bottom, and accumulation phases. He frames the cycle as macro-driven rather than halving-driven, citing parallel equity-market cycle precedents going back to the 1950s.

## Current Thesis

BTC is in Stage 3 of the bear market (June–October 2026), with October 2026 as the most likely cycle bottom month — a target now supported by five independent frameworks converging: cycle-day count, midterm-year classification, supply-in-profit/loss crossover, realized-price gap, and composite on-chain risk. The composite on-chain risk metric (~0.18–0.198) is currently in a historically rare sub-0.2 accumulation zone, which has appeared most commonly in midterm years and always precedes a recovery — strengthening the time-based thesis without requiring a price-based capitulation event. The realized-price condition (~$53–54K) from the prior brief remains an open question: Cowen has not walked it back, and continues to operate under "time-based capitulation" while holding open the possibility of pivoting to "price-based capitulation" if conditions warrant. The behavioral rule is unchanged: the current zone is an accumulation window; the exit target is the 2027–2028 bull phase, which Cowen now frames as potentially more euphoric than the last cycle given the apathetic 2025 top (2019 analogue).

## Key Evidence

- BTC topped on cycle day 1,162 — within one week of the prior two cycles' tops (days 1,059 and 1,168) — making cycle-day timing the single strongest structural argument that the four-year cycle is intact; the same methodology pins the cycle bottom at Oct 19, 2026 (1,428 days from Nov 21, 2022). (7byCfkDQxq8, QMn7cm4nfYU)
- A composite on-chain risk metric aggregating ~9 normalized indicators (MVRV Z-score, Puell Multiple, MVRV score, miner cap to thermocap, transaction fees, market cap to thermocap, terminal price, RHODL ratio, supply in profit/loss) currently reads ~0.18–0.198 — below the historically rare 0.2 threshold — and sub-0.2 readings cluster in midterm years, confirming the current position as a low-risk accumulation zone. (Di8YR9nX8Q8)
- The supply-in-profit vs. supply-in-loss crossover has occurred (June 2026); historically the cycle bottom has never preceded this crossover, and the lag to the actual low has ranged 1–4 months — placing the current bottom window between now and October 2026. (vxnpP3EOl-8, Di8YR9nX8Q8)
- In every prior bear market (2011, 2014–15, 2018, 2020), Bitcoin dipped below the realized price (~$53–54K currently) before the cycle bottom was confirmed; BTC has not yet broken below that level, meaning this near-necessary condition remains unsatisfied. (R9vEpIpyj2s)
- The midterm-year classification formalizes structural bearishness: BTC dropped in H1 of every midterm year (2014, 2018, 2022, 2026), all three prior midterm cycles produced a significant June low, and sub-0.2 on-chain risk readings occur most commonly in midterm years. (dIa8HUYDNEY, QMn7cm4nfYU, Di8YR9nX8Q8)
- Cowen's distilled rule — buy BTC at the end of midterm years, sell at the end of post-halving years — is explicitly stated as the only indicator that ever worked; the bottom is time-based, not price-based. (slTIiS-Y65k, Tj7mvTHDe1U, dIa8HUYDNEY)
