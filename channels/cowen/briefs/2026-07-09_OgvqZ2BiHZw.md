## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin has been range-bound around 60k for a long time with no real movement; Nick expects it to stay range-bound roughly between 62k-68k depending on macro developments.
2. Nick cites several "bottom signal" indicators that have fired: over 50% of on-chain BTC supply held at a loss, monthly RSI at its lowest (or second-lowest) level in 17 years, and an on-chain valuation Z-score near 1.5 standard deviations.
3. Nick's watch list for confirmation of a bottom/recovery: a solid recovery above the 200-week SMA (currently on the cusp), 3-4 days of sustained ETF inflows after a terrible outflow month, and continued strength in the Coinbase premium (indicator of US institutional buying).
4. Macro/geopolitical risk (potential Iran peace deal vs. resumption of Middle East fighting) is seen as the biggest swing factor for BTC direction in the near term; renewed fighting could push oil and the dollar higher.
5. Fed minutes were hawkish — about half of Fed governors reportedly want a rate hike by year-end; concerns raised about demand-driven inflation from AI/data-center buildout (chips, memory, electricity costs) even as oil price pressure has eased somewhat.
6. New York Fed consumer inflation expectations hit their highest level in nearly 3 years, and a recent 10-year Treasury auction had the highest yield since February of last year — both seen as signs the Fed will stay hawkish.
7. Panel flags the Fed reportedly tweaking how it measures PCE inflation (including a "trimmed PCE" measure), viewed skeptically as an attempt to make inflation look lower without actually addressing it.
8. Clarity Act is seen as unlikely to pass this year (or even if Democrats win the House) — the main sticking point is a provision barring officials and family members from trading crypto, which is politically difficult after Trump's ~$1.4 billion in crypto-related profits became a talking point.
9. Extensive discussion of Michael Saylor/Strategy: MSTR's mNAV fell below 1 (a "negative flywheel" problem for stock issuance), Strategy sold Bitcoin (~$200M+ on one day, ~3,000 BTC over two weeks) as part of a monetization plan of up to $1.25B to buy back preferred stock (Stretch/STRC) and MSTR; Strategy still holds 830,000+ BTC. Nick argues ripping off the band-aid and selling is preferable to prolonged overhang; Rob notes optics are bad given Saylor's "never sell" reputation and shared a chart showing the position could reach breakeven infinity with even modest (3.3%) BTC returns over 31 years.
10. Panel contrasts Saylor's selling with Tom Lee continuing to buy Ethereum despite a poor prediction track record, with Cowen suggesting Tom Lee will "probably do great" for buying during the bear market.

## Predictions made
- BTC likely to stay range-bound between roughly 62k-68k over the near term (weeks), per Nick, contingent on macro/geopolitical developments.
- Clarity Act unlikely to pass this year, and unlikely even if Democrats win the House, due to the crypto-trading-ban provision for officials/family.
- Cowen states Strategy (MSTR) will likely underperform Bitcoin over a long enough period of time, partly because it may be forced to sell BTC to pay shareholders.
