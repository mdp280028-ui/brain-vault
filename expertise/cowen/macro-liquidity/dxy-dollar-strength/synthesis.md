# DXY Dollar Strength

The US Dollar Index (DXY) as a counter-indicator for risk assets. Cowen treats sustained DXY weakness as supportive for Bitcoin and SPX, and DXY strength as a headwind that compresses risk-asset valuations.

## Current Thesis

Cowen's directional call remains unchanged: the dollar bottomed in early 2026 (mirroring its Trump-first-term trough in early 2018) and is now "begrudgingly" grinding higher toward the 105–106 area, a headwind he expects to persist for the next 3-4 months of the midterm year before topping out and rising only marginally the following year. He now explicitly frames this eventual topping-out as the setup for the longer-term Bitcoin bull case, while in the nearer term he continues to tie persistent dollar strength to both his expected back-half-2026 crypto weakness and the 10-20% SPX correction he expects in August-September. The political-cycle backdrop (Republican sweep, midterm year) still implies structural dollar softness as a longer-term base case, subordinated to the cyclical analog that has DXY topping at inauguration, troughing roughly a year later, and grinding higher through the midterm year. A new secondary thread — a 40-year-high yen and Bank of Japan rate hikes — adds a related risk-off overhang, with Cowen noting crypto tends to be the first asset class to catch a yen-carry-trade unwind. The potential for a Democratic Congress post-2026 midterms to flip the regime to dollar-supportive remains a watch item.

## Key Evidence

- DXY followed the same cycle template across Trump's first and second terms: topped January 2017 / January 2025, bottomed January–February 2018 / February 2026, then began grinding higher — Cowen has been calling for this dollar rise all year (prior)
- Cowen targets the 105–106 area as the next DXY destination and explicitly frames continued dollar strength as a macro headwind for crypto in the second half of 2026 (prior)
- Republican sweep regimes have historically produced the largest DXY declines since 1980; midterm years are on average weak for the dollar — structural softness remains the longer-term base case even as near-term direction is higher (prior)
- Dollar performance under Trump's first vs. second term tracks similarly (down, bottom, "begrudging" grind higher); Cowen links this persistent-strength path directly to his expected later-year SPX correction (prior)
- Ben reiterates the dollar will "begrudgingly" grind higher through the rest of the midterm year as a crypto headwind for the next 3-4 months, notwithstanding a possible countertrend rally, before topping out and rising only marginally the following year — a path he now frames as the eventual Bitcoin bull case (oj2n8nkTTTE)
- Yen strength at a 40-year high and Bank of Japan rate hikes are flagged as a related risk-off overhang, with crypto tending to be the first asset class to catch a yen-carry-trade-driven move (oj2n8nkTTTE)
