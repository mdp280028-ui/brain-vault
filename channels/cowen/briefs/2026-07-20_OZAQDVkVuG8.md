## Key points (the distilled video)
1. ETH currently trades just below $1,900; Cowen frames the rest of 2026 as a question of which prior midterm-year analog (2018 or 2022) it will follow, since altcoins don't always bottom exactly when Bitcoin bottoms — ETH bottomed before BTC last cycle.
2. Structurally 2026 closely resembles 2018 (same president, BTC low in February, low swept in June, lower high in May) — but in 2018 ETH broke down to new cycle lows in August after failing to hold support, whereas in 2022 ETH printed a higher low when BTC dropped.
3. Buying Bitcoin from July through December in midterm years has historically worked out well, but buying altcoins in that window is "hit or miss" — buying ETH in July 2018 led to an 80% loss by year-end, while buying ETH in July 2022 was close to the cycle bottom.
4. Cowen's base case is "somewhere in between" those two outcomes: he does not expect an 80%+ drop from current levels, but thinks ETH could see something deeper than 2022's pullback (potentially a ~40% drop from a July high, which would mean sweeping/slightly undercutting the April 2025 low near $2,000-2,100).
5. Social participation/interest in crypto is a key risk factor: current social risk reading is ~0.25, matching July 2018 (~0.25) rather than July 2022 (~0.5), suggesting less buying support now than in the more bullish 2022 analog.
6. He does not expect the window of weakness to hit until August, possibly very late July; the biggest window of weakness for ETH is expected in the August–October time frame, with confirmation coming by September/October on whether ETH holds levels like $1,800.
7. On the ETH/BTC pair, price has been tagging the bull market support band roughly every 3 months (mid-January, mid-April, mid-July) and getting rejected; Cowen thinks it could test that support "one last time," partly due to drying social interest and a potential Fed rate hike (or just the threat of one) in the September–October time frame that could shake out higher-risk altcoin positioning against BTC.
8. He's watching the 20-month moving average on ETH/BTC — last cycle ETH didn't durably reclaim it until the election year, and price is currently getting rejected right at that level again.
9. Year-to-date ETH ROI in 2026 currently looks very similar to the same point in 2018 (relative to yearly open), and notably weaker than 2022 at the same point; combining the last two midterm years' patterns produces a similar down-up-down-up-down shape to what 2026 has shown so far.
10. Near-term (July) he isn't overly bearish since July is typically a green month, but he expects ETH to stall out at or before the bear market resistance band, and doesn't expect a durable break above that band until next year (drawing on how in 2022 rejection persisted until January of the pre-halving/pre-election year).

## Predictions made
- ETH's biggest window of weakness expected in the August–October 2026 time frame, tied to Bitcoin's typical August/September midterm-year weakness.
- If ETH drops roughly 40% from a July high, that would put it around/slightly below the April 2025 low (~$2,000-2,100 area if it first rallies to that level).
- Expects ETH to stall at or before the bear market resistance band and not durably break above it until next year (2027).
- Possible Fed rate hike or rate-hike scare in the September–October 2026 time frame that could trigger another altcoin selloff against Bitcoin, potentially setting up a macro higher low on the ETH/BTC pair (possibly sweeping the June 2025 low).
