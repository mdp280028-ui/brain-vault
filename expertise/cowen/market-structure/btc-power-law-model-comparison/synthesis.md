## Current Thesis

Cowen's asymmetric tail curvature quantile model remains his current framework for comparing BTC price models, superseding the regression rainbow and Giovani's power law by fitting lower and upper price tails with mathematically distinct curvature. The model is not static: Cowen has announced a planned refit after end-of-2026 to incorporate full midterm-year data, with the current below-fair-value period in 2026 itself serving as real-world validation of the midterm-year dynamic the refit is meant to capture. At the lower tail, the new model offers no advantage over the simple power law, which Cowen treats as the reliable structural-low benchmark; the claimed advance remains at the upper tail, where diminishing curvature across successive cycle peaks avoids the overshoot characteristic of stock-to-flow and simpler single-curve models. The 2026-07-14 brief does not engage this model framework directly — Cowen's bear-market analysis there runs on fractal/business-cycle comparisons (2018, 2019-2020) rather than the regression/power-law apparatus, so the model comparison thesis carries forward unchanged.
