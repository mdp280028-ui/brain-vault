## Key points (the distilled video)
1. New July Bitcoin cycle memo published on the ITC website; Cowen recommends reading it alongside prior reports since this one builds on them.
2. Bitcoin currently trading around $64,000.
3. ROI-from-low chart: current cycle is on day 1333; the last cycle bottomed on day 1436, and the one before on day 1432 — meaning historically a bottom would be roughly 100 days out from now.
4. ROI-from-peak-to-low chart: currently on day 283 since the cycle peak; the last three bear markets ended (post-halving year) at 364, 377, and 406 days respectively, implying more time may be needed before a low.
5. Election-cycle lens: Bitcoin's return under Trump's second term is tracking closely with Biden's term (summer dip, small rally, giving it back in August/September, bottom in Q4); Trump's second term differs sharply from his first term for Bitcoin, but the Dollar Index (DXY) has behaved similarly across both Trump terms, and dollar strength later this year could remain a headwind for Bitcoin.
6. Comparing 2026 to prior midterm years (2022, 2018, 2014): current price action sits "in the middle" of the historical range, and averaging prior midterm years plus one standard deviation shows Bitcoin often finds relief in July — consistent with what's happening now.
7. Pattern match to 2018/2022: February low, higher low in March/April, lower high in May, lower low in June/early July, followed by a July rally that gets given back in August/September — Cowen says this cycle is following the same script.
8. Compared to 2019: the price-based-capitulation window (which would have hit in June) has already passed without a price-based capitulation, which was never the base case anyway — the base case remains time-based capitulation; a price-based capitulation event (like 2020) would shift his timing expectations for the low.
9. Social interest metric is currently around 0.25 risk, matching July 2018 (~0.25) rather than July 2022 (~0.5) — corroborated by crypto YouTube channel view counts (~350K/day now vs. ~1 million/day in July 2022).
10. Overall framing: market structure most resembles 2019 (due to the cycle top occurring near the end of QT, leaving more time left in the four-year cycle), while social interest most resembles 2018; Cowen reiterates DCA'ing is a better strategy than trying to time the exact bottom.
