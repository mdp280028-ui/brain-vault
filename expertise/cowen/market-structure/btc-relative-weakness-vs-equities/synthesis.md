## Current Thesis

BTC is structurally further down the risk curve than SPX — underparticipating on equity rallies and absorbing harder hits on declines — a dynamic Cowen and his panel frame as both a cycle artifact and a monetary-policy sensitivity story. The midterm-year pattern (bleed through June, counter-trend bounce in July–August, final capitulation low in Q4) mechanically explains BTC's continued underperformance versus equities, gold, and energy in H1 2026, while the macro narrative is that crypto is more dependent on loose monetary policy than equities, and with rate cuts fully priced out and hikes now being priced in, there is no monetary tailwind. The June 9 video adds a fair-value framing to the same underlying call: Cowen expects BTC to spend the rest of 2026 below the logarithmic regression trend line, consistent with the broader thesis that BTC lags SPX and other risk assets through cycle lows — a dynamic he likens to the 2019–2020 business-cycle trough — with outperformance not resuming until 2027 or later.

- BTC is further down the risk curve than SPX: in 2018 and 2022, the second SPX drop was not dramatically lower than the first, but BTC went substantially lower on the second leg — Cowen expects the same in 2026 (Ibh7wDgNGCE).
