## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin has had ample time to make new all-time highs if the supercycle thesis were valid, but it has continued to lag even as the S&P 500 repeatedly put in new all-time highs — Cowen treats this as the supercycle thesis failing in real time.
2. The S&P 500 has now risen for seven to eight consecutive weeks, making a near-term correction increasingly overdue.
3. When that SPX correction comes, Bitcoin will likely be hit even harder than equities precisely because it has already been lagging — the weakest asset on the way up gets punished most on the way down.
4. Cowen frames this through a risk-curve hierarchy: altcoin holders in 2023–2025 experienced the same dynamic against Bitcoin — alts would lift when Bitcoin rose, but when Bitcoin dropped, alts were "annihilated."
5. Bitcoin is now in that same subordinate position relative to the S&P: it participates in equity rallies but, being in a bear market, gets hit harder whenever equities pull back.

## Predictions made
- When the coming SPX small correction arrives (implied near-term, given 7–8 consecutive up weeks), Bitcoin will be hit harder than the S&P due to its lagging position and risk-curve placement.
