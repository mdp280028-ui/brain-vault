# On-Chain Metrics

Bitcoin and Ethereum on-chain indicators including HODL waves, supply-shock metrics, exchange flows, miner behavior, and realized-price bands. Cowen integrates on-chain data with macro and price signals rather than treating it in isolation.

## Current Thesis

On-chain metrics confirm Bitcoin is in a late-bear phase with the MVRV Z-Score now at ~0.35 and approaching the zero threshold — a level that has historically marked multi-month accumulation windows preceding new bull markets. Cowen's view has incrementally shifted from "not yet at a bottom" to "approaching the zone where patient accumulation has historically paid off," with the MVRV Z-Score reset serving as the primary timing signal. Supporting indicators (composite on-chain risk at 0.216, 1-year ROI at 0.589, realized price ~$53.8K) remain consistent with late-bear conditions but have not yet fully capitulated. Cowen continues to treat on-chain data as one confirming tool within his broader macro/cycle framework, not a standalone trigger.

## Key Evidence

- MVRV Z-Score at ~0.35 and approaching zero — historically every major bull market has been preceded by a reset below zero, with prior below-zero periods lasting 5 months to over a year (hXlR5xu0HK4)
- Historical cycle peaks have been lower each cycle (~10 in 2013, ~7 in 2021, ~3 most recently), suggesting the current cycle's top is behind us and the reset phase is underway (hXlR5xu0HK4)
- Composite on-chain risk indicator at 0.216, needs to approach 0.1 to signal cycle-low conditions; components include pure multiple, MVRV score, MVRV Z-score, transaction fees, terminal price, and thermocap ratios (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- Running 1-year ROI at 0.589; prior cycle lows have required a reading below ~0.3–0.4 (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- Realized price (~$53.8K) as a mid-bear confidence level: trading below it would strengthen conviction that the low is in; balance price (~$38.65K) historically marks cycle-low territory (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- Bitcoin crossed below Q1 (1st percentile floor) on the asymmetric quantile regression model, with key dislocation levels at ~$58K, ~$52K, ~$48K, and ~$40K corresponding to prior crisis events (QMn7cm4nfYU)
