## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen frames July of mid-term years as a recurring "brief window of strength" within a broader mid-term-year "window of weakness," pointing to June 2018 and June 2022 as prior examples of a low forming in late June/early July.
2. In both 2018 and 2022, a counter-move rallied into late July/mid-August before topping out and giving back gains into August/September, with 2018's rally rejected at the 21-week EMA/20-week SMA and 200-day moving average.
3. Historical July monthly returns: Bitcoin was up ~19-20% in July 2022, ~38% in July 2018, and down ~5% in July 2014; the average July return going back to 2012 is about +9% (+/- 15-16%), narrowing to +8.2% (+/- 13.6%) using data from 2014 onward.
4. He notes that when July is red (as in 2014), August and September also tended to stay red, but when July is green (2018, 2022), August/September still turned red afterward.
5. References a same-day news item that Strategy sold some more Bitcoin, causing a brief wick down that was quickly recovered, which he says demonstrates the seasonal strength is present.
6. Draws a direct analogy between the current rally off ~$57,000 and Bitcoin's 2018 rally off $5,700, which topped near 6,800 before pulling back to form a higher low in mid-July, then swept that low in August.
7. Says the 200-day moving average is currently around 74K but declining quickly; he expects it near/just north of 70K by late July and around 70K by mid-August, calling it a likely target/rejection zone for the current bounce.
8. Describes Bitcoin as stuck "between a rock and a hard place" — between the 200-week moving average and a bear market resistance band — and expects it will be forced to make a decision later this year.
9. Reiterates his recommended strategy is DCA-ing Bitcoin in the second half of mid-term years rather than trying to time exact tops/bottoms, warning that people who fight the cycle (selling lows, buying local tops) get caught in repeated "mental gymnastics" and toxic-emotion cycles.
10. Expects the four-year cycle to complete with a market-cycle bottom later this year (Q4-type timing implied by 2022 comparison), after which a new four-year cycle would begin.

## Predictions made
- BTC's current rally (off ~$57,000) likely stalls out similar to 2018/2022 patterns, with the 200-day moving average — projected to fall to just north of 70K by late July and to ~70K by mid-August — acting as a likely resistance/rejection zone.
- Market-cycle bottom for this four-year cycle expected to form later in 2026 (following the seasonal pattern of prior cycles bottoming in Q4).
