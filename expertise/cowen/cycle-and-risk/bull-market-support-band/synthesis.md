# Bull Market Support Band

A combined indicator (20-week SMA + 21-week EMA) Cowen treats as the bull/bear regime line for Bitcoin. Sustained closes below it signal bear-market continuation; reclaims signal regime shifts. Also applied to SPX and select altcoins.

## Current Thesis

The bear market resistance band (~$70–74K) remains the defining ceiling of the current bear regime, and BTC has now confirmed the downside break Cowen anticipated — posting its first weekly close below the 200-week MA this cycle, removing even that floor as support. With BTC now below both the band and the 200-week MA, Cowen's roadmap is unchanged in structure but lower in price: a near-term low in early summer, a countertrend rally into mid-to-late summer (likely targeting the band or 200-week MA from below as resistance), then a final leg down to the cycle bottom in late Q3 or early Q4. Any summer rally is still explicitly a within-bear bounce, not a regime shift, and a reclaim of the bull market support band would be required to change that view. For gold (monthly timeframe, 20-month MA / 21-month EMA), the band continues to play the opposite role — structural bull-market support expected to hold during the midterm-year correction.

## Key Evidence

- Bitcoin rejected at the 20-week SMA / 21-week EMA band in the current midterm year, matching the pattern from 2014, 2018, 2019, and 2022 midterm bear cycles (dy3E7Jzte88)
- The BTC/SPX ratio was also rejected at the same 20-week SMA / 21-week EMA level, extending the bear-regime signal beyond spot BTC to relative performance (dy3E7Jzte88)
- BTC posted its first weekly close below the 200-week MA this cycle in late June 2026, mirroring June 2022 and June 2018; the bear market resistance band now sits as overhead resistance above both the 200-week MA and spot price (1LI9wfqxOmc)
- The 2026 price structure (February low, higher low late March/early April, lower low in June) is a near-exact repeat of 2018, reinforcing the band's continued bear-resistance role with an analog target near ~$60K (1LI9wfqxOmc)
- The recent BTC rally produced minimal follow-through before retesting the band — far less than 2023 — matching the classic midterm fake-out pattern (slTIiS-Y65k)
- For gold, the 20-month MA / 21-month EMA band held as support throughout the 1970s and 2000s bull markets and is expected to provide support again during the current ~28% midterm-year correction (DoteyQFNfnE)
