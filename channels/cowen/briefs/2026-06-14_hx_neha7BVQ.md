## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen distinguishes between "being right" (timing the exact bottom) and "making money" (taking action at low-risk levels and letting the trend work). His core argument is that most profits come from trading the trend, not from nailing the precise low.
2. The current market environment closely mirrors 2019: Bitcoin topped on apathy rather than euphoria, there was no altcoin rotation, Bitcoin topped roughly two months before quantitative tightening began, and the Fed cut rates three times in both 2019 and 2025.
3. The foundational tool is a Bitcoin risk metric (developed ~six years ago, published as "Bitcoin Risk Analysis Using Machine Learning") that scores Bitcoin's current valuation on a 0-to-1 scale, accounting for diminishing returns across cycles. Current reading: 0.296.
4. Cowen's dynamic DCA buying scheme for this cycle is scaled to the risk metric: $100 base between 0.2–0.3 risk, $200 between 0.1–0.2, $300 between 0–0.1. He does not buy above 0.3 risk this cycle (more risk-averse than prior cycles where he bought up to 0.4 or 0.5).
5. The selling framework mirrors the buy side: he sells nothing between his buy ceiling and 0.6 risk (a "do nothing" gray zone), then scales out in fifths above 0.6, with the largest tranche (5/15ths) sold between 0.9–1.0 risk.
6. A simulation on his DCA tool shows that equal-weight DCA of $30/week since 2014 (total invested: $18.3K) would produce a portfolio worth ~$700K today; the same total invested via dynamic DCA (buying only below 0.3 risk, with scaled amounts) produces ~$2.1 million — roughly 3x the outcome.
7. Bitcoin historically spends only 14.73% of its days in the 0.2–0.3 risk band, 12.5% in the 0.1–0.2 band, and just 2.34% (135 days total across its entire history) in the 0–0.1 band. These windows are rare and tend to close quickly, which is why dynamic DCA rewards patience.
8. An upgraded "summary risk" metric combines price risk, on-chain risk (MVRV, MVRV-Z, puell multiple, terminal price, miner cap to thermocap ratio, transaction fees), and social risk (Google Trends, Coinbase app ranking, YouTube/Twitter metrics). Current summary risk is at the same level as June 2022, but social risk is now lower than in 2022 — meaning apathy is deeper this time.
9. Cowen did not buy when Bitcoin briefly dipped below 0.3 risk in February because he applied "time-based capitulation" — he judged it too early in the midterm year, and the supply P&L crossover had not yet occurred. He is now actively DCAing as the second half of the midterm year approaches.
10. October remains his modal expectation for the cycle low, but he would pivot immediately if risk dropped to 0.1 — he is not dogmatically waiting. He also acknowledges it's possible the June low is already in, and even at 0.3 risk, prices today will likely look cheap in hindsight within a few years.

## Predictions made
- Bitcoin cycle bottom most likely in October 2026, though it could occur sooner or later; Cowen states he would pivot immediately if risk dropped to 0.1
- Bitcoin likely finds at minimum a June low in mid-2026, analogous to the June 2018 low, before potentially going lower
- If Bitcoin breaks below the realized price (currently in the $50Ks), that would not historically be a bad time to begin DCAing

## Tools/indicators referenced
- Bitcoin Risk Metric (price-based, accounts for diminishing returns across cycles; originally published ~2020)
- Summary Risk Metric (composite of price risk, on-chain risk, and social risk)
- On-chain components: Puell Multiple, MVRV, MVRV-Z Score, transaction fees, terminal price, market cap to thermocap ratio, miner cap to thermocap ratio
- Social risk components: Google Trends, Coinbase app store ranking, YouTube subscriber/view counts for crypto analysts, Twitter/X follower counts for analysts/exchanges/layer-1s
- Supply in profit and loss crossover (realized price crossover signal)
- Realized price
- Fear & Greed Index
- Bitcoin DCA simulation tool (available at intothecryptverse.com)
- Best day to DCA chart (7-day SMA extension by day of week; Monday is historically best for BTC)
