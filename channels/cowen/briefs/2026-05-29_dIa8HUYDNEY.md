## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin is at ~$73K, down from ~$83K a few weeks ago; the rejection occurred precisely at the 200-day moving average — the same level that served as resistance in 2018 and 2022.
2. Cowen structures the Bitcoin four-year cycle into four year-types: post-halving years (2013/17/21/25), election years (2012/16/20/24), pre-election years (2011/15/19/23), and midterm years (2014/18/22/26) — the midterm year is the primary window of weakness for the entire cycle.
3. Bitcoin has dropped in the first half of every midterm year (H1 2014, 2018, 2022, and now 2026), making H1 counter-trend rallies traps rather than buying opportunities; seasonality is right roughly 70% of the time.
4. Within each midterm year, lows cluster at predictable intervals: end of the prior post-halving year, February, April (or very close), and June — all three prior midterm cycles (2014, 2018, 2022) saw a significant low in June.
5. The February 2026 low and the higher low in late March (just before April) have both already printed, matching the 2018 pattern almost exactly; the only meaningful difference is 2026 has less downside volatility because BTC did not have a euphoric blow-off top into the cycle peak.
6. The Bank of Japan — not the Fed — is the key mid-June catalyst: Cowen assigns a "good chance" (not certainty) of a BoJ rate hike, and notes BTC bottomed roughly one week after the July 2024 BoJ hike.
7. After the anticipated June low, Cowen expects a counter-trend rally in July (early Q3), mirroring the July 2018 and July 2022 rallies — explicitly flagged as not tradable and not the actual cycle low.
8. The final bear market low is expected in Q4 2026 (September–October timeframe), after the July rally fades; Cowen states there has "always" been a Q4 capitulation in midterm years.
9. A possible scenario: BTC drops toward the 200-day EMA or sweeps the prior low (potentially to ~$58K), bounces back toward $70–80K in July, then makes the ultimate low in Q4 before the bull market begins — but the Q4 outcome cannot be confirmed until we are actually in Q4.
10. Core behavioral advice: ignore Bitcoin in H1 of midterm years, fade every rally as a fake low, and only start looking for real entry points in H2 — "you don't have to buy the bottom to make money."
