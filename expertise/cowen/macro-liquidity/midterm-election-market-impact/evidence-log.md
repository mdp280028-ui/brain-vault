# Evidence log: Midterm Election Market Impact

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

He flags the current Republican sweep as likely to transition post-2026 into the most bearish equity regime (Republican president + Democratic Congress, avg 4.9%), where most historical recessions have occurred. [23:00] "You can see the midterm year on average is -65% on average. What has Bitcoin done so far this year? Well, it's down. About 7%."

- 2026-05-30 (uFn3KUE-VTI): Cowen's new asymmetric quantile model shows Bitcoin has broken below its 1st-percentile structural support (Q1) in every major cycle, with the 2022 instance occurring specifically in the midterm year — reinforcing the midterm-as-reset-window thesis. At ~185 weeks since the 2022 Q1 event, the current cycle sits within the historical inter-dislocation range (139–238 weeks), making a late-2026 sub-Q1 break plausible. Conditional downside floors are mapped to historical analog depths: ~$57–58K (2022), ~$51–52K (March 2020), and ~$48–49K (2015). Cowen states directly: [29:01] "if later this year you're looking at Bitcoin and it's below say Q1 and it's late midterm year, just consider that maybe this time's not different."
