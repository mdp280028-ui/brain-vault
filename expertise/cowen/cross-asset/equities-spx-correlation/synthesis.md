# Equities & SPX Correlation

SPX (S&P 500) price action, structural corrections, and its correlation with Bitcoin. Cowen treats SPX as the dominant macro asset crypto correlates with during risk-off events, and the four-year cycle as shared across both; SPX cycle-tops and major corrections often coincide with crypto-cycle inflections.

## Current Thesis

SPX has already logged its first major correction in Q1 2026 and Cowen expects a second, larger correction in Q3 or Q4 2026, making the full-year structure analogous to the 2018 stock market pattern where a September top preceded a December crash that coincided with Bitcoin's collapse to ~$3,100. The four-year cycle underpinning this view is not Bitcoin-specific — the S&P 500 has bottomed approximately every four years since the 1950s, which Cowen uses as evidence that the cycle is a shared macro rhythm rather than a halving artifact. Resurging inflation (CPI trending up YoY, PPI spiking sharply) and structurally higher oil prices close the door on a Fed pivot, removing the catalyst that would end the correction prematurely. SPX and BTC are expected to find their cycle lows in tandem in Q4 2026.

## Key Evidence

- SPX has bottomed approximately every four years since at least 1958 (1958, 1962, 1966, 1970 ... 2014, 2018, 2022), establishing the four-year cycle as a macro-structural feature independent of any crypto-specific driver. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- The 2018 analogue is explicit: SPX topped in September 2018, crashed into December, and Bitcoin simultaneously collapsed to ~$3,100 — Cowen maps the 2026 SPX double-correction onto this same structure. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- SPX already logged its Q1 2026 correction; Cowen rates a second correction in Q3/Q4 2026 as the base case with conviction 3 on both the year and mid-term timeframes. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- CPI is trending back up YoY and PPI month-over-month is spiking (described as approximately 6%), removing the Fed's room to cut and sustaining the macro headwind for risk assets including equities. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Oil's 21-week EMA has already provided support once and longer-term MAs are catching up, raising the risk of another oil surge later in 2026 that would further entrench the no-pivot environment. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- In Cowen's own portfolio, energy, metals, manufacturing, international stocks, and emerging markets are all currently outperforming Bitcoin — consistent with a late-cycle risk-off rotation away from high-beta assets. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
