# Fed Policy & Rates

The Federal Reserve's policy-rate decisions, FOMC meeting cycles, rate-cut and rate-hike paths, and the politics around Fed leadership transitions (Powell, Warsh, successors). Cowen treats Fed policy as the primary macro driver for crypto and equities.

## Current Thesis

With Powell gone and Kevin Warsh at the helm, the Fed faces a near-impossible mandate: political pressure to cut rates is intensifying while CPI is trending back up year-over-year and PPI month-over-month is spiking sharply (~6% recent print). Warsh holds only one FOMC vote and cannot unilaterally pivot — any cut requires full committee consensus that the inflation data currently does not support. Cowen's base case is rates hold steady through summer 2026, with a meaningful risk of hikes beginning in 2027, making prediction-market pricing of >50% cut probability within 12 months look misplaced. The Fed's inability to pivot is the primary macro headwind driving his bearish outlook on BTC and SPX into late 2026.

## Key Evidence

- Kevin Warsh replaces Powell but commands only one FOMC vote; a rate cut requires full committee consensus, meaning political pressure alone cannot force a pivot (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- CPI is trending back up year-over-year and PPI month-over-month printed approximately 6%, directly undermining the cut case (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Oil prices are structurally higher for the rest of 2026 with risk of another surge as longer-term moving averages catch up to spot — a second oil spike would close the door on any Fed pivot (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Two scenarios both end in market pain: (1) inflation stays hot, no cuts possible, hikes arrive in 2027; (2) something breaks, forcing consensus cuts — but that consensus arrives only as markets are falling apart (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Prediction markets showing >50% probability of a cut within 12 months are viewed skeptically given the inflation trajectory (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Powell's legacy marked by failure to hit the 2% target in five years, raising the hard-landing risk from the full hike-then-cut cycle (FgxAe_NAh5c)
