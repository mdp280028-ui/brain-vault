# Fed Policy & Rates

The Federal Reserve's policy-rate decisions, FOMC meeting cycles, rate-cut and rate-hike paths, and the politics around Fed leadership transitions (Powell, Warsh, successors). Cowen treats Fed policy as the primary macro driver for crypto and equities.

## Current Thesis

The Fed under Warsh faces a structural paradox that has hardened since May: Warsh is acutely aware of Powell's "transitory" reputational blunder and will not want to underestimate inflation at the start of his tenure — yet imminent oil-shortage inflation (strategic reserves depleting, real shortages not yet in official data) is about to hit consumers harder, making rate hikes the most likely policy response once that spike lands. This reinforces the "checkmate" framing established in May: headline CPI at 3.78% with core at 2.74% and reversing upward has already forced markets to fully price out cuts through 2027 and price in a hike, and the oil-shortage channel adds a second, independent inflation driver that closes any remaining window for easing. A longer-term structural risk has also crystallized: 30-year bond yields are at or above their October 2023 highs, and Cowen raises the possibility that the decades-long era of falling rates has ended — a regime shift that would be most damaging to zombie companies and high-risk assets. The "something breaks" forced-cut scenario remains a tail, not a base case, arriving only via a post-midterm recession that is itself a function of the bearish Republican president + Democratic Congress political configuration.

## Key Evidence

- CPI rose to 3.78% YoY (above 3.6–3.7% consensus), led by housing (3.37% → 3.63%) and transportation (6.89%); core CPI at 2.74% reversed its February trough of 2.47%, moving away from — not toward — the 2% target (Rf-gAQJk5-0)
- Markets have fully repriced: no cuts priced for 2026 or 2027; a 2027 hike is now more likely than a cut — a 180° reversal from the multi-cut path priced at year-start (Rf-gAQJk5-0)
- Strategic petroleum reserves are being drawn down to buffer the Hormuz/oil-shortage shock; once exhausted, real shortages of oil, naphtha, and fertilizer will hit consumers and official data, delivering an inflation spike Warsh's Fed will respond to with hikes (8PQee-onXrU)
- Warsh's reputational incentive: he will not risk a Powell-style "transitory" misjudgement at the start of his tenure; if real-time inflation tools (e.g. Trueflation) surface worse rent inflation than lagging surveys, the policy response could be more aggressive than markets expect (8PQee-onXrU)
- 30-year Treasury yields at or above October 2023 highs; Cowen raises the structural possibility that the 30–40-year era of falling rates has ended — a secular regime shift most damaging to zombie companies and high-risk assets (8PQee-onXrU)
- Post-midterm political configuration (Republican president + Democratic Congress) is historically the most bearish equity regime and the primary setting for business-cycle recessions under Republican presidents — the structural trigger for any "something breaks" forced-cut tail scenario (9a-LA7tm8Mo)
