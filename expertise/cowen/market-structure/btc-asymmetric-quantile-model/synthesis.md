## Current Thesis

Cowen's asymmetric tail curvature quantile model fits Bitcoin's lower and upper price tails with mathematically distinct curvature, superseding his earlier regression rainbow and non-rigorous quantile attempts. As of June 2026, BTC has crossed below Q1 (~$62K), placing it in the historical dislocation zone with conditional bear-case floors mapped at ~$58K, ~$52K, ~$48K, and ~$40K. The realized price (~$53–54K) — which sits inside that floor sequence and which BTC has not yet breached — continues to function as the model's primary unmet condition for cycle-bottom confirmation, a point Cowen reiterated on June 22 by noting that Bitcoin "normally goes below it in bear markets" and simply hasn't yet. Cowen's base case remains that BTC will eventually break lower to test the deeper analog floors, with countertrend rallies possible in the interim but not altering the directional thesis.
