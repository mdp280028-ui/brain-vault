## Key points (the distilled video)
1. The chart Cowen is presenting shows the percentage of Bitcoin supply in profit (top line) versus the percentage in loss; in a bull market the profit line reaches 100%, while in bear markets the two lines converge and eventually cross.
2. The core rule: historically, the market cycle bottom has never occurred *before* the supply-in-profit and supply-in-loss lines cross — every single past cycle low came only after that crossover event.
3. The crossing has now occurred as of the video date (June 2026), which Cowen frames as the critical permission condition for calling a cycle bottom — prior to this cross, he said all rallies should be treated as counter-trend and faded.
4. Historical timing of the lag between crossover and actual cycle low: 2011 — crossed September, low end of year (~3 months); 2014 — crossed September, low January (~4 months); 2018 — crossed November, low December (~1 month); 2022 — first crossed June (briefly), crossed again September, low November.
5. Based on that historical range of 1–4 months post-cross, Cowen estimates the market cycle bottom is likely somewhere between now and July–October 2026. He explicitly notes the low is "usually not right when they cross."
6. He raises the 2022 analog as a risk scenario: in that cycle the lines uncrossed briefly after first crossing in June, then crossed again in September before the November low — the same could happen here.
7. Supply-in-loss peaked at varying levels across cycles: ~64% in 2011, ~60% in the next cycle, ~55% in the last cycle, and currently around 51% — Cowen uses this to caution against expecting an extreme reading; the current level is already in a historically normal range.
8. Preferred strategy: start DCA-ing Bitcoin after the June low and maintain that cadence, accepting that Q4 might inflict short-term pain, but with the expectation that the subsequent bull market (2027–2028) renders that drawdown irrelevant — he cites his own experience buying in summer 2022 and summer 2018 as personal examples.
9. Cowen reiterates strong conviction in the four-year Bitcoin cycle, calling it "so obvious and clear" and expressing puzzlement that people continue to fade it; he expects the biggest current critics to become the biggest cheerleaders later in the year.
10. He closes by projecting that the current crossed-over period will likely last only a few months, after which the 2027–2028 bull phase should resume.

## Predictions made
