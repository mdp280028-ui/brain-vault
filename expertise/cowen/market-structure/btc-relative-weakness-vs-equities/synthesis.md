## Current Thesis

BTC is structurally further down the risk curve than SPX — underparticipating on equity rallies and absorbing harder hits on declines — a dynamic Cowen and his panel frame as both a cycle artifact and a monetary-policy sensitivity story. The midterm-year pattern (bleed through June, counter-trend bounce in July–August, final capitulation low in Q4) mechanically explains BTC's continued underperformance versus equities, gold, and energy in H1 2026, while the macro narrative is that crypto is more dependent on loose monetary policy than equities, and with rate cuts fully priced out and hikes now being priced in, there is no monetary tailwind. BTC's failure to make new ATHs despite sustained SPX strength falsifies the supercycle thesis, and the BTC/SPX ratio's rejection at the 20-week SMA / 21-week EMA bear market resistance band confirms structural weakness. Cowen expects BTC to lag SPX for months, with the cycle low in Q4 2026 and outperformance unlikely before 2027.
