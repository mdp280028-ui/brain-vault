# Business Cycle Phase

The position of the US economy within its expansion-peak-contraction-trough business cycle, including Cowen's M2-normalized composite (SPX, unemployment, rates, inflation, M2). Cowen overlays the business cycle with Bitcoin's four-year cycle to time long-horizon allocation, treating cycle-bottoms as accumulation windows.

## Current Thesis

Cowen maintains a late-cycle macro framework — inflation persistently above target, the Fed under Warsh tilted toward hikes, and real disposable income turning negative — but continues to treat the Bitcoin four-year cycle as the dominant, more reliable signal for timing. He now frames the current bounce off ~$57K as a historically typical "brief window of strength" within an otherwise weak mid-term year, directly analogous to June/July rallies in 2018 and 2022 that stalled and gave back gains into August/September; he expects this rally likewise to stall near the declining 200-day moving average (projected ~70K by mid-August) before the four-year cycle completes with a final market-cycle bottom later in 2026. He describes BTC as caught "between a rock and a hard place" — the 200-week moving average and a bear-market resistance band — forced to resolve one way or the other later this year. He continues to soften macro's predictive weight for cycle timing while keeping it as backdrop, and reiterates DCA through the second half of midterm years as the strategy rather than timing the exact bottom, warning that fighting the cycle leads to toxic, repeated "mental gymnastics."

## Key Evidence

- BTC's ~$57.7K early-summer low is a near-exact 10x match to 2018's $5,743 low, sharing a February low, higher low in spring, May rejection at the 200-day MA, and a June/July sweep lower — reinforcing the four-year-cycle analog ("Getting a rally off of 57,000 just like we got one off of 5,700 back in 2018." — n9yTkx3r904 [10:47]).
- July of mid-term years shows a recurring "brief window of strength" pattern: average July return since 2012 is about +9% (±15-16%), narrowing to +8.2% (±13.6%) from 2014 onward, with 2018 (+38%), 2022 (+19-20%), and 2014 (-5%) as the key precedent months, though red Julys (2014) tend to stay red into August/September while green Julys (2018, 2022) still turned red afterward (n9yTkx3r904).
- The 200-day moving average, currently ~74K, is falling quickly and is projected to be just north of 70K by late July and ~70K by mid-August — Cowen's expected rejection/resistance zone for the current bounce, echoing 2018's rejection at the 21-week EMA/20-week SMA/200-day MA (n9yTkx3r904 [06:29]).
- Using ROI-measured-from-low-to-low, Bitcoin is on "day 1331" of the cycle, and the last two cycles bottomed about 100 days (~3 months) from a comparable point, implying a late September/October bottom (oj2n8nkTTTE).
- Cowen distinguishes time-based capitulation (his base case: a grinding, months-long bear market) from price-based capitulation (a sudden violent event like 2020 or 2022 FTX/Luna), and says no pandemic-style price-based capitulation signs have appeared yet (8TmOvGK235I).
- Inflation has been above the 2% target every month since 2021, and under Warsh's Fed, ~84.5% market-implied odds exist of at least one hike by December 2026, a headwind that could coincide with capitulation (prior).
