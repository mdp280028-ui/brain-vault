# Social Engagement Risk

Social-media engagement metrics (mentions, Google Trends, sentiment indices) used as a contrarian signal. High retail engagement coincides with cyclical tops; apathy coincides with cyclical bottoms in Cowen's framework.

## Current Thesis
Cowen continues to read social/retail engagement as subdued, with his social interest/risk metric holding at ~0.25 — matching July 2018 rather than the hotter July 2022 (~0.5) reading. For Bitcoin, he still frames this apathy as consistent with a "not yet topped" mid-cycle backdrop rather than a cyclical top. Applied to Ethereum specifically, however, he now treats the same low reading as a warning sign rather than reassurance: with social participation this thin, there's less buying support to cushion ETH, raising the odds of a deeper drawdown (worse than 2022's higher-low bounce, though not an 80%-style 2018 collapse) into the August–October window. He also ties drying social interest to a potential ETH/BTC pair breakdown if a Fed rate-hike scare hits in September–October.

## Key Evidence
- Social interest/risk metric currently ~0.25, matching July 2018 levels rather than July 2022's ~0.5 reading (prior; reaffirmed OZAQDVkVuG8)
- Crypto YouTube channel view counts ~350K/day now vs. ~1 million/day in July 2022, used as a corroborating proxy for retail engagement (prior)
- Cowen explicitly separates two lenses: overall market structure resembles 2019, but social interest specifically resembles 2018 (prior)
- Low social participation cited as a specific reason ETH's drawdown could exceed 2022's pullback, since there's less retail buying support to hold levels (OZAQDVkVuG8)
- Drying social interest, combined with a possible Fed rate hike/scare in September–October, flagged as a catalyst for one more test of the ETH/BTC bull market support band (OZAQDVkVuG8)
