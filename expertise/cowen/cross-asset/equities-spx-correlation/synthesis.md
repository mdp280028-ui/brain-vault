# Equities & SPX Correlation

SPX (S&P 500) price action, structural corrections, and its correlation with Bitcoin. Cowen treats SPX as the dominant macro asset crypto correlates with during risk-off events, and the four-year cycle as shared across both; SPX cycle-tops and major corrections often coincide with crypto-cycle inflections.

## Current Thesis

Cowen's base case for SPX in 2026 is a two-drop structure: a smaller correction in June-July, a rebound, then a larger drop beginning in September (possibly August) extending into early Q4 — with the S&P/M2 fractal in time specifically pinning September as the larger-drop window. The 2018 and 2022 midterm-year templates both exhibit this double-dip pattern, and the fractal confirmed itself by correctly mapping the recent ~10% drop. Cowen explicitly hedges that the fractal could break to the upside first and wipe out bears before the larger decline materializes, but the directional base case remains bearish for H2. Bitcoin is now positioned further down the risk curve than SPX: it fails to participate in SPX rallies to new ATHs but gets hit harder on SPX drops, and a September SPX top could catalyze a BTC cycle low as early as October 2026 — consistent with his four-year cycle framework and the pessimistic business-cycle overlay introduced in May 2026.

## Key Evidence

- The S&P/M2 fractal overlaid from 1996 has correctly mapped both the 2023 ~10% correction and the recent ~10% drop; the fractal in time specifically points to September 2026 for the larger decline. (APCjkL5gfFI, M5d5fbiSfhc, Ibh7wDgNGCE)
- Midterm-year historical pattern supports the two-drop structure: 2022 had a large early-year drop then a second major low late in the year; 2018 had a large February drop then an even larger Q4 drop; late 2014 also had a notable drop. (Ibh7wDgNGCE)
- The 2018 analogue is explicit: SPX topped in September 2018, crashed into December, and Bitcoin simultaneously collapsed to ~$3,100 — in 2018 the second SPX drop was not dramatically lower than the first, but Bitcoin went substantially lower on the second leg. Cowen maps the same divergence onto 2026. (FgxAe_NAh5c, APCjkL5gfFI, Ibh7wDgNGCE)
- SPX has bottomed approximately every four years since at least 1958, establishing the four-year cycle as a macro-structural feature shared with Bitcoin. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- CPI is trending back up YoY and PPI month-over-month spiking (~6%), removing the Fed's room to cut and sustaining the macro headwind for risk assets into H2 2026. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Cowen's pessimistic overlay: if the broader business cycle ends in late 2026, an SPX bear market could extinguish Bitcoin's bull entirely rather than merely triggering a corrective leg — mirroring how BTC's own bear market ended altcoin season in Q4 2025. (M5d5fbiSfhc)
