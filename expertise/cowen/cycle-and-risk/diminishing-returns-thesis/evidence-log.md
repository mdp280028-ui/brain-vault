# Evidence log: Diminishing Returns Thesis

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-07 (PnveYgxtd1w): Cowen observes that BTC has trailed the S&P 500, Nasdaq, gold, and energy on a year-to-date basis, and that its recent price recovery amounts to nothing more than returning to where it stood a couple of months ago — no new net gains. He frames the asset as "undervalued" in a comparative sense but explicitly does not expect the market to close that gap within the current cycle, stating: [00:22] "I would expect this to stay undervalued for the rest of the year, and then perhaps in the next cycle we'll see it go back up." This cycle-deferral language is consistent with the diminishing-returns thesis: the current cycle is simply not generating the outsized relative returns that prior cycles did.

- 2026-05-26 (CNiGShpj3M0): Cowen defends the four-year cycle against "it's dead" claims by comparing current ROI-from-yearly-open (0.62) to the equivalent 2014 reading (0.54), treating the near-match as evidence the cycle is attenuating normally rather than collapsing. He emphasizes the yearly-open baseline is methodologically necessary because a large pre-open drop in 2014 would otherwise distort the comparison. The projected path — June local low, October primary low — is consistent with a cycle delivering lower but still-present returns: "There's still enough evidence to suggest that the 4-year cycle is not dead." [00:43]

- 2026-06-21 (hXlR5xu0HK4): Cowen cites MVRV Z-Score cycle peak compression — approximately 10 in 2013, 7 in 2021, and 3 in the most recent cycle — as direct evidence that each successive bull market delivers diminished peak returns. The most recent peak's resemblance to the 2019 "apathetic top" reinforces the attenuating pattern. With the current reading at ~0.35, Cowen frames the market as transitioning toward the reset that precedes the next cycle: "this thing historically resets and from those reset levels, it then begins a new bull market, which again could very well line up with that four-year cycle" [04:43].
