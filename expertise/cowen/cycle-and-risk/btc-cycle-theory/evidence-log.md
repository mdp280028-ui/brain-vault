# Evidence log: BTC Cycle Theory

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-15 (FgxAe_NAh5c): Cowen uses Powell's departure and the Warsh Fed's inflation dilemma to reinforce his Q4 2026 BTC cycle-low thesis, arguing that resurging CPI and spiking PPI make rate cuts impossible and rate hikes in 2027 a real risk — both outcomes bad for risk assets into the cycle bottom. He grounds the four-year cycle in equity-market history rather than the halving: "the four-year cycle exists outside of Bitcoin, which is why I don't think the reason for Bitcoin's cycles, four cycles, is not because of the having. If it was because of the having, why does the stock market like to bottom approximately every four years?" [07:03]. A current counter-trend rally toward the mid-80s (38.2% Fibonacci retracement) is acknowledged but treated as consistent with the bear market, matching the same rally-to-382-then-lower pattern from 2018, 2020, and 2022.

- 2026-05-08 (APCjkL5gfFI): With SPX and NASDAQ at all-time highs, Cowen highlights BTC's failure to match them as structural cycle weakness — "Bitcoin is weak relative to the assets that are putting in all-time highs" [05:22] — and maps 2026 onto the 2018 midterm template (January ATH, September SPX top, Q4 collapse), expecting the secondary SPX correction window in late Q3/early Q4 2026 to drive BTC to a lower low. The S&P/M2 fractal continues tracking prior patterns, consistent with a ~September 2026 SPX top, and the labor market is explicitly framed as downstream of asset prices — cycle peak must precede macro deterioration, not the other way around.

- 2026-05-11 (Ox0mi5n3Ju4): With BTC reaching its 200-day moving average, Cowen maps current price action against every prior bear-market analogue: the 2018 structural sequence (Feb low → April higher low → May 200-day test) has replicated at ~10x price levels, and monthly Heikin Ashi candles — which stayed red throughout the 2018 and 2022 bear markets — show the same brief mid-bear green pattern seen in 2014 and 2019 before price continued lower. The 2019 countercase (BTC topped two months before QT ended, mirroring an October 2025 QT end) is the one credible alternative, but Cowen remains unconvinced: "I still unfortunately have the bear goggles on and think that it most likely will play out like we've seen in prior bare markets and that the price will likely eventually roll over and head back down." [05:53]

- 2026-05-11 (Li3m6eEWECA): Cowen identifies the 2018 price structure (Feb low → April higher low → May 200-day MA rally) as having replicated exactly in the current cycle at approximately 10x the price levels, calling it "actually really similar to how it played out in 2018." He frames the defining difficulty of this cycle as determining whether the outcome matches 2018 (continued losses) or 2019 (recovery), but makes no directional call in the clip — leaving the 2018 vs. 2019 question explicitly unresolved. The structural evidence strengthens the fractal case already documented in Ox0mi5n3Ju4 without adding new bearish conviction.

- 2026-05-12 (Rf-gAQJk5-0): This brief makes no explicit BTC cycle directional call — BTC outlook cells are blank and the "Thesis shifts" note confirms prior bearish arrows are unaddressed rather than flipped. The macro underpinning of the cycle-low thesis is nonetheless reinforced: CPI rose to 3.78% on a supply-driven energy shock, and Cowen notes that as of this video "the market now thinks that it is more likely that we get a rate hike in 2027 than a rate cut" [02:25], fully foreclosing the Fed pivot scenario. He frames this as the historically reliable late-cycle mechanism — "it is inflation... that causes the end of the business cycle because the Fed gets checkmated" [10:42] — consistent with his Q3–Q4 2026 SPX-top and BTC-cycle-low timeline.

- 2026-05-12 (RxD-bxH6tMg): With BTC sitting at its 200-day moving average, Cowen walks through the two historical exceptions where BTC broke above the level during bear markets — 2014 and 2019 — and characterizes both as failed breaches: "Bitcoin's surge above the 200 day was relatively short-lived" [00:18]. In 2014, BTC cleared it in June but could not hold, recaptured it briefly into July, then sold hard into October; in 2019 it crossed above in January and was back below by February and March. The consistent pattern across both cases frames the current 200-day test as bear-market resistance rather than a potential launchpad, reinforcing the cycle-low thesis.

- 2026-05-13 (9a-LA7tm8Mo): Cowen presents historical BTC-by-political-regime data (from 2013) showing that median BTC returns are negative under both full-sweep configurations — Democratic and Republican — with the best BTC regimes being split-Congress scenarios attributable to legislative gridlock reducing policy uncertainty. Most directly relevant to the cycle thesis: midterm years have averaged −65% for BTC, and Cowen explicitly notes that BTC is currently down ~7% in 2026, stating "You can see the midterm year on average is -65% on average. What has Bitcoin done so far this year? Well, it's down. About 7%." [23:00] — implying substantial further downside remains if the historical pattern holds, with a full replication taking BTC below $40K from the year's open.

- 2026-05-14 (zKHgKnv51r0): Cowen elevates the 2019 QT-end analog as his primary cycle comparison — BTC topped two months before QT ended in both 2019 (June peak, August QT end) and the current cycle (October 2025 peak, December 2025 QT end), with a ~52-53% decline preceding each countertrend rally, and the current price sequence has replicated the early steps of the 2019 pattern. He identifies the 0.382 Fibonacci retracement (~$85K) as the consistent stall point for mid-bear countertrend rallies across 2014, 2018, and 2022, and flags June as the historical turning-point month when BTC enters it on a rising trajectory — making an early-June peak at or below $85K his base case before a decline to an October 2026 cycle low. The macro catalyst for the final leg down is a second Q4 2026 S&P 500 correction mirroring Q4 2018: "it would seem to me like the most likely outcome is that it will likely go down into Q4. Just like Bitcoin went up into Q4 of 2025, it'll likely go down into Q4 of 2026." [15:01]

- 2026-05-17 (WHK1S6MrlCA): Cowen uses historical time-to-new-low data across four bear-market cycles (15 weeks in 2022, 19 in 2018, ~21 in two instances, 25 in 2014) to place the current cycle's next low window between mid-June and late July 2026 — only 14 weeks having elapsed so far. He anchors his Q4 $40K target to on-chain levels, noting that realized price (~$54K) and balanced price (~$39K) have been breached in every historical bear market, making "if we're sitting here and it's October or Q4 and Bitcoin is trading around like 40k, it will not have been any different than either 2018 or 2019" [14:17] a structurally grounded call rather than a guess. He reaffirms June as the critical turning-point month — high or low — but states that "regardless of what happens in June, I still think you'll go lower into October" [06:35], with the 200-day MA (~$78K) and 0.382 Fib (~$85K) as the two possible resistance caps for the countertrend high.

- 2026-05-19 (C1W7rFTWQWo): Cowen labels BTC bull sentiment around the counter-trend bounce as psychological self-deception, arguing that the levels bulls are celebrating — 77K, 80K, 85K — are not meaningful recoveries because BTC traded above all of them for essentially the entire prior year. [00:06] "Like, think about how far we've dropped and for the bulls to look at 77K, I know 77K or 80K or even 85K feels like a win, but the reality is Bitcoin was trading at above these prices for basically all of last year." The implied conclusion is that the bounce merely returns price to previously mundane territory, confirming rather than challenging his bear-market cycle framework.

- 2026-05-19 (M5d5fbiSfhc): BTC's rejection off the 200-day SMA is now confirmed, consistent with bear-market capping behavior in 2018 and 2022; Cowen maps two explicit paths to the October 2026 low — a more bearish sequence (May/June drop sweeping the February low, then a July–August countertrend rally before a new October low) and a less bearish path (one more push to the 0.382 Fibonacci near $85K, which was tagged before every prior mid-term flush but has not yet been reached this cycle). He frames the October low as the *optimistic* view — "When I talk about Bitcoin bottoming potentially in Q4, that's the optimistic view, right? For me. Like, that is the optimistic way to look at the market." [14:25] — and adds that the mid-term year seasonal average with one standard deviation places BTC ~40% below the yearly open (~$51–52K) by mid-2026, meaning current prices are significantly elevated relative to historical mid-term norms.

- 2026-05-20 (mIjviu7Q4ag): Cowen introduces stablecoin dominance (USDT + USDC combined) as a new structural confirmation of the bear market — the indicator formed a 2+ year base, broke out, and is completing a textbook A-B-C-D backtest of its breakout zone, with Cowen arguing "you don't form a base that holds for over 2 years only to have a false breakout above it" [01:36]. He overlays the 2018 seasonal analog (February low → higher low in April → 200-day rejection in May → June sweep below February) onto current BTC price action, framing the 200-day MA rejection and stablecoin dominance signal as a confirming confluence that the bear market structure remains intact and a June 2026 low undercutting the February lows is the most likely near-term path.

- 2026-05-21 (mwrgOz852wI): Though the video's primary focus is ETH, Cowen explicitly reaffirms his Q4 2026 BTC cycle-low base case and draws on the 2019 analog to note a sequencing nuance — ETH may bottom in June while BTC follows later, replicating the 2019 pattern in which ETH bottomed in June and BTC in November. The monetary policy pillar of the bear thesis receives direct confirmation: [08:50] "The rate cuts that we had priced in to enter the year have now been priced out. And now the market is starting to worry about rate hikes rather than rate cuts." — framing tighter macro conditions as the structural force keeping capital out of risk assets through the cycle low.

- 2026-05-21 (8PQee-onXrU): In a three-host panel, Cowen reaffirms his Q4 2026 cycle-low thesis and layers in midterm-year seasonality as a behavioral macro constraint: "cash cash has often been a pretty good play over crypto at least until the fourth quarter of of the midterm year" [26:11]. He extends this into a gold/BTC relative trade, arguing gold typically bottoms around summer in midterm years and rallies in H2 while BTC sells off — noting BTC would need to drop ~40% vs. gold just to return to its 2022 relative level. An ironic timing observation ties the cycle to the Clarity Act: Cowen speculates the bill might finally pass in Q4 2026 precisely when Bitcoin is expected to find its low, making it a real but ultimately too-late catalyst.
