# BTC Cycle Theory

Cowen's framework that Bitcoin and broader risk assets follow an approximately four-year cycle of bull-top, bear-bottom, and accumulation phases. He frames the cycle as macro-driven rather than halving-driven, citing parallel equity-market cycle precedents going back to the 1950s.

## Current Thesis

BTC remains in the late stage of the 2026 bear market, with Cowen framing this year as a "less volatile version" of 2018 at roughly 10x the price scale — both years sharing a February low, higher low in late March/April, 200-day-MA rejection in May, and a sweep of the February low in June ($57K in 2026 vs. $5,700 in 2018). He has now sharpened one detail of the analogy: the May 2026 lower high specifically formed at the 200-day moving average, a technical marker he didn't call out in his description of the 2018 pattern, reinforcing the structural match as more than coincidental. His base case calls for the 2018 script to continue a bit further before diverging: a countertrend rally starting in early July with a possible mid-July pullback, then a bigger push toward the 200-day MA in late July/early August, followed by giveback in August/September. Where he expects this cycle to "take a detour" from 2018 is the endgame — rather than bottoming in December like 2018, he thinks Bitcoin breaks below the current pattern before November and sees October as the strongest candidate for the cycle low, though December remains possible if the bear market runs as long as 2014's did. He continues to reject macro-indicator-driven forecasting in favor of the four-year cycle, and his strategy remains unchanged: ignore BTC in the first half of midterm years, DCA in the second half, and abandon the framework only if the cycle itself eventually breaks.

## Key Evidence

- 2026's price action is a "less volatile version" of 2018 at 10x the scale: both years show a February low, a higher low in late March/early April, a 200-day-MA rejection in May, and a sweep of the February low in June — June 2026's $57,000 low almost exactly 10x June 2018's $5,700 low. (sy6AxBbgico, 8TmOvGK235I, oj2n8nkTTTE, rxekpAAHwRs)
- The May 2026 lower high specifically formed at the 200-day moving average — a technical detail Cowen highlights as present in 2026 but not flagged in his account of the 2018 analog, sharpening the precision of the pattern match. (rxekpAAHwRs)
- Using ROI-measured-from-low-to-low, BTC is on cycle day 1,331, with the last two cycles bottoming roughly 100 days (~3 months) from a comparable point — implying a bottom around late September/October; Cowen flags October as the strongest candidate month, with December possible if the bear market runs as long as 2014's. (oj2n8nkTTTE, sy6AxBbgico)
- The crypto asset class remains below its fair value logarithmic regression trendline, with 2023-2025 framed as analogous to 2019 (rising BTC dominance, rate cuts, QT ending, no altcoin rotation); total crypto market cap (~$2.125T) is now slightly below its 2021 level. (uoF9yzXHmt4, BoSW-PLx1Ms)
- Every prior bear market bottom (2014, 2018, 2022) featured a massive volume capitulation spike that reset all on-chain metrics; that spike has not occurred yet, which remains Cowen's primary reason for rejecting any current bottom call. (psz9FwqWGns, g4FiHz14W14, 1LI9wfqxOmc, 8TmOvGK235I, prior)
- Cowen's distilled rule — ignore BTC in the first half of midterm years, DCA in the second half, sell at the end of post-halving years — remains undefeated across three cycles; he'll only abandon the framework if the four-year cycle itself eventually breaks. (sy6AxBbgico, psz9FwqWGns, vTcABO_0Exg, hXlR5xu0HK4, 5nIhiijBQt4, g4FiHz14W14, OYcGIifaO4Q, 1LI9wfqxOmc, 8TmOvGK235I, oj2n8nkTTTE)
