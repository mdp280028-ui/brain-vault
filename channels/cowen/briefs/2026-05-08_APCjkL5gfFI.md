## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Unemployment rate held flat at 4.3%; initial claims remain well below the analyst's stated 300K recession threshold; layoffs are at roughly pre-pandemic levels — no recession signal in the current data.
2. Job openings are low (fewer openings than available workers), quits remain low, but hires picked up this past month — analyst flags it as a data point to monitor, uncertain whether it marks a new trend or a one-off.
3. Total non-farm employment is still growing YoY by roughly 250K, but the YoY change is approaching zero. ADP private payrolls have ticked up slightly, a mild positive.
4. The analyst's recession framework requires lower asset prices to trigger the labor market feedback loop. As long as equity markets stay elevated, the labor market is unlikely to deteriorate into a self-reinforcing cycle.
5. Seasonally, initial claims tend to rise into summer (pattern confirmed in 2023, 2024, and 2025). A seasonal uptick in claims — and a likely lift in the unemployment rate — is expected by late summer 2026.
6. SPX is at all-time highs, which the analyst views as consistent with the midterm-year pattern. In 2014 the market trended up throughout the year; in 2018 the S&P made new ATHs in January and again into September before a Q4 collapse. The analyst maps 2026 onto the 2018 template.
7. The 2026 roadmap per the analyst: the early-year weakness (first window) already occurred, followed by the current rally to all-time highs. A secondary window of weakness in late Q3/early Q4 2026 is the base case — analogous to the September 2018 SPX peak and subsequent Q4 selloff.
8. The S&P/M2 fractal (S&P 500 divided by M2 money supply) is still tracking the prior pattern. The analyst expects it to eventually break but notes it has not broken yet; if it holds, the SPX top would land around September 2026.
9. Bitcoin has rallied but remains far from all-time highs while SPX and NASDAQ are printing record highs. The analyst frames BTC's relative underperformance not as a bullish setup but as evidence of structural weakness — assets that lead at strength windows do so for a reason, and BTC is not one of them this cycle.
10. The recession risk dashboard (composite of employment, national income/product accounts, production/business activity, and interest rates) has not exceeded 0.16 since 2020 — still no widespread alarm. More U.S. states are showing rising unemployment vs. 6 months ago, but the pattern is not yet economy-wide the way it was in 2001 or 2008.

## Predictions made
- Initial claims expected to rise seasonally into summer 2026, potentially lifting the unemployment rate by late summer.
- SPX expected to have a secondary window of weakness culminating in late Q3/early Q4 2026 (possible top around September, analogous to the September 2018 high).
- Bitcoin expected to follow the SPX secondary correction lower and likely set a lower low in the back half of 2026.
- Energy stocks (XLE) expected to bounce higher over the coming months as late-cycle dynamics favor energy.
