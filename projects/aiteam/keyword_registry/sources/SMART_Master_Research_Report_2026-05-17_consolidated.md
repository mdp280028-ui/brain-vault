## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Six rounds of Ahrefs validation (~580 keywords submitted, ~482 returned with data) surfaced **158 high-quality keywords across 21 B2B SaaS clusters** clearing the screen of KD ≤10 + volume ≥100 + CPC ≥$5, plus ~30 sub-threshold keywords flagged for extreme CPC ($25–$95). The data revealed three things that change the operating model:

1. **The "vs" / "alternatives" pattern accounts for ~85% of high-CPC affiliate-friendly keywords.** Informational content (current SSG model) is the wrong content shape for affiliate revenue. Comparison content is what converts.

2. **SmartSourceGuide's three current niches (fleet/IT/answering) are NOT where the highest-CPC opportunities live.** The strongest clusters surfaced were helpdesk software, EOR/global HR, ATS/recruiting, and sales outreach — none of which fit SSG's current identity. 110 of 158 winners target verticals SSG doesn't cover.

3. **The single highest-CPC keyword across all 580 submitted was `moneypenny reviews` at $95 CPC** (answering services — fits SSG directly). The second-highest was `remote vs deel` at $80 (EOR — does not fit SSG). The third-highest was `zendesk alternatives` at $55 (helpdesk — does not fit SSG).

**Two biggest findings that ARE SSG-direct fit:**

- The answering services cluster contains the highest-CPC keyword surfaced in 6 rounds — `moneypenny reviews` at $95 CPC. Combined with adjacent winners (`voicenation reviews` $30, `answerconnect pricing` $25, `answerconnect reviews` $12, `patlive reviews` $12, `abby connect reviews` $10), this forms a 7-keyword "answering service review/comparison" sub-cluster funneling toward Ruby and AnswerConnect — both already enrolled affiliates.

- The MSP/IT support cluster (`datto alternatives` $40, `manageengine alternatives` $35, `connectwise alternatives` $30, `ninjaone vs atera` $30, `atera reviews` $25) was not in the original SSG roadmap. ManageEngine is the enrolled affiliate — every comparison page where ManageEngine appears as an alternative directly drives signups.

**Strategic decision raised five times, still parked:** Do these clusters expand SmartSourceGuide, go to a new domain, or get parked? This report organizes the data so the decision can be made cleanly, but doesn't force it.

**Tactical recommendation:** Ship the 8-article SSG-direct roadmap (Section 8) before any further research. Estimated revenue at full ranking: $1,650–4,000/month from these 8 pages alone.

## 1. METHODOLOGY & SCREENING THRESHOLDS

Six Ahrefs Keywords Explorer rounds, each ~100 keywords. Two parallel screens were applied during the research:

**Primary screen (used for non-SSG clusters):**

| Filter | Threshold | Strong Signal |
|---|---|---|
| KD (Keyword Difficulty) | ≤ 10 | ≤ 5 |
| Monthly Volume | ≥ 100 | ≥ 500 |
| CPC | ≥ $5 | ≥ $15 |
| Intent | Buyer-shopping / comparison preferred | "alternatives", "vs", "reviews", "pricing" |
| Exclusion | "near me" local intent | — |

**Looser screen (used for SSG-direct fit, where existing-page integration changes the math):**

| Filter | Threshold |
|---|---|
| KD | ≤10 |
| Monthly Volume | ≥40 |
| CPC | ≥$5 |

The looser volume threshold for SSG-direct keywords is justified because each one stacks onto an existing page or shares a funnel target with enrolled affiliates — the marginal cost of including a sub-100-vol keyword as a section is near zero when the page is already being written.

Sub-threshold keywords (typically 10–90/mo) were flagged separately when CPC exceeded $25. These represent low-volume, high-LTV opportunities where a single conversion can pay for the article 5–10x over.

Categories intentionally excluded mid-scan per operator instruction:
- Payroll, accounting, legal, medical (E-E-A-T burden)
- AI writing tools, password managers, VPNs (consistent dead CPC across all rounds)
- Hosting (overcrowded SERPs)
- Anything healthcare / financial advisory

## 9. THE "VS" / "ALTERNATIVES" PATTERN

Of 158 greenlit keywords across 6 rounds, **~135 (85%) contain the words "vs", "alternatives", or "reviews"**. This is the single most consistent signal in the data.

**Why it matters:**
- These queries are buyer-shopping, not researching. The reader has a shortlist and is comparing options.
- Conversion rate on comparison pages is typically 3–5x informational content.
- Articles are faster to write — "list 5–7 alternatives, one paragraph per option, price, pros/cons, CTA per option."
- Affiliate placement is natural — every "alternative" listed is a link.

**What it means for SSG:**

The current SSG content model (best-of guides, how-to articles, "for [industry]" pages) targets informational intent. The data says affiliate revenue follows comparison intent. There are two interpretations:

1. **SSG's existing pages are positioned wrong for revenue.** Even within fleet/IT/answering, the high-CPC keywords are "samsara alternatives", "ruby vs answerconnect", "atera vs ninjaone" — not "best fleet GPS tracking" or "answering service for plumbers". The roadmap was built around informational pillars; the data says comparison pages are where the money is.

2. **The existing pages serve a different role.** Informational pages capture top-of-funnel readers who become repeat visitors and find the comparison pages via internal links. If this is true, the comparison cluster should be added alongside, not instead.

Most likely answer: a mix. Keep the informational pillars for topical authority and indexing, but add comparison articles as the primary affiliate-driving pages. Internal-link the informational pages to the comparison pages.

## 14. PATTERNS AND FUTURE-USEFUL FINDINGS

### Pattern 1 — "Vs" and "alternatives" queries dominate the high-CPC, low-KD intersection

Across all 6 rounds and ~480 keywords returned, comparison/alternatives queries accounted for ~85% of survivors after screening. This pattern should drive SSG's editorial direction going forward: every future SSG article should be either a comparison (X vs Y), an alternatives roundup (best X alternatives), or a single-product review (X reviews/pricing).

Informational content (best practices, how-to-choose, what-is-X) does NOT convert at the same rate per click and faces stiffer SERP competition from vendor blogs and Reddit.

### Pattern 2 — KD 0-1 dominates the survivor list

Of the 158 keywords across all 6 rounds that passed screening, ~70% have KD 0 or 1. The opportunity is in low-authority SERPs where 200-1000-word independent comparison content can rank against thin vendor-self-rankings. Don't chase KD 15+ keywords — the SERP is saturated and SSG has no domain authority yet.

### Pattern 3 — CPC correlates with B2B vertical seriousness, not search volume

`moneypenny reviews` at 150 vol / $95 CPC delivers higher per-click value than `obsidian vs notion` at 2,100 vol / $0.25 CPC. Don't chase volume — chase volume × CPC.

### Pattern 4 — SaaS vendor affiliate programs churn faster than expected

Of the affiliate programs mentioned across web research this session, some confirmed live (Ruby, AnswerConnect, ManageEngine, Atera, HubSpot, GetResponse, ClickUp, Apollo via PartnerStack), others remain unverified. Programs at smaller vendors (Goodcall, Numa, Bland AI, Synthflow, Rosie AI) may have changed terms since training data cutoffs. ALWAYS verify each program's current terms on the vendor's official affiliate page before building CTAs around them.

### Pattern 5 — The site as currently identified ≠ where the highest-CPC keywords live

110 of the 134 high-quality non-SSG keywords surfaced in this scan target verticals SmartSourceGuide doesn't cover (helpdesk, HR/payroll, sales outreach, ATS, billing infrastructure, etc.). Strategic decision on whether to expand SSG or start a new domain remains open.

### Pattern 6 — Hit rate degrades as research goes deeper

Round 1 returned 65/100 with 25 winners. Round 4 (deeper longer-tail) returned 92/100 but only 22 winners. The first 200 keywords scanned in any vertical produce the bulk of the winners; rounds 5+ have diminishing returns. **Future research sessions should target NEW verticals, not deeper into known ones.**

Buyers are companies hiring international contractors — this isn't quite "financial advice" but Google may apply E-E-A-T standards to it. ⚠️ **The "alternatives" content model has SERP risk.** Google has periodically clamped down on low-effort "Top 10 Alternatives" articles. If SSG goes heavy on this pattern, each article needs real differentiation — not just a list, but real pricing tables, honest use-case fit, screenshots, decision frameworks. The pattern can be ranked, but not by writing the same 5-paragraph template 30 times.

⚠️ **Helpful Content classifier risk on multi-niche sites.** Google has been increasingly skeptical of multi-niche generalist sites since the 2024 Helpful Content updates. A 100-page site spanning helpdesk + ATS + email marketing + fleet GPS + answering services may look like an affiliate farm to modern site-quality classifiers. This is a factor in favor of Path 3 (new domain) over Path 2 (expand SSG) — sites that look like Reddit-replacement aggregators have been getting downranked in batch.

⚠️ **Operator stated in memory: "Build and validate manually before automating — real failure modes calibrate tools."** This research session built a 580-keyword library before any SSG page has indexed. The over-engineering risk is real. Path 1's 30 articles are validation-scale.
