# Business Cycle Phase

The position of the US economy within its expansion-peak-contraction-trough business cycle, including Cowen's M2-normalized composite (SPX, unemployment, rates, inflation, M2). Cowen overlays the business cycle with Bitcoin's four-year cycle to time long-horizon allocation, treating cycle-bottoms as accumulation windows.

## Current Thesis

Cowen characterizes the US economy as in a late-cycle environment defined by a supply-side energy shock sustaining elevated inflation, a Fed that has moved from "checkmate" (unable to cut) to a market now pricing active rate hikes — a further tightening of the macro vice. SPX continues to rise on AI-sector earnings momentum, structurally decoupling from crypto, which remains purely liquidity-driven and therefore more exposed to rate-hike pricing. BTC's underperformance relative to SPX, gold, and energy is expected to persist for at least several more months, with BTC/SPX ratio outperformance not anticipated until 2027, consistent with the next cycle's accumulation window opening in late 2026/early 2027.

## Key Evidence

- Headline CPI rose to 3.78% YoY in May 2026, driven by a supply-side energy shock — consistent with a late-cycle inflation impulse rather than an early-cycle recovery (Rf-gAQJk5-0).
- Markets have repriced beyond zero cuts to now pricing rate hikes; Cowen calls this "straightforwardly bearish for crypto" as it eliminates the liquidity tailwind speculative assets require (Rf-gAQJk5-0, dy3E7Jzte88).
- Cowen frames the Fed as approaching "checkmate": rising inflation prevents cuts while simultaneous labor weakness would leave the Fed without a viable policy defense — a structural late-cycle signal (Rf-gAQJk5-0).
- SPX is rising on AI-sector earnings, not liquidity — a late-cycle divergence that structurally advantages equities over crypto, which lacks an earnings catalyst (dy3E7Jzte88).
- BTC/SPX ratio rejected at the 20-week SMA / 21-week EMA bear market resistance band as of May 2026, confirming continued BTC underperformance relative to equities within this cycle (dy3E7Jzte88).
- Cowen sees structural risk of energy prices rising again in Q3, which would drive additional rate-hike pricing and extend BTC's macro headwind through year-end (dy3E7Jzte88).
