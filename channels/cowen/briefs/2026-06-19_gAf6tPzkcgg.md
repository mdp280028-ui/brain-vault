## Key points (the distilled video)
1. The "social risk" composite metric — built from Google Trends, Coinbase app rankings, YouTube subscribers, YouTube views, Wikipedia page views, and Twitter followers for analysts/exchanges/layer-ones — has been trending down and continues to drop as of this video.
2. Cowen draws an explicit parallel between the 2019 top and the 2025 top: in both years the Fed cut rates exactly three times for a total of 75 basis points (2.5%→1.75% in 2019; 4.5%→3.75% in 2025), and in both years quantitative tightening ended roughly two months after the Bitcoin price peak (June top/August QT end in 2019; October top/December QT end in 2025).
3. The key structural difference from 2021 and 2017: retail social interest never came back in this cycle. This cycle's top was an "apathetic top" like 2019, not a euphoric retail-driven top.
4. Crypto YouTube channels that were gaining 40,000–60,000 subscribers per week at the 2021 peak are now losing subscribers on a 7-day moving average, averaging net losses of roughly 400–500 subscribers per week. YouTube views that ran 3–4 million per day in 2021 have collapsed.
5. As social interest trends down, Bitcoin dominance trends up — a relationship Cowen says has held since 2021. He notes BTC dominance excluding stablecoins continues to make higher highs even as the headline dominance figure has stalled.
6. The root cause of retail's absence is threefold: exhaustion from meme coin scams and altcoin rug pulls; a high-rate/QT macro environment that kept people focused on basic costs rather than speculation; and an identity crisis in crypto exposed by events like a sitting president launching a meme coin.
7. The path back for crypto runs through looser monetary policy, but Cowen sees no near-term catalyst — rate hikes are being priced back in due to the AI trade keeping equities at new highs and geopolitical inflation pressures. There is no promise of easier conditions right now.
8. Cowen expects the stock market to show weakness in late Q3/early Q4 2026, which could pressure the Fed to pivot. Until that happens, crypto remains stuck.
9. The altcoin market is tracking the post-2019 fade: it bounced off the lows, failed to sustain, and is slowly bleeding as participants give up. Many 2021 altcoins that made new lows in 2022 are now below even those 2022 lows in 2026. New meme-coin launches failed to mask this structural weakness the way fresh altcoin classes did in prior cycles.
10. Cowen believes social interest could bottom later in 2026. He argues that when retail eventually returns, the industry needs to be in a better place — less influencer shilling, more actual technology building — or newcomers will again be led astray and leave thinking Bitcoin is just like the scam altcoins.
