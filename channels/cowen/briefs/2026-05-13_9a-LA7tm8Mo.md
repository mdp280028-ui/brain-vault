## TL;DR
Historical S&P 500 data (1953–present) shows the most bullish regime is a Democratic president with a split or Republican Congress (avg 16–17%), while the most bearish is a Republican president with a full Democratic Congress (avg 4.9%). With the 2026 midterms approaching from a current Republican sweep, Cowen presents this regime-return framework as a forward-looking risk tool for equities, Bitcoin, and gold.

## Key points (the distilled video)
1. S&P 500 by regime (1953–present): the single most bullish setup is a Democratic president + split Congress — avg 17%, median 18.3% (6 years of data). Democratic president + Republican Congress is nearly as strong: avg 16.2%, median 19.7%.
2. Sweep comparison: Republican sweep outperforms Democratic sweep in average annual return (13.3% vs 8%); Cowen notes this was surprising to him. A single 1954 data point (+45%) is a major outlier inflating the Republican sweep average.
3. The worst regime for equities: Republican president + full Democratic Congress — avg 4.9%, median 4%, the lowest of all configurations. Most historical recessions attributed to Republican presidents occurred in exactly this configuration, not during full Republican sweeps.
4. The logic: recessions that appear to be "under Republican presidents" are concentrated in periods of divided government (Republican president + Democratic Congress), which explains why Republican sweeps have better average returns than intuition suggests.
5. Presidential cycle: Year 3 (pre-election) is the best year on average; Year 2 (midterm year) is historically the worst. The current market (midterm 2026) is up ~8% YTD — described as "fine" but tracking a historically weak year. Recent years: 2023 +24%, 2024 +23%, 2025 +16%.
6. Bitcoin by regime (data from 2013 only): median return under both a Democratic sweep and a Republican sweep is negative (−2% avg and −6% median respectively under each). Best Bitcoin regimes: split Congress scenarios. Theory: Bitcoin benefits from gridlock because neither party can push through legislation unilaterally, reducing policy uncertainty.
7. BTC midterm year historical average: −65%. BTC is currently down ~7% in 2026 — by historical precedent, substantial further downside remains possible before year-end. Cowen does the math: a 65% drop from the year's open would take BTC below $40K.
8. Gold performs best under Republican sweep (the current regime); 2025 was flagged as one of gold's best years. Cowen holds gold and expects it to continue performing well, even if Congress shifts to split or Democratic control (still positive on average). The worst regime for gold: Democratic president + Republican Congress — which he flags as a potential 2029–2030 exit signal.
9. USD/DXY drops the most under Republican sweep (since 1980) and performs best under Democratic president + Republican Congress. Midterm years have historically been weak for the dollar on average — a finding obscured by recent midterm strength (2018, 2022) creating recency bias.
10. Business cycle risk: Cowen speculates the long-running business cycle reset (recession) may ultimately be triggered by a post-midterm shift to Republican president + Democratic Congress — the historically most bearish configuration for equities — and notes we may be headed there.
