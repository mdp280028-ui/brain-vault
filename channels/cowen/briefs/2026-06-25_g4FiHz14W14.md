## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen's single most important indicator — deserving "50% of attention" — is ROI from the cycle low, which nailed the top within one week across the last two cycles (day 1,068 and day 1,059). Applying the same logic, the bottom should arrive around day 1,424–1,432, which is roughly 100–110 days from the June 25 broadcast date, pointing to late Q3/early Q4.
2. BTC is currently on day 1,324 from the cycle low. The cycle-from-peak indicator similarly suggests more time is needed: prior cycles bottomed at days 364–406 from the peak; we are currently on day 262.
3. Cowen distinguishes two types of capitulation: time-based (the slow bleed that follows cycle averages) and price-based (a sudden sharp drop that resets all on-chain metrics at once). He defers to time-based unless price-based capitulation occurs, at which point price-based wins regardless of timing.
4. Expected path: a near-term low (possibly now or slightly lower), then a counter-trend rally starting mid-to-late July (matching June starts in the prior two cycles), followed by the final drop into Q4 to mark the market cycle bottom.
5. Volume is a key price-based capitulation signal: the 2018 and 2022 bottoms both featured extended low volume followed by a massive spike. Current volume has remained low; Cowen expects a similar spike to confirm the eventual bottom.
6. MVRV Z-Score is at 0.251; the market has always bottomed after this metric goes below zero. He expects it may cross zero later in 2026 — one indicator to watch.
7. Running one-year ROI typically forms its trough near the end of the midterm year — in this case, one year after October 2025 — making it another timer to track.
8. Bitcoin has historically dropped below the realized price (~$53K currently) in every bare market. Cowen notes we haven't done so yet this cycle, which is consistent with prior cycles (2018: not until November; 2014: not until September/October). He views a dip below $53K as likely, not as a deviation.
9. The clearest price-based capitulation signal would be a touch of the balance price (~$38K). BTC historically spends only a few days below this level (seen in 2011, 2015, 2018, 2022). Hitting it would fully reset on-chain risk metrics and make calling the bottom straightforward for most observers — but Cowen is not predicting this must happen.
10. On-chain risk composite (including MVRV, puell multiple, terminal price, miner cap to thermocap, RHODL ratio, supply in profit/loss) is currently just below 0.2; historical cycle lows cluster near 0.1, indicating more downside is still possible before a full reset.

## Predictions made
- BTC counter-trend rally expected to begin mid-to-late July, consistent with June/July starts in prior cycles.
- Cycle bottom expected in Q4 2026, based on ROI-from-low timing (day ~1,424–1,432, approximately 100–110 days from June 25).
- BTC likely to drop below the realized price (~$53K) at some point this cycle, consistent with every prior bare market.
