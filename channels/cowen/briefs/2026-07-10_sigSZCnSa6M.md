## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen reiterates his 4-year cycle/seasonality framework: Bitcoin has historically formed lows in February of midterm years (2014, 2018, 2022, 2026) followed by a rally into early March, and he had called for a bottoming window in "late September or early October" based on ROI-from-low symmetry with prior cycles.
2. He argues the strategy of ignoring Bitcoin in the first half of midterm years worked again this year: BTC fell against gold (-28%), the S&P (-39%), energy (-44%), silver (-18%), and Microsoft (-13%) in the first half of 2026, with the actual cycle low forming July 1st.
3. Bitcoin's 2026 price action closely mirrors 2018: matching lows, a matching lower high in May, a matching drop into late June/early July, and a similar counter-trend rally now underway; in 2018 price rallied to the 200-day MA in both May and late July before resuming down into Q4.
4. Bitcoin is described as "squeezed" between the 200-week MA (support) and the bear market resistance band (resistance), unable to break out or break down, with a resolution expected in about a month or two (Q4).
5. On-chain indicators haven't fully reset: MVRV Z-score hasn't gone below zero yet as it has in every prior cycle bottom; realized price (~53K) and balance price (~38K) are historical downside levels Bitcoin has undercut in every prior midterm year, implying further downside is likely before a true bottom.
6. Social/retail interest has been trending down since May 2021; crypto YouTube channel views are down roughly an order of magnitude from ~3-4M/day in 2021 to ~400K/day now, and the advanced-decline index of the top 100 cryptocurrencies has been falling for years — evidence of broad apathy.
7. This cycle is the first where there was no altcoin rotation at cycle end; Cowen compares 2025/2026 to 2019/2020 (three rate cuts each year, QT ending roughly two months after BTC's cycle top in both cases) as the closest historical analog, also a "no rotation to alts" cycle.
8. He expects a time-based capitulation (roughly a year-long bear market) rather than a price-based one, marked by a volume spike like at the ends of 2014, 2018, and 2022 bear markets — which hasn't happened yet; he's not calling for a "pandemic-style" crash but doesn't rule out a similar shock event.
9. July is historically a green month in midterm years (2022 +~20%, 2018 +~38%, 2014 -5%), but August/September (and often October) have historically been red in every midterm year cited, suggesting any July strength likely fades again into fall.
10. He advises DCAing Bitcoin starting in the second half of midterm years, ignoring narratives, fading gurus who've repeated the same bullish or alt-season narratives for 5 years, and maintaining a long time horizon; he believes the low is likely in by his November ITC conference (60-65% chance by Nov 21st) setting up the next bull market into 2027.

## Predictions made
- Bitcoin bottoming window originally called (about 8 months ago) as "late September or early October" 2026, based on cycle-top-to-bottom day-count symmetry.
- A resolution of the current 200-week-MA/bear-market-resistance-band squeeze is likely to kick off "in about a month or two" (i.e., Q4 2026).
- Time-based capitulation bear market normally lasts about a year from the apathetic top; Cowen expects the low to likely be in by November 21, 2026 (60-65% chance), setting up the next bull market into 2027.
- July is likely to be a green month for BTC (seasonally), with August/September/October likely red based on every midterm year cited (2014, 2018, 2022).
