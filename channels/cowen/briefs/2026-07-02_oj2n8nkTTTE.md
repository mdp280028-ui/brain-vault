## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Ben says there's an "increasing probability" the early-summer low is in, reiterating the 2018 analog: Bitcoin's 2026 low of ~$57.7K almost exactly mirrors 2018's $5,743 low (a 10x scaled match), with both cycles sharing a February low, a higher low in late March/early April, a May rejection at the 200-day MA, and a lower low in late June/early July.
2. He expects Bitcoin to find "a little bit of relief" but does not think the bear market is over — he still expects one more rejection at the 200-day moving average, echoing 2018's late July/early August rejection.
3. He attributes any further weakness later in the year to a stock-market correction rather than a crypto-specific catalyst, after which Bitcoin would put in the market cycle bottom.
4. His stated strategy remains to DCA Bitcoin through the second half of midterm years rather than time the bottom exactly, noting the first half of the midterm year already played out with Bitcoin underperforming.
5. Using ROI-from-low-to-low measurement, Bitcoin is on "day 1331" of the cycle, and the last two cycles bottomed about 100 days (~3 months) from a comparable point — implying a bottom around late September/October, possibly later into Q4 but "not much later."
6. He argues the four-year cycle "remains intact," noting 2026's year-to-date performance is outperforming 2018 and 2022 but underperforming 2014, and that 2014 saw a similar rally-then-fall-to-an-October-low pattern.
7. He flags a potential rate hike priced in for September as a possible headwind/narrative catalyst that could coincide with Bitcoin capitulating shortly after.
8. Rob adds context that the current drawdown (~51% from highs) is far milder than the 71% drawdown at the same point in the 2022 cycle, suggesting this bear market is comparatively less brutal despite lower forward returns.
9. On the dollar: Ben shows a chart comparing the dollar in Trump's second term to his first term, arguing the dollar will "begrudgingly" grind higher through the midterm year (a headwind for crypto for the next 3-4 months, notwithstanding a countertrend rally), then top out and only marginally rise the following year — which he frames as the eventual bull case for Bitcoin.
10. Discussion of yen strength (40-year high) and Bank of Japan rate hikes as a risk-asset overhang, with Ben noting crypto tends to be the first asset class to catch any yen-carry-trade-driven risk-off move.

## Predictions made
- Bitcoin cycle bottom expected roughly 100 days (~3 months) from the recent low, landing around late September to October 2026, possibly later into Q4 but not much beyond that.
- A rejection at the 200-day moving average expected in the "late July, early August" window, mirroring 2018.
- Possible rate hike priced in for September 2026, potentially acting as a capitulation catalyst shortly after.
- Dollar expected to keep grinding higher through the rest of the midterm year (next 3-4 months) as a headwind for crypto, before topping out and rising only marginally next year.
