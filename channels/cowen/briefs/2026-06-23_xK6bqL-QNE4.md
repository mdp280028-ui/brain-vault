## Key points (the distilled video)
1. SpaceX gave back all of its IPO gains within a week of launch, which Cowen explicitly predicted in his prior video — roughly 90% of IPOs eventually trade below their opening price after the initial surge.
2. Cowen draws a detailed analog between SpaceX (IPO June 12, 2026) and Tesla (IPO June 29, 2010): both run by Elon Musk, both launched in June of a midterm year, both surged ~50–60% from launch before selling off sharply.
3. Tesla dropped 50% from its IPO high; SpaceX has so far dropped about 34% from its high — the pattern is similar but not identical, partly because SpaceX's market cap at launch was much larger than Tesla's.
4. The standard IPO pattern Cowen describes: initial pop → give it all back → sweep the IPO price low → another rally → then determine if it's a higher high or lower high, which signals whether the eventual year-end low will be higher or lower.
5. SpaceX is now in the "sweeping the low" phase; Cowen expects this to take days to weeks, followed by another rally, and then a final pullback later in the year aligned with the typical H2 midterm correction.
6. The S&P 500 context matters: in 2010 when Tesla IPO'd, the S&P put in a major low in late June/early July (taking out the February low), and Tesla bottomed at exactly that moment and never revisited that low. Today the S&P is not near taking out its March 2026 low, so the setup is different — the idiosyncratic bottom for SpaceX is less clear.
7. Historical midterm S&P corrections: 2010 saw a ~17% drop; 2014 ~10%; 2018 ~20%; 2022 ~20%. In 2026, the first drop was ~10% but the second drop typical of midterm years has not yet occurred. Cowen does not think it has happened yet.
8. The S&P typically finds a local top in H2 of midterm years — September in both 2018 and 2014 — before a larger correction. Cowen expects this pattern to repeat, which would weigh on SpaceX later in 2026.
9. The Apple IPO analog is also referenced: Apple launched late 1980, surged, dropped, fell further into a recession low ~18 months later — yet the entire window was a good accumulation zone in hindsight.
10. Cowen's personal stance: he plans to accumulate SpaceX in H2 2026, does not recommend betting against Elon Musk long-term, and believes SpaceX will be highly disruptive over a 20-year horizon even if the near-term path is choppy and uninspiring.

## Predictions made
- SpaceX will likely see another rally after the current sweep of the IPO low, followed by a pullback later in 2026 aligned with H2 midterm S&P weakness
- SpaceX prices likely stay suppressed through the rest of 2026
- If the S&P takes out its March 2026 low or drops ~20%, that would likely mark a durable long-term low for SpaceX
- Tesla did not durably break above its IPO launch-day spike high until 2.5 years after IPO; Cowen implies SpaceX could follow a similar multi-year timeline
