## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen opens by celebrating 1 million subscribers, noting he started the channel in June 2019 after presenting physics research at a conference in Portland — the channel grew from 100 subscribers to 1 million over 7 years.
2. The "Beauty of Mathematics" chart series has been running for 5–6 years; Cowen is considering an update to the underlying model and expects to refit it after the end of 2026 to incorporate full midterm-year data, which he believes will improve predictions of the next cycle top.
3. He reiterates his core speculation: Bitcoin will likely spend the rest of 2026 below the "fair value" logarithmic regression trend line, consistent with the prior brief. He frames this as speculation rather than a high-conviction call.
4. June is cited as a candidate month for at least a local low; he continues to take it "one step at a time" rather than calling a precise bottom.
5. The current cycle is compared to the 2019–2020 era in terms of business cycle positioning — the implication being that the asset class is in a trough analogous to that period before the next major bull run.
6. Cowen attributes the prolonged below-fair-value period to macro conditions: monetary policy tightened significantly and inflation was a concern starting in 2021, tempering expectations.
7. Total crypto market cap is currently $2.179 trillion, up from $261 billion when Cowen first made videos on this chart — nearly an order of magnitude increase.
8. Long-term, Cowen reiterates a thesis he has held since 2019–2020: the total crypto market cap is heading to $10 trillion, "plus or minus a few trillion." He closes with his recurring sign-off line about that figure.
9. He plans to update the Bitcoin regression model in one or two upcoming videos once the year is complete, to have the most accurate model heading into 2027–2028.
