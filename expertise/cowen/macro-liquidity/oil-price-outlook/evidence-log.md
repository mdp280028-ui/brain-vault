# Evidence log: Oil Price Outlook

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-15 (FgxAe_NAh5c): Cowen argues oil is structurally elevated for the remainder of 2026, pointing to the 21-week EMA having already provided support and warning that as longer-term moving averages catch up, there is meaningful risk of another oil price surge later in the year. He treats this directly as a Fed-constraint mechanism — energy market conditions are what make rate cuts functionally impossible regardless of political pressure, quoting: [14:24] "He's looking at the energy market and he's like, 'How the hell do I cut?'" Oil's role here is explicitly macro-tightening: it feeds into spiking PPI and re-accelerating CPI, reinforcing his no-pivot, bearish-risk-assets thesis through at least year-end 2026.

- 2026-05-21 (8PQee-onXrU): The panel identifies Strait of Hormuz disruption as the primary inflation driver, with strategic petroleum reserve drawdowns having buffered the shock so far — but Guy argues those reserves are nearly exhausted, meaning real shortages of oil, naphtha, and fertilizer are imminent and have not yet been reflected in official data or consumer prices. Once shortages bite, the panel concludes rate hikes become the likely response: "my guess is that inflation is going to spike because of these shortages really starting to bite because of reserves running out and the only option then is to raise rates" [20:21]. Cowen also notes energy stocks historically lag the equity market top by 6–12 months (citing 2000 and 2007 cycles), suggesting energy outperformance has further to run before the cycle turns.
