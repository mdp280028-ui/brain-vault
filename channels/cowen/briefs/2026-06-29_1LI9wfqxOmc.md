## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin just posted its first weekly close below the 200-week moving average this cycle — a threshold that triggers alarm, but Cowen notes it has happened before: most recently in June 2022, and also during the pandemic drop.
2. The 2026 price structure (low in February, higher low in late March/early April, lower low in June) is a near-exact repeat of 2018 (low in February, higher low in late March/early April, lower low in June).
3. Cowen's base-case roadmap: Bitcoin forms a low early in summer → countertrend rally mid-to-late summer → final drop into the market cycle bottom in late Q3 or early Q4.
4. He distinguishes between two types of capitulation: time-based (the cycle plays out gradually over months) vs. price-based (a sudden, massive volume spike that resets all on-chain metrics). His default assumption is time-based.
5. Every prior bear market bottom (2014, 2018, 2022) featured a massive volume spike; that spike has not occurred yet in this cycle, which is why he does not call a bottom now.
6. The only scenario that would cause a premature bottom is a price-based capitulation event — analogous to the pandemic crash in 2020 or to FTX/Luna in 2022. He notes that even with those extreme events in 2022, the four-year cycle still played out.
7. In 2018, Bitcoin fell in late June, bounced briefly into early July, then sold back down to ~$6,000 in mid-July. Cowen has previously drawn an analog where the 2018 $6,000 level corresponds to roughly $60,000 in 2026 — a break decisively below that level would signal the cycle bottom is forming.
8. His recommended strategy is not to trade the countertrend rallies (too difficult to time) but instead to DCA Bitcoin in the second half of midterm years, which has historically worked out over a longer time horizon even if short-term drawdowns continued.
9. If price-based capitulation does occur and on-chain metrics fully reset, Cowen says he would pivot and remove the expectation of a lower low — he explicitly warns against marrying any single prediction.
10. Core message: dropping below the 200-week MA is "a little scary" but not unprecedented; context and cycle positioning matter more than the single indicator.

## Predictions made
- BTC most likely forms a low early in summer 2026, rallies mid-to-late summer, then makes a final cycle bottom in late Q3 or early Q4 2026.
- A decisive break below ~$60,000 would likely signal the market cycle bottom is forming (2018 analog: $6,000 level).
