# Business Cycle Phase

The position of the US economy within its expansion-peak-contraction-trough business cycle, including Cowen's M2-normalized composite (SPX, unemployment, rates, inflation, M2). Cowen overlays the business cycle with Bitcoin's four-year cycle to time long-horizon allocation, treating cycle-bottoms as accumulation windows.

## Current Thesis

Cowen now explicitly runs two overlapping cyclical lenses at once — the four-year Bitcoin cycle (currently a "less volatile" 2018 fractal replay) and the business-cycle/liquidity lens (which he says feels like the 2019-2020 "digestion phase" after the 2019 apathetic top) — arguing "why not both?" rather than picking one. He still expects a brief mid-July bounce/CPI-related higher low, most gains given back by late July/early August, one more squeeze toward the bear-market resistance band to "fully reset onchain indicators," and a stock-market correction in August/September that triggers Bitcoin's final leg down to a market-cycle bottom, base-cased at late September/early October 2026 (a December tail case exists if the cycle stretches to 13-14 months like 2013-2014, which he calls "getting a little greedy"). He continues to downweight macro as a precise timing tool relative to the price/seasonality fractal, while still using it as directional backdrop, and separates "being right" from "making money," reiterating that he starts DCA-buying through summer without waiting for a confirmed bottom.

## Key Evidence

- Cowen now frames the current period through two simultaneously valid lenses: the four-year-cycle fractal (2026 as a less-volatile 2018 replay: February low, higher low late March/early April, lower high in May, sweep of the February low late June/early July) and the business-cycle/liquidity lens (resembling the 2019-2020 digestion phase after the 2019 apathetic top) — "it looks more like the digestion phase after the 2019 apathetic top" / "this is a less volatile version of the 2018 bear market" (Wf7vUIoGQrE [00:24], [01:31]).
- He expects the YTD-ROI fractal match to 2018 to eventually break, but via a brief mid-July bounce (CPI-related higher low) followed by most gains given back by late July/early August, echoing both 2018 and 2022 seasonal patterns (Wf7vUIoGQrE).
- BTC is squeezed between the 200-week moving average and the bear-market resistance band; Cowen expects one more retest/rejection at the resistance band to the downside, calling this necessary "to fully reset all the onchain indicators" ahead of the true cycle bottom (Wf7vUIoGQrE [09:22]).
- Base case for the market-cycle bottom is late September/early October 2026 via low-to-low ROI measurement — "my guess is that we won't have to wait until the end of December for the low" / "Maybe like late September, early October seems about right" — with a non-base-case tail (2013-2014 analog) pushing the bottom to December (Wf7vUIoGQrE [07:32], [07:40]).
- A stock-market correction expected in August/September is now the proximate trigger for BTC's final drop to the cycle bottom, rather than sideways drift into November as in the 2018 analog (Wf7vUIoGQrE).
- BTC's ~$57.7K early-summer low is a near-exact 10x match to 2018's $5,743 low, reinforcing the four-year-cycle analog and underpinning the fractal-based timing framework (n9yTkx3r904, 8TmOvGK235I).
- Inflation has been above the 2% target every month since 2021, and under Warsh's Fed, ~84.5% market-implied odds exist of at least one hike by December 2026 — a late-cycle macro headwind Cowen treats as backdrop rather than the primary timing signal (prior).
