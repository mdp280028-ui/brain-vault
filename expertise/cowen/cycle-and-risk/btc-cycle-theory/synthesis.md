# BTC Cycle Theory

Cowen's framework that Bitcoin and broader risk assets follow an approximately four-year cycle of bull-top, bear-bottom, and accumulation phases. He frames the cycle as macro-driven rather than halving-driven, citing parallel equity-market cycle precedents going back to the 1950s.

## Current Thesis

The Q4 2026 cycle-low thesis is intact but the near-term picture has sharpened materially: BTC has been rejected at the bear market resistance band (20-week SMA / 21-week EMA) — a pattern confirmed across all prior midterm bear years (2014, 2018, 2019, 2022) — and two consecutive red weeks have materially raised the probability that June 2026 marks a cyclical low, with a sweep of the April lows (~$60K) now called "highly probable" within the next couple of months. Two scenario paths remain: accelerating losses lead to a June low and bounce into July–August; BTC holding elevated shifts to the 2014 analog with decline delayed to August. The macro bear pillars deepen — the market is now pricing rate hikes rather than cuts (driven by elevated oil/energy prices), crypto lacks the AI-earnings catalyst lifting the S&P, and the BTC/SPX ratio has been rejected at the same resistance band, with BTC expected to underperform the S&P for at least several more months before outperformance possibly resumes in 2027. The BoJ's June meeting is now an explicit timing catalyst: the August 2024 precedent (BTC sold off sharply after the BoJ hike, then found a low within a week) is Cowen's template for a potential June capitulation-then-bounce.

## Key Evidence

- The 2019 QT-end analog is Cowen's primary cycle comparison: BTC topped 2 months before QT ended in both 2019 (June peak, August QT end) and the current cycle (October 2025 peak, December 2025 QT end), with a ~52–53% decline preceding the countertrend rally in each case. (zKHgKnv51r0)
- June is a recurring seasonal turning-point in BTC bear markets, and the 20-week cycle between successive bear-market lows (observed in 2014 and 2018) places the next BTC low in late June 2026 — coinciding with the Fed meeting (June 17) and a potential BoJ rate decision; two consecutive red weeks in May have materially raised the probability that June becomes a low rather than a high. (zKHgKnv51r0, WHK1S6MrlCA, mIjviu7Q4ag, dy3E7Jzte88)
- BTC and the BTC/SPX ratio have both been rejected at the bear market resistance band (20-week SMA / 21-week EMA) — a pattern confirmed across all prior midterm bear years (2014, 2018, 2019, 2022) — and the 200-day MA rejection in May 2026 (~$78K) is consistent with bear-market capping behavior in 2018 and 2022. (RxD-bxH6tMg, M5d5fbiSfhc, mIjviu7Q4ag, dy3E7Jzte88)
- Stablecoin dominance (USDT + USDC combined) formed a 2+ year base and broke out above it; Cowen argues bases of this size almost never produce false breakouts, making the trend structurally durable and pointing to continued capital sitting out of risk assets. (mIjviu7Q4ag)
- On-chain levels anchor the Q4 $40K target: realized price (~$54K) and balanced price (~$39K) have both been breached to the downside in every historical BTC bear market (2011, 2014, 2015, 2018, 2020, 2022), making a Q4 print near $40K consistent with all prior cycle lows. (WHK1S6MrlCA)
- BTC midterm years (Year 2 of the presidential cycle) have averaged −65% historically; cash outperforms crypto until Q4 while gold typically bottoms in summer and rallies H2 — BTC would need to fall ~40% vs. gold just to return to its 2022 relative level, and the current Republican sweep is one of the historically weaker configurations for Bitcoin. (9a-LA7tm8Mo, M5d5fbiSfhc, 8PQee-onXrU)
