# DXY Dollar Strength

The US Dollar Index (DXY) as a counter-indicator for risk assets. Cowen treats sustained DXY weakness as supportive for Bitcoin and SPX, and DXY strength as a headwind that compresses risk-asset valuations.

## Current Thesis

Cowen frames the DXY through a political-cycle lens: the dollar tends to drop the most under Republican sweeps (the current regime since 1980) and performs best under a Democratic president with a Republican Congress. Midterm years have historically been weak for the dollar on average, though recency bias from 2018 and 2022 midterm strength obscures this pattern. The implication is that DXY weakness in the current regime is the historical norm, which would be structurally supportive for risk assets and Bitcoin — though Cowen does not make an explicit bullish risk-asset call on this basis in this brief.

## Key Evidence

- Republican sweep regimes have historically produced the largest DXY declines since 1980; the current political setup (Republican sweep) fits this pattern (9a-LA7tm8Mo)
- Democratic president + Republican Congress is the strongest regime for the dollar — flagged as a potential future headwind for risk assets if Congress flips post-2026 midterms (9a-LA7tm8Mo)
- Midterm years are on average weak for the dollar, a trend obscured by the strong-dollar midterm years of 2018 and 2022 creating recency bias (9a-LA7tm8Mo)
- DXY is listed among Cowen's referenced tools/indicators alongside the ITC business cycle chart and presidential cycle, signaling it is an active input to his macro framework (9a-LA7tm8Mo)
