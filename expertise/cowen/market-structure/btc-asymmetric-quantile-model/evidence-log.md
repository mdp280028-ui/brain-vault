# Evidence log: Btc Asymmetric Quantile Model

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-30 (uFn3KUE-VTI): Cowen introduces a rigorously fitted asymmetric quantile model that applies distinct curvature to Bitcoin's lower and upper price tails, explicitly crediting Giovani (power law) and Real Plan C (exponential decay upper tail) as predecessors. BTC sits at the 9.4th percentile with Q1 structural support at ~$62K; the four prior Q1 dislocation events translate to today's equivalent downside levels of $57–58K, $51–52K, $48–49K, and a discounted $40K. At the upper tail, both the 2019 and 2025 peaks stalled at roughly the 75th percentile, and Cowen states directly: "Bitcoin never became euphoric because it topped out before getting to the 95th percentile." [25:20]

- 2026-06-04 (QMn7cm4nfYU): With BTC trading live at ~$61.8K and briefly dipping to $61.3K, Cowen confirms the asymmetric quantile regression model (described as his proprietary working paper) shows Bitcoin has already crossed below Q1 — the 1st percentile floor. He uses the model's four historical dislocation analogs to lay out the conditional support sequence for the current bear phase: ~$58K (FTX-crash equivalent, would sweep the Feb 2026 low), ~$52K (March 2020 equivalent), ~$48K (August 2015 level), and ~$40K (corresponding to the balance price at ~$38.65K). No direct quote from the model's presentation is included in the brief, though the framing is operationalized as: "I don't think I can remain a bear if we go to 40" [27:03], implying the $40K level is the outermost boundary the model would ratify as a cycle low.

- 2026-06-11 (R9vEpIpyj2s): Cowen's realized-price analysis places the current network cost basis at ~$53–54K, which falls directly within the asymmetric quantile model's Q1 dislocation floor sequence ($52K March 2020 analog, $48K August 2015 analog) — BTC is trading just above it, meaning the historical capitulation condition remains unsatisfied. He explicitly notes that a break below realized price "would break some models" but frames it as a real possibility, invoking "all models are wrong, some are useful" [03:29]: "there's a lot of models that would say that 60k is the low, and that we won't go lower, but you know, those are certainly more optimistic models." The realized-price framework thus corroborates rather than contradicts the asymmetric model's deeper bear-case floors, with both pointing to the low not yet being in.

- 2026-06-22 (d71wYhwKpFY): The June 22 video does not invoke the asymmetric quantile model by name but directly reinforces its core lower-tail implication: the realized price (~$53–54K), which falls within the model's Q1 dislocation floor sequence, remains unbreached. And so, for me it makes me think that we likely will go below it at some point later on this year" [02:35], sustaining the thesis that the model's deeper analog floors ($52K, $48K) are still live targets and the cycle low is not yet confirmed.
