# Diminishing Returns Thesis

Cowen's thesis that each successive Bitcoin bull cycle delivers a lower percentage return than the prior cycle. Used to set realistic cycle-top targets and ground long-horizon expectations.

## Current Thesis

Cowen holds that the four-year cycle is still intact but delivering diminished returns each cycle — a pattern visible in both price ROI metrics and in the MVRV Z-Score, where cycle peaks have compressed from ~10 (2013) to ~7 (2021) to ~3 (most recent cycle). The current MVRV Z-Score reading of ~0.35 is approaching the zero threshold, which Cowen frames not as cycle death but as the normal reset that historically precedes the next bull market setup. He treats the attenuated peak and the forthcoming below-zero accumulation window as consistent with a maturing, halving-driven cycle — diminishing in magnitude but not in structure.

## Key Evidence

- MVRV Z-Score cycle peaks have compressed with each successive cycle: ~10 in 2013, ~7 in 2021, ~3 in the most recent cycle — a direct quantitative expression of diminishing returns at the top (hXlR5xu0HK4).
- The most recent cycle peak of ~3 resembled the "apathetic top" pattern of 2019, consistent with a cycle that exhausted itself at a lower ceiling than prior cycles (hXlR5xu0HK4).
- Current MVRV Z-Score is ~0.35, well off the cycle peak, signaling the current cycle's momentum has largely dissipated and the market is transitioning toward the reset phase (hXlR5xu0HK4).
- At the equivalent stage in the 2014 cycle, BTC's ROI from the yearly open was 0.54; the current reading was 0.62 — close enough that Cowen treats it as confirmation the cycle is tracking within normal diminishing-returns variance (CNiGShpj3M0).
- BTC is underperforming every major asset class (S&P 500, Nasdaq, gold, energy) on a YTD basis as of May 2026, with price recovery only returning to ~February 2026 levels — a hallmark of a cycle with weakening momentum (PnveYgxtd1w).
- Cowen explicitly does not expect a within-cycle catch-up trade, framing any meaningful recovery as a next-cycle event: "perhaps in the next cycle we'll see it go back up" (PnveYgxtd1w).
