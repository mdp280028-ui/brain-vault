# Bitcoin Dominance

BTC's market-cap share of the total crypto market. Cowen tracks dominance as the primary lens for altcoin-season timing — falling dominance signals capital rotation into alts, rising dominance signals BTC-favoring regime.

## Current Thesis

BTC dominance excluding stablecoins continues making higher highs, with headline dominance suppressed only by rising stablecoin dominance — capital sheltering in stables, not rotating into alts. The stablecoin dominance chart is replaying the 2022 bear market pattern precisely: weekly closes below the 21-week EMA followed by bounces, each time preceding resumed bear market conditions. The macro backdrop reinforces the BTC-favoring regime: zero cuts priced through 2027, with a hike more probable than a cut, removing the liquidity catalyst altcoins require. A new dimension now frames BTC's own subordinate position: just as altcoins were "annihilated" relative to BTC in prior cycles when BTC dropped, BTC is now in that same risk-curve subordinate position relative to the S&P 500 — it participates in equity rallies but gets hit harder on pullbacks, consistent with a BTC bear market that further entrenches dominance of the asset class's defensive tier (stablecoins and, eventually, BTC itself).

- The risk-curve hierarchy — alts annihilated vs BTC, now BTC hit harder vs SPX — structurally confirms capital does not rotate into lower-risk-curve assets during drawdowns, reinforcing BTC dominance over alts (OuHBTQ6fkfQ)
