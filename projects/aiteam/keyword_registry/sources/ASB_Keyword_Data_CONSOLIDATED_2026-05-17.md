### Executive Summary

The asbestos keyword ecosystem is **~4-5x larger** than the April 22 research clone estimated. The original clone projected ~18,200 SV addressable across test kit + directory; actual addressable pool after related-terms pulls is **~150,000+ SV** when the equipment/PPE parallel cluster is included.

**Two strategic pivots vs. the clone:**

1. **Popcorn ceiling is now a flagship anchor cluster (~10,800 SV)** — tied with test kit as Phase 1 priority, not a sub-cluster.
2. **Equipment/PPE parallel cluster (~80,000 SV)** — respirators, Tyvek suits, HEPA vacuums, air scrubbers — was not in the clone and represents the largest single addressable pool in the entire research sweep. This reframes the site from "asbestos directory + test kit affiliate" to "renovation/hazmat PPE + asbestos authority."

**Single best keyword: `asbestos test kit`** — 6,200 SV / KD 3 / $0.70 CPC.
**Highest-CPC keyword: `does plaster have asbestos`** — 300 SV / KD 0 / **$20.00 CPC** (mesothelioma legal adjacent, SERP-check required).
**Biggest sleeper: `air scrubber`** — 12,000 SV / KD 2 / $0.60 CPC (cross-niche equipment).
**Biggest asbestos-specific sleeper: `transite pipe`** — 1,400 SV / KD 0 / **$7.00 CPC** (commercial water utility).

### Part 7: Strategic Rules Learned During Research

#### Rules Already Established (from April 22 clone, confirmed in MSG 4-17)

1. **Metro-first, NOT state-first.** State terms are ~1/30th metro volume for asbestos.
2. **Affiliate-first sequencing.** Test kit hub ships before directory expands.
3. **Buyer vocabulary, not contractor vocabulary.** Generic "asbestos removal [city]" beats "licensed asbestos contractor [city]" (which returns NULL).
4. **Info-intent traffic is internal link fuel.** $0.02 CPC on 12K SV = no commerce.
5. **CPC+KD trap rule.** Attractive CPC/KD in consolidated verticals triggers SERP-check.

#### New Rules Established in MSG 4-17

**6. CPC below $0.15 = non-commercial traffic regardless of SV.**
"What does asbestos look like" at $0.02 is the classic tell.

**7. CPC above $5 + legal-adjacent term = DR 80+ legal SERP, skip.**
Mesothelioma law firms bid everything from $5-400. Never winnable without decade of authority.

**8. Always check parent topic before writing.**
Vermiculite 44K SV is gardening. PACM 150 SV is Princeton math. Amiante is French. Fire suit is racing. Lava suit is scuba. Ahrefs lists SV but Google has decided what the query means.

**9. Misspellings are orphan traffic.**
Vermeculite (250 SV KD 6), resperator (800 SV KD 12), tyvex (300 SV KD 2), airscrubber (300 SV KD 2) — competitors ignore, easy wins.

**10. Material clusters need a friable/nonfriable hub at the center.**
1,160-2,000 SV friable cluster at KD 0-2 isn't about ranking — it's the topical authority anchor every material page (popcorn, pipe, tile, siding, mastic) links back to.

**11. Year-of-home triggers are scenario-specific buyer entry points.**
"Does a house built in 1976 have asbestos" at KD 0 / $1.40 CPC proves this works. Each decade variant = its own page.

**12. Cross-niche product pages multiply addressable pool.**
Same respirator page competes in asbestos + mold + lead + construction + renovation + biohazard. Build once, rank four times.

**13. Rental affiliates beat Amazon on equipment.**
Air scrubber Amazon sale: $1-3 commission. Air scrubber rental conversion: $20-40 commission.

**14. Info-to-commerce bridges are concentrated around trigger moments.**
"Does my house have asbestos" ($1.40 CPC) vs "what does asbestos look like" ($0.02 CPC) = same curiosity, different moments. Buyer-trigger phrasing converts.

**15. Brand queries convert higher than comparison queries.**
EMSL Analytical (600 SV KD 3) has a credit-card-ready searcher. Best asbestos test kit (200 SV KD 3) has a researcher. Build both, prioritize brand.

**16. Stop after 3 consecutive pulls of diminishing returns.**
MSG 13-15 surfaced mostly fold-ins to existing clusters. MSG 16-17 reopened the pool via equipment cross-niche angle. The test is whether new anchors emerge, not whether any keywords return.

### Appendix B: Keyword Research Principles (Established in April 22 Session)

1. **CPC below $0.15 = non-commercial traffic.** Regardless of SV, these convert poorly. "What does asbestos look like" at $0.02 is the archetype.
2. **CPC + KD together reveal saturation.** High SV + low CPC = oversupply of bidders = saturated vertical.
3. **NULL returns are data.** NULL usually means "not how people search," not "not enough volume to show."
4. **State vs metro split depends on buyer type.** Regulated B2B = state-first (UST). Residential one-time service = metro-first (asbestos).
5. **Info-intent terms are internal link fuel.** Build, don't monetize.
6. **Material-specific long-tails are cheap wins.** KD 0-1 dominates specialized application queries.
7. **Throwaway keyword sets contain the signal.** Research must be wide enough to catch the unexpected.
8. **Stop after three boring blocks.** Diminishing returns hit fast after 200 keywords in a vertical.
9. **High CPC does not equal SERP lockout.** Legal bid inflation (e.g., plaster at $20 CPC) can coexist with a rankable content SERP. Verify composition before assuming lockout.
10. **Buyer vocabulary, not contractor vocabulary.** Buyers search "asbestos removal [city]," not "licensed asbestos contractor [city]."
