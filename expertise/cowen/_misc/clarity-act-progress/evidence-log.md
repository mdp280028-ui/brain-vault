# Evidence log: Clarity Act Progress

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-21 (8PQee-onXrU): The Clarity Act passed Senate Banking Committee markup 15-9 with two Democrats, but both flagged those votes would not carry to the full Senate floor. The central deadlock is an ethics-language standoff: Democrats won't vote without provisions barring politicians from profiting from the industry they regulate, while Trump has signaled he will not sign a bill containing such language. The White House wants a signing by July 4th, but Cowen expects the bill to keep slipping and anticipates it will still be unresolved through midterms, potentially passing only in Q4 2026 — at which point, as he noted at [06:33], "that'd be kind of interesting" given it would coincide with the expected Bitcoin cycle low.

- 2026-06-18 (xutthOFM_RA): The Clarity Act's prolonged delay is framed as the primary drag on crypto market momentum — Cowen notes the bill has been "about to pass" for eight months with no resolution, leaving the market in a holding pattern. Passage is treated as a concrete upside catalyst: "If the Clarity Act gets passed, I can see a lift in the market." [04:38]. No new details on the legislative mechanics emerged; the update is confirmatory — the impasse documented in May remains intact as of mid-June.

- 2026-07-09 (OgvqZ2BiHZw): The panel (Cowen, Rob, Nick) now characterizes Clarity Act passage as unlikely this year, and unlikely even if Democrats retake the House, marking a hardening from prior "delayed but possible" framing. The sticking point remains the provision barring officials and their family members from trading crypto, which has become especially hard to pass politically after Trump's roughly $1.4 billion in crypto-related profits became a talking point — tying the legislative deadlock directly to Trump's personal financial exposure rather than a purely partisan dispute.
