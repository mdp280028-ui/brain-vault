# Business Cycle Phase

The position of the US economy within its expansion-peak-contraction-trough business cycle, including Cowen's M2-normalized composite (SPX, unemployment, rates, inflation, M2). Cowen overlays the business cycle with Bitcoin's four-year cycle to time long-horizon allocation, treating cycle-bottoms as accumulation windows.

## Current Thesis

Cowen maintains a late-cycle macro framework — inflation persistently above target, the Fed under Warsh tilted toward hikes, and real disposable income turning negative — but continues to treat the Bitcoin four-year cycle as the dominant, more reliable signal, having proven out almost mechanically against its 2018 analog regardless of the macro narrative. He now says there's an "increasing probability" the early-summer ~$57.7K low is the cycle low, given its near-exact 10x match to 2018's $5,743 low and the shared February-low/spring-higher-low/May-rejection/June-July-sweep structure, though he still expects one more rejection at the 200-day moving average around late July/early August before any final bottom. Using a low-to-low ROI day count (day 1331 of the cycle), he now anchors his timing to the last two cycles' ~100-day (~3 month) gap from a comparable point, pointing to a final, stock-market-correction-driven cycle bottom around late September–October, possibly slipping into Q4 but "not much later." He continues to soften macro's predictive weight for cycle timing while keeping it as backdrop (flagging a possible September rate hike and a dollar that should "begrudgingly" grind higher through the midterm year as headwinds), and reiterates DCA through the second half of midterm years as the strategy rather than timing the exact bottom.

## Key Evidence

- BTC's ~$57.7K early-summer low is a near-exact 10x match to 2018's $5,743 low, sharing a February low, higher low in spring, May rejection at the 200-day MA, and a June/July sweep lower — reinforcing the four-year-cycle analog ("in 2026 the low coincidentally is 57,000, right? So, again, it's just a 10x move higher." — oj2n8nkTTTE).
- Using ROI-measured-from-low-to-low, Bitcoin is on "day 1331" of the cycle, and the last two cycles bottomed about 100 days (~3 months) from a comparable point, implying a late September/October bottom, with Cowen concluding "I can't find any other indicators that have been more helpful than just assuming that Bitcoin bottoms and tops in sort of this normal cadence" (oj2n8nkTTTE).
- Cowen distinguishes time-based capitulation (his base case: a grinding, months-long bear market) from price-based capitulation (a sudden violent event like 2020 or 2022 FTX/Luna), and says no pandemic-style price-based capitulation signs have appeared yet (8TmOvGK235I).
- Seasonality is not universal: 2022 (BTC crashed in May instead of rallying) and 2014 (BTC fell in July instead of rallying) both broke from the "normal" pattern, so Cowen treats the 2018 analog as probabilistic, not guaranteed (8TmOvGK235I).
- The current ~51% drawdown from highs is far milder than the 71% drawdown at the same point in the 2022 cycle, suggesting this bear market is comparatively less brutal despite lower forward returns (oj2n8nkTTTE).
- Inflation has been above the 2% target every month since 2021, and under Warsh's Fed, ~84.5% market-implied odds exist of at least one hike by December 2026, with a possible September hike flagged as a headwind that could coincide with capitulation (prior; oj2n8nkTTTE).
