# BTC Cycle Theory

Cowen's framework that Bitcoin and broader risk assets follow an approximately four-year cycle of bull-top, bear-bottom, and accumulation phases. He frames the cycle as macro-driven rather than halving-driven, citing parallel equity-market cycle precedents going back to the 1950s.

## Current Thesis

The Q4 2026 cycle-low thesis is intact and Cowen's most comprehensive standalone treatment to date formalizes the midterm-year framework explicitly: the four-year cycle divides into four year-types (post-halving, election, pre-election, midterm), with midterm years — 2014, 2018, 2022, and now 2026 — serving as the primary window of weakness for the entire cycle. H1 counter-trend rallies are structurally traps, not entries; the correct posture is to ignore BTC through H1 and begin looking for deals only in H2. The roadmap — June local low, July–August counter-trend rally (explicitly not the final low), September–October ultimate capitulation — is now stated as a near-certain sequence rather than a conditional one, with the June BoJ meeting flagged as the most likely near-term catalyst. The distilled behavioral rule is unchanged: fade every H1 rally, buy at the end of midterm years, sell at the end of post-halving years — all other indicators treated as noise.

## Key Evidence

- BTC topped on cycle day 1,162 — within one week of the prior two cycles' tops (days 1,059 and 1,168) — making cycle-day timing precision the single strongest structural argument that the four-year cycle is intact. (7byCfkDQxq8)
- The midterm-year classification formalizes why this year is structurally bearish: BTC dropped in H1 of every midterm year (2014, 2018, 2022, 2026), and all three prior midterm cycles produced a significant June low — making the June-low call a seasonality claim with 100% historical hit rate, caveated at ~70% accuracy overall. (dIa8HUYDNEY)
- The current cycle mirrors 2018 at every structural stage: February 2026 low = February 2018 low; higher low in late March/April 2026 = April 2018; early-May 2026 peak = early-May 2018 peak; 200-day MA rejection in 2026 matches 2018 and 2022 precisely. The midterm pattern repeats: rally above 21-week EMA → push to 200-day MA → retrace → one more bounce → roll over lower. (7byCfkDQxq8, M5d5fbiSfhc, slTIiS-Y65k, dIa8HUYDNEY)
- Multi-panel convergence: Cowen, Guy, and Ben independently arrived at the same June-low → July/August-bounce → Q4-final-low sequence in a live discussion, with Ben treating it as a reliable pattern with 2014, 2018, and 2022 precedents. The absence of a blowoff top explains the dull grind rather than a sharp crash-and-bounce — a cycle-structure nuance not present in prior cycles. (Tj7mvTHDe1U)
- Cowen's distilled rule — buy BTC at the end of midterm years, sell at the end of post-halving years — is explicitly stated as the only indicator that ever worked; all others failed to signal the absent blowoff top and add noise rather than edge. The bottom is time-based, not price-based, with the behavioral corollary: "Usually, the way to be successful at Bitcoin is to ignore it for the first half of the midterm year. Pretend like it doesn't exist." [12:58] (slTIiS-Y65k, Tj7mvTHDe1U, dIa8HUYDNEY)
- On-chain levels anchor the Q4 target: realized price (~$54K) and balanced price (~$39K) have been breached to the downside in every historical BTC bear market; the 200-week MA will come into play before the bear market ends. At the equivalent 2014 stage, BTC's ROI from the yearly open was 0.54 vs. 0.62 now — near-identical variance confirming normal cycle tracking. (WHK1S6MrlCA, slTIiS-Y65k, CNiGShpj3M0)
