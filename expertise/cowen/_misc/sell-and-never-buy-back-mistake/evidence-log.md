# Evidence log: Sell And Never Buy Back Mistake

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-06-16 (vTcABO_0Exg): Cowen frames the sell-and-never-buy-back pattern as the defining investor error in Bitcoin bear markets. He validates the emotional logic of reducing exposure — he's done it himself — but argues the failure mode is the failure to re-enter, not the initial sale. He specifically calls out investors who sold late last year and still haven't returned, concluding with a direct logical trap: [00:55] "if you're never going to buy back, you would've been better off had you not sold."

- 2026-06-25 (5nIhiijBQt4): The brief does not address the sell-and-never-buy-back mistake by name, but its anti-cash thesis is directly adjacent: both Cowen and co-host argue that sitting in cash guarantees loss to inflation, with Cowen stating [19:26] "you have to own assets over the long term otherwise inflation will eat you alive." Ben's comment that "the time to sit out was eight months ago" further implies that investors who sold and stayed out have compounded the original error by missing the re-entry opportunity now materializing in Q4 2026.
