# Business Cycle Phase

The position of the US economy within its expansion-peak-contraction-trough business cycle, including Cowen's M2-normalized composite (SPX, unemployment, rates, inflation, M2). Cowen overlays the business cycle with Bitcoin's four-year cycle to time long-horizon allocation, treating cycle-bottoms as accumulation windows.

## Current Thesis

Cowen characterizes the US economy as in a late-cycle environment where inflation has remained above the 2% target continuously since 2021 — a structural failure of political will, not a temporary overshoot — and the Fed under Warsh has now institutionalized a posture of restraint, with nine FOMC members projecting a rate hike and markets pricing ~84.5% odds of at least one hike by December 2026. The two-year yield is rising again, which Cowen treats as a near-mechanical forcing function on the Fed funds rate, and the dollar appears to be breaking out of a large base — both additional late-cycle headwinds for risk assets. SPX faces a potential test via a back-half 2026 correction as markets assess whether Warsh will hike into higher inflation or run things hot; either path pushes long yields higher. BTC underperformance relative to all major asset classes persists, with outperformance deferred to 2027 when the next cycle's accumulation window is expected to open.

## Key Evidence

- Inflation has been above the 2% target every month since 2021 and never actually touched 2% even at its lowest; Cowen frames this as a choice — political will to allow asset prices to fall (the direct cure) is absent (zKPohkQdq_Q).
- Under Warsh, nine FOMC members project a rate hike before year-end; CME FedWatch prices only a 15.5% chance of no hike by December — an ~84.5% implied probability of at least one hike, tightening the macro vice further (zKPohkQdq_Q).
- The two-year yield is rising again, which Cowen treats as a leading indicator that has historically forced Fed funds rate hikes, citing 1960s and late-1990s precedents (zKPohkQdq_Q).
- Headline CPI rose to 3.78% YoY in May 2026, driven by a supply-side energy shock — consistent with a late-cycle inflation impulse rather than an early-cycle recovery (Rf-gAQJk5-0).
- SPX is rising on AI-sector earnings rather than liquidity expansion — a late-cycle divergence that structurally advantages equities over crypto, which remains purely liquidity-dependent (dy3E7Jzte88).
- The dollar is forming a large base and appears likely to break out, which Cowen identifies as an additional headwind for risk assets broadly in this late-cycle phase (zKPohkQdq_Q).
