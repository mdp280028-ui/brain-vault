## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin is trading around $77K and has been rejected at the bear market resistance band — a recurring pattern across all prior midterm bear years (2014, 2018, 2019, 2022).
2. Midterm-year seasonality identifies February, late March/early April, and June as the three major windows of weakness; May is typically mild, and July often holds up following a June capitulation.
3. Two consecutive red weeks in Bitcoin materially raises the probability that June becomes a low rather than a high — the logic being that if Bitcoin had kept rallying, June couldn't have been a low.
4. Two scenarios from here: (a) if losses accelerate, look for a June low followed by a bounce into July–August; (b) if BTC stays elevated, the 2014 analog applies — holding up through July before declining in August.
5. The BTC/SPX ratio has been rejected at the 20-week SMA / 21-week EMA bear market resistance band; Cowen expects BTC to underperform the S&P 500 for at least a few more months, then outperform again — likely starting 2027.
6. SPX is rising primarily because AI-sector earnings are lifting it; crypto lacks this earnings catalyst and is instead driven by monetary policy and liquidity, making it structurally more sensitive to rate expectations.
7. The market is now pricing in rate hikes rather than cuts — Cowen calls this straightforwardly bearish for crypto; elevated oil/energy prices are the driver, and he sees structural risk of energy prices rising again in Q3.
8. Macro scenario: energy stays elevated → more rate hikes get priced in → BTC headwind continues through year-end → BTC prices in all of it and begins recovering by late 2026/early 2027.
9. The 20-week cycle between successive bear-market lows (observed in 2014 and 2018) places the next BTC low in late June; both the Fed (June 17) and Bank of Japan meet at roughly that time, with week 16 of the current cycle already elapsed.
10. BoJ precedent: when the BoJ raised rates in August 2024, BTC sold off sharply and then found a low within roughly a week — Cowen flags this as a plausible template for a June capitulation-then-bounce.

## Predictions made
- BTC to sweep the April 2025 low (~$60K) within the next couple of months — called "highly probable"
- June 2026 likely to be a cyclical low for BTC, based on two recent red weeks and midterm-year seasonality
- BTC to underperform SPX for at least a few more months; outperformance resumes likely in 2027
- If BoJ raises rates in June, BTC likely sells off to a low and then bounces (analog to August 2024)
- Energy prices may rise again in Q3, driving additional rate-hike pricing and extending BTC's bear headwinds through year-end
