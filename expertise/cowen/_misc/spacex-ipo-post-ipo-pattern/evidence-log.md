# Evidence log: Spacex Ipo Post Ipo Pattern

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-06-16 (gFiKyyPXMFo): Cowen documented SpaceX's ~50% post-IPO surge and argued it fits the well-established pattern for successful companies: an initial surge lasting days to weeks, followed by a sharp pullback, then a durable long-term advance. The Tesla parallel is the video's spine — both Musk-led companies IPO'd in June of a midterm year, both surged ~50–60%, and Tesla then fell 51% within a single month before establishing a lasting floor. Rivian is offered as the failure-mode counter-example to distinguish pattern-following from guaranteed success. [00:30] "expect to see the price surge after launch and oftentimes a surge in price after the IPO can last you know between one day to a few weeks."

- 2026-06-23 (xK6bqL-QNE4): SpaceX retraced all of its IPO gains within a week of launch, validating Cowen's earlier prediction; he notes roughly 90% of IPOs eventually trade below their opening price after the initial surge. He maps the current action as the "sweeping the low" phase of his multi-step IPO cycle (pop → give back → sweep IPO low → rally → final H2 low), with the Tesla 2010 analog as the primary template — SpaceX has dropped ~34% from its high versus Tesla's 50% drop. A key difference from 2010: the S&P is not near taking out its March 2026 low, making the idiosyncratic bottom timing less certain. As Cowen states at [09:49]: "I think it would be prudent for people to kind of recognize, hey, this might take a while. And also, you know, we're likely going to see prices stay somewhat suppressed for the rest of the year in alignment with the second half of the year midterm weakness."
