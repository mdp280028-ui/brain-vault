## Key points (the distilled video)
1. This is a short clip addressing a single question: what non-time-based indicator would confirm full price-based capitulation in Bitcoin?
2. Cowen's answer: a touch of the balance price, currently at approximately $38K (within the $30K–$40K range), would constitute full price-based capitulation.
3. Historically, Bitcoin has only spent a few days below the balance price — seen in 2011, 2015, 2018, and 2022 — making a sustained break below it extremely rare.
4. The significance of reaching the balance price is that it would fully reset all on-chain indicators simultaneously, removing ambiguity about whether the bare market has ended.
5. Cowen frames this conditionally — he is not predicting it must happen, but states that if it does, it would make the bare market call "a lot easier for a lot of people."
