## Key points (the distilled video)
1. The MVRV Z-Score formula: (market cap − realized cap) ÷ standard deviation of market cap.
2. Historical cycle peaks: ~10 in 2013, ~7 in 2021, ~3 in the most recent cycle — each cycle's peak has been lower, with the last resembling the "apathetic top" pattern of 2019.
3. The key signal Cowen focuses on is not the top but the reset: when the MVRV Z-Score falls below zero, it has historically marked an attractive accumulation zone.
4. Below-zero periods have occurred after every major bull market: 2011–2012, 2014–2015 (~1 year below zero), 2018–2019 (~5 months), briefly in 2020 (pandemic), and June 2022–January 2023 (~6 months).
5. Current reading is ~0.35 — relatively close to the zero threshold and well off the cycle peak of 3–4.
6. Cowen emphasizes that when the score does cross below zero, it rarely stays there for just a day; prior episodes lasted 5 months to over a year, meaning patient accumulation is typically rewarded.
7. The pandemic drop is flagged as the one exception — a very brief dip below zero — but explicitly dismissed as not the base case for this cycle.
8. Cowen reiterates his recurring theme: accumulating Bitcoin in the second half of mid-term years has historically worked out, and the MVRV Z-Score reset aligns with that framework.
9. The indicator is framed as one tool in the toolkit, not a precise top-caller, but reliable for identifying broad accumulation windows that precede the next bull market leg.
10. The four-year cycle interpretation: resets below zero historically mark the beginning of the next bull market setup, consistent with Bitcoin's halving-driven cycle.
