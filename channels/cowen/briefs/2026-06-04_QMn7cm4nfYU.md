## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin is live at its "date with destiny" during the stream — the 200-week exponential MA (~$61.8K) — and briefly dips to $61.3K, going ~$500 below it. Cowen says the capitulation low is forming in real time, likely around the week of June 13–18, matching the timing of prior-cycle June lows (2018: week of June 18th; 2022: week of June 13th).
2. The 2018 analog remains his core framework: February low → late March/early April higher low → May lower high → June capitulation. 2026 has replicated this sequence exactly. He expects the June low to sweep or wick below the February 2026 low (~$60K), consistent with what happened in 2018 when the June low swept the February low.
3. His three-phase bear market map: phase one was late 2025; phase two was the extended counter-trend rally (March–May 2026) that convinced bulls "this time is different"; phase three is now beginning with the June capitulation. One more counter-trend rally (July–August) precedes the final Q4 cycle low.
4. Two independent metrics converge on October as the cycle bottom: (a) the last two Bitcoin cycle bottoms occurred at days 1,424 and 1,432 from the prior low — splitting the difference at 1,428 days from November 21, 2022 lands on October 19, 2026; (b) MicroStrategy took 98 weeks to bottom in the prior cycle — 98 weeks from its current top points to the week of October 5, 2026.
5. The June low is not expected to be the final cycle low unless Bitcoin crashes to ~$40K. A simple retest of $60K would not be sufficient to declare the bear market over; going 30%+ below the February low is the threshold at which he would abandon his bearish stance and call the cycle low early.
6. Key on-chain metrics still not at cycle-low levels: MVRV Z-score is still above zero (historically cycle lows happen below it); composite on-chain risk indicator currently at 0.216 (needs to approach 0.1); running 1-year ROI at 0.589 (needs to drop below ~0.3–0.4).
7. Asymmetric quantile regression model (his proprietary working paper) shows Bitcoin has already crossed below Q1 (the 1st percentile floor). Key price levels derived from historical dislocations: ~$58K (FTX-crash equivalent, would sweep the Feb 2026 low); ~$52K (March 2020 equivalent); ~$48K (August 2015 / near ETF launch level); ~$40K (corresponds to the balance price at ~$38.65K).
8. Realized price (~$53.8K) and balance price (~$38.65K) as bear market floor indicators: going below the realized price would provide confidence the low is in; going below the balance price is historically associated with cycle lows, but each successive cycle has needed a progressively smaller breach — in 2022 it was only breached on an intraday wick. He explicitly cautions against "marrying" the balance price as a hard requirement.
9. Bitcoin vs. gold: BTC was rejected at bare market resistance against gold and he expects Bitcoin to drop ~30% further against gold in 2026, through a combination of BTC falling and gold recovering. Gold is expected to find a low within the next 1–2 months and then trend back up.
10. Accumulation advice: he tells viewers not to panic sell into the June capitulation (the time to sell was into recent strength), and says the right time to start DCA-ing Bitcoin is after the June low forms — a time-based signal, not a price-based one.
