# Evidence log: Lengthening Cycles Thesis

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-28 (slTIiS-Y65k): Cowen's roadmap in this video implicitly confirms the lengthening-cycles thesis: with the final cycle low targeted for September–October 2026 and a bull market resumption not expected until "late this year, early 2027," the current cycle runs materially longer than prior post-halving recoveries. He grounds this in a time-based rather than price-based capitulation framework — "It's not like Bitcoin has to go to some specific price for the market to bottom. It's time-based capitulation" [18:10] — and notes that BTC measured against gold has already been declining for ~18 months, illustrating the bear market's extended duration relative to what the USD chart shows. The 2018 and 2022 midterm analogues he cites both required multi-month bottoming processes stretching across Q2–Q4, reinforcing that each successive cycle takes longer to complete.

- 2026-06-04 (QMn7cm4nfYU): With Bitcoin touching the 200-week exponential MA (~$61.8K) live during the stream, Cowen declared the cycle is tracking the 2018 analog exactly and pinpointed October 2026 as the final cycle low via two independent metrics: days-from-prior-low timing (1,428 days from November 21, 2022 = October 19, 2026) and MicroStrategy's 98-week bottoming pattern pointing to the week of October 5, 2026. He stated explicitly that a mere retest of $60K would not be sufficient to call the bear market over, with ~$40K the only price level that would force an early abandonment of the bearish stance — affirming time, not price, as the gating condition. "So my guess is that a low will form in June. You will get a counter trend rally in July that may persist into August and then a correction in the stock market will cause Bitcoin to form its market cycle bottom in Q4." [23:03]
