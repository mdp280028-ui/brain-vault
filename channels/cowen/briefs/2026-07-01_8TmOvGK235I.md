## TL;DR
Cowen points to Bitcoin's July 1st low of $57.7K as an almost exact repeat of the $5,743 low in late June/early July 2018, reinforcing his "simulation confirmed" thesis that the 2018 seasonal cycle is playing out again; he expects a possible relief rally into July/August followed by a stock-market-driven correction that puts in the final cycle bottom later in Q3/Q4 2026.

## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen's core "simulation confirmed" observation: Bitcoin's low on July 1, 2026 hit $57.7K, almost identical to the 2018 low of $5,743 formed in late June/early July — he treats this as further confirmation of the 2018 cyclical analog he's been tracking since February.
2. He recaps the parallel structure: both cycles had a February low, a higher low in late March/early April, a rally to the bear-market resistance band/200-day MA in May, then a drop back down to sweep the February low in June/July.
3. He explicitly cautions that every bear market differs somewhat — citing 2022 (Bitcoin crashed in May instead of rallying) and 2014 (Bitcoin fell in July instead of rallying) as examples where seasonality broke from the "normal" pattern.
4. His plausible near-term path, mirrored on 2018: a low forms around now, a countertrend rally into early-to-mid July (2018 rallied to ~$6,768 before getting rejected near the 200-day MA), then a pullback to a higher low around mid-July, followed by continuation.
5. He distinguishes time-based capitulation (bear market grinds out over months, his base case) from price-based capitulation (a sudden violent event like the 2020 pandemic crash or 2022 FTX/Luna), and says the market has not shown pandemic-style pricebased capitulation signs yet.
6. He reiterates his one-year-from-the-top bear market thesis, meaning roughly three more months left, but says he'd be willing to pivot sooner if BTC decisively breaks and capitulates on price (or if by October it's still range-bound around $60K).
7. Expected confirmation for a genuine countertrend rally is a higher low forming; absent that, he treats price action as "a falling knife until proven otherwise," citing 2022 as the falling-knife example.
8. He expects any countertrend rally to top out around late July to late August near the 200-day MA/bear market resistance band, then fade again as a stock market correction in the back half of midterm years typically drags Bitcoin down into the final cycle low.
9. His stated strategy: DCA Bitcoin through the second half of midterm years rather than trying to time the exact bottom or trade countertrend rallies, buying some in July as a hedge against missing a rally, saving the bulk of capital for later in the year.
10. He argues macro variables (inflation, money supply, ISM data) haven't mattered for predicting Bitcoin's cyclical turning points — the feb-low/June-low pattern repeated in both 2018 and 2026 regardless of the macro narrative — and frames over-complicating the analysis as counterproductive.

## Predictions made
- Bitcoin bear market expected to last about one year from the top, implying roughly three more months (into Q3/Q4 2026) before a final cycle bottom.
- A possible countertrend rally topping out near the 200-day moving average/bear market resistance band sometime between late July and late August 2026.
- If Bitcoin is still trading around $60K by October 2026, Cowen says he may pivot his view regardless of the one-year framework.
