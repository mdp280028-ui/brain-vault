## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin just tagged the 200-week moving average at ~$61.8K — Cowen calls this "our date with destiny," noting that every Bitcoin cycle follows the same sequence: break below the 50-week MA, then the 100-week MA, then reach the 200-week MA during the post-halving/midterm year. No narrative needed; it's what Bitcoin does every ~4 years.
2. The timing is historically precise: Bitcoin broke through the 200-week MA the week of June 13, 2022; four years later to the week, it has tagged the same level in June 2026.
3. Cowen's base case: BTC sets a local low around mid-June, likely during a "late night in the middle of June," then drifts into a counter-trend rally in July–August — consistent with what happened after June lows in both 2018 and 2022.
4. October remains his base case for the final market cycle bottom. Both 2018 and 2022 saw a June low followed by a counter-trend rally, then a Q4 capitulation to the definitive cycle low.
5. He expects BTC will likely at least sweep the February 2026 low (the ~$60K wick), mirroring the 2018 pattern where the February wick low was not broken until mid-June 2018.
6. If BTC only bottoms around $57K–$58K in June, the jury remains out — that would still leave a Q4 capitulation on the table, just as occurred in both prior cycles.
7. BTC's YTD drawdown of ~29–30% from the yearly open closely tracks the historical midterm-year average of ~32% at this point in the calendar. The midterm-year average trajectory implies a bottom in the ~$50K range in Q4, reached gradually rather than through an immediate crash.
8. The 300-week MA (~$54K) and realized price (just below $54K) are near-coincident and represent his worst-case level on the radar — last cycle, BTC briefly dipped below the 300-week MA before staging a counter-trend rally back above the 200-week MA.
9. The Bank of Japan is expected to raise rates around mid-June (alongside the June 17th Fed meeting), and Cowen names this as the most plausible macro trigger for the current weakness — crypto historically sells off as BoJ hikes unwind the carry trade. He draws a direct parallel to the August 2024 BTC capitulation, which came the week after a BoJ hike in late July.
10. He flags an alternate scenario: outright capitulation (March 2020-style) that drives BTC well below $60K would cause him to abandon the bear thesis. He notes the 2019 analog places the current day count at Day 242 versus the Day 260 capitulation in 2019, so the window for that kind of event is near. He also briefly notes that SPX is at all-time highs but expects a H2 correction — and that Bitcoin often leads or accompanies stock market drops — as a structural reason the final BTC bottom is more likely Q4 than June.
