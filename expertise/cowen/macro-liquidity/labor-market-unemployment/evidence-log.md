# Evidence log: Labor Market & Unemployment

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-08 (APCjkL5gfFI): The May 2026 jobs report shows no recession signal — unemployment steady at 4.3%, initial claims well below the analyst's explicit 300K threshold, and layoffs near pre-pandemic levels. Non-farm payrolls are still growing YoY (~250K) but the YoY rate is approaching zero, and JOLTS data shows openings below available workers with low quits. Cowen ties the labor market's continued resilience directly to elevated asset prices: "one of the things you have to have in order to have a recession and for the business cycle to end is you have to have lower asset prices" [02:42]. A seasonal uptick in claims — and a likely modest rise in the unemployment rate — is expected by late summer 2026 based on confirmed patterns from 2023–2025.
