### The caveat (this matters for future audits)

The "Sonnet wins" result is **transitively guaranteed** given the construction of the test (manual = Opus, Sonnet ≈ Opus at 0.995). What makes the result substantive is operator's OOB step — personally reviewing the 27 articles and confirming ~98% alignment with Opus. If that OOB validation is sound (operator's word), the conclusion holds. CC noted this in the audit_log payload. It's not hidden.

**For future Claude sessions:** if operator ever revisits the editor tier decision and wants more rigor, the move is to have him score independently on a fresh corpus without seeing any AI scores first. Not now — he's burned out on this test.

### The "Opus ≈ Sonnet" diagnostic

This is one of the most valuable findings of the session. **Opus 4.7 is dead weight for editor scoring.** It costs 5x more than Sonnet for r=0.995 identical output. Hold Opus only for periodic confirmation runs (quarterly?) or for tasks where Sonnet might be insufficient (architecture, deep strategy — ai-think.sh tier).

### The "Haiku is generous" diagnostic

Haiku doesn't reliably discriminate slop from quality. It's ceiling-stuck. It can't imagine "6" on a 1-5 scale, so good content piles at 5/5/5/5/5 and the only signal lives at the bottom. **Don't use Haiku as a final editor gate.** It's fine for first-pass triage (filtering obvious garbage) but Sonnet has to be the final judge.

### Cascade pattern (DEFERRED — important to remember)

Production strategy that emerged from this test:
- Haiku scores everything cheaply (first-pass triage, kills obvious slop)
- Anything Haiku scores ≥4 → Sonnet rescores
- Sonnet's score is the final gate

Math at scale: 1,000 articles. Sonnet-only ~$2-5. Haiku-only ~$0.40-1.00 (but publishes more slop). Cascade (Haiku → Sonnet on top ~30%) ~$1.00-2.00 with Sonnet discrimination.

**Status: DEFERRED until production volume justifies.** Captured here so the next chat doesn't forget.
