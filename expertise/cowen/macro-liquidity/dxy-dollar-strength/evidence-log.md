# Evidence log: DXY Dollar Strength

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-13 (9a-LA7tm8Mo): Cowen presents DXY performance broken down by political regime (data since 1980), finding that the dollar declines the most under a Republican sweep — the current configuration — and performs best under a Democratic president paired with a Republican Congress. He also notes that midterm years have historically been weak for the dollar on average, but that strong dollar readings in the 2018 and 2022 midterms have created recency bias distorting that impression. No direct DXY quote is provided in the brief, but the finding is framed as a forward-looking regime signal relevant to risk-asset positioning.

- 2026-06-18 (zKPohkQdq_Q): Cowen observes that the dollar is forming a large technical base and identifies a likely near-term breakout, which he frames as an additional macro headwind for risk assets on top of rate-hike pricing and rising yields. He treats DXY strength as compounding the bearish setup rather than as an isolated factor: "the dollar is sort of forming a massive base down here. And it looks to be likely breaking out relatively soon, which also could be a headwind for risk assets." [12:47] This marks a shift from his prior political-cycle framing, which implied structural dollar softness under the current Republican sweep regime.

- 2026-06-28 (OYcGIifaO4Q): Cowen uses a presidential-cycle overlay to show that DXY followed an identical template across Trump's first and second terms — topping at inauguration, bottoming in January–February of the following year, then grinding higher — and notes he has been calling for this dollar rise all year. He puts a specific target on the recovery: "My guess is that the dollar will likely head kind of back up to that 105, 106 area. And that might continue to be a macro headwind for crypto, you know, in sort of later in the year." [06:02] The dollar's trajectory is presented as a key reason the Biden-era and Trump-second-term BTC cycles look so similar — macro headwinds, not politics, explain the suppressed returns.
