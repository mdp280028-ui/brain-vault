# Business Cycle Phase

The position of the US economy within its expansion-peak-contraction-trough business cycle, including Cowen's M2-normalized composite (SPX, unemployment, rates, inflation, M2). Cowen overlays the business cycle with Bitcoin's four-year cycle to time long-horizon allocation, treating cycle-bottoms as accumulation windows.

## Current Thesis

Cowen now explicitly characterizes the US economy as in a late-cycle environment, defined by rising inflation from a supply-side energy shock and a Fed approaching "checkmate" — unable to cut if labor weakens because inflation is already re-accelerating. Capital rotation in this phase continues to favor equities, gold, and energy over crypto, with energy stocks historically peaking months after the broad market, making them a viable late-cycle hold even at SPX all-time highs. Bitcoin's underperformance relative to traditional assets remains structural through this cycle, with no accumulation window expected until the next cycle begins.

## Key Evidence

- Headline CPI rose to 3.78% YoY in May 2026, driven by a supply-side energy shock, not demand expansion — consistent with a late-cycle inflation impulse rather than an early-cycle recovery (Rf-gAQJk5-0).
- Markets have fully repriced to zero cuts in 2026–2027, with a 2027 rate hike now more likely than a cut — eliminating the liquidity tailwind that speculative assets including crypto require (Rf-gAQJk5-0).
- Cowen frames the Fed as approaching "checkmate": rising inflation prevents cuts while simultaneous labor weakness would leave the Fed without a viable policy defense — a structural late-cycle signal (Rf-gAQJk5-0).
- Energy stocks (XLE) historically peak months after the broad market in late-cycle environments (SPX topped March 2000, XLE held until Oct 2000–May 2001; SPX topped Oct 2007, XLE held until May 2008), supporting energy as the preferred late-cycle hold (Rf-gAQJk5-0).
- BTC is underperforming the S&P 500, Nasdaq, gold, and energy on a YTD basis as of May 2026, with nominal gains only recovering to levels from months prior — no net new cycle appreciation (PnveYgxtd1w).
- Cowen defers BTC recovery to "the next cycle," confirming the current phase is not an accumulation window; the window is a future event (PnveYgxtd1w).
