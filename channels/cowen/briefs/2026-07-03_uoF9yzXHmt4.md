## Key points (the distilled video)
1. The crypto asset class as a whole remains below the "fair value" logarithmic regression trend line and Cowen expects it to stay below it for the rest of this year.
2. He notes the market never got a durable move into overvaluation territory, but says that was expected given the 2019-2020-like monetary policy backdrop.
3. He frames 2023-2025 as analogous to 2019: Bitcoin dominance rose, rate cuts occurred, QT ended, and there was no rotation into altcoins — meaning no justification for a durable overvalued move.
4. Total crypto market cap is currently about $2.125 trillion, well off the 2025 highs and actually slightly below where it was in 2021.
5. He expects the market to try to find support at the fair value trendline over the coming months, then hopefully trend back upward starting in 2027-2028.
6. He expects the rest of this year to be somewhat sluggish, likely forming a low later in the year after a "counter-turn rally" in the summer, before entering a new bull market.
7. In percentage terms, the market is getting fairly far below the fair value trendline, but in terms of duration, this level of below-fair-value has only been seen before going back to 2010 — implying more time may still be needed to play out.
8. Longer term, he still expects the asset class to eventually reach about $10 trillion in total market cap, plus or minus a few trillion.

## Predictions made
- Crypto asset class to remain below the fair value log regression trendline for the rest of 2026, seeking support at that line over the coming months.
- Market likely forms a low later in 2026 following a "counter-turn rally" in the summer, then transitions into a new bull market.
- Trend back upward toward/above fair value expected starting 2027-2028.
- Total crypto market cap eventually reaching ~$10 trillion (plus or minus a few trillion) in a future cycle, with no specific timeframe given.
