## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen references a specific historical episode: in the early part of a given year (January/February), the S&P formed a low. Later, in late June/early July, the S&P took out that low — making a lower low.
2. At the exact moment the S&P broke below that January/February low in late June/early July, Tesla formed its bottom.
3. Tesla never revisited that low ever again — making it the definitive long-term bottom for the stock.
4. The reason Tesla held that low is directly tied to the S&P: the S&P itself also never went back below that level.
5. Cowen emphasizes the durability of this signal: that low has held for 16 years, underscoring the significance of the S&P's lower-low retest as a bottoming mechanism for correlated assets.
