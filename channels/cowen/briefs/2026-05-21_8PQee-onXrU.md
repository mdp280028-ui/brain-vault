## TL;DR
A three-host panel (Cowen, CoinBureau's Guy, Digital Asset News's Rob) surfaces three converging bearish signals for crypto: the Clarity Act stalled on ethics provisions, an imminent oil-shortage inflation spike that makes rate hikes increasingly likely under Kevin Warsh, and Bitcoin's historically poor midterm-year seasonality. Cowen maintains his Q4 2026 cycle-low thesis and explicitly favors cash and gold over Bitcoin until then.

## Key points (the distilled video)
1. The Clarity Act passed Senate Banking Committee markup 15-9 with two Democrats on board, but both flagged those votes would not necessarily carry to the full Senate floor. The central sticking point is ethics language: Democrats won't vote for a bill without provisions barring politicians from profiting from the industry they regulate, while Trump has signaled he will not sign a bill containing such language — creating a structural impasse.
2. The White House wants the bill signed by July 4th to coincide with the 250th US anniversary; after that, the August recess begins and midterms loom, dramatically compressing the window. Polymarket odds sit at ~68-70% passage but Cowen expects the bill to keep getting pushed and suspects it will still be hanging over the market come midterms, potentially used as political leverage.
3. Cowen notes the ironic possibility the Clarity Act might finally pass in Q4 2026 — just as Bitcoin is expected to find its four-year cycle low — making it an interesting but ultimately too-late catalyst.
4. Oil and the Strait of Hormuz closure are the primary inflation driver. Guy argues the US and other nations have been burning through strategic petroleum reserves (SPR) to buffer the shock, but those reserves are running out — real shortages of oil, naphtha, and fertilizer are imminent and have not yet fully hit consumers or been reflected in official data.
5. Kevin Warsh faces a structural paradox: Trump wants rate cuts, but Warsh will be acutely aware of Powell's "transitory" reputational blunder. Guy argues Warsh will not want to underestimate inflation at the start of his tenure, making rate hikes the likely response once oil-shortage inflation bites. Rob raises the possibility Warsh uses real-time inflation data (Trueflation-style) rather than lagging surveys, which could surface far worse rent inflation and force a more aggressive response.
6. AI hype is short-term negative for crypto in Cowen's framing: AI stocks prop up the broad equity market, keeping asset prices elevated and removing the rationale for rate cuts — without monetary easing, the liquidity conditions crypto needs do not materialise. He draws a direct analogy to how Bitcoin elevated altcoins for years before entering a bear market and exposing altcoin weakness.
7. 30-year bond yields are approaching or exceeding October 2023 highs. Cowen raises the structural possibility that the decades-long era of falling rates has ended, which would be most damaging to zombie companies and high-risk assets like altcoins; under tighter monetary policy, altcoins historically bleed back to Bitcoin and Bitcoin bleeds to cash.
8. Midterm-year seasonality: crypto is historically "allergic" to midterm years — cash tends to outperform crypto until Q4, while energy stocks typically outperform crypto in these years. Cowen expects this pattern to repeat in 2026 and is positioned accordingly.
9. Gold has already outperformed Bitcoin in 2026 and Cowen expects it to continue. In midterm years gold typically bottoms around summer and rallies in H2 while Bitcoin sells off; Bitcoin would need to drop 40% vs. gold just to return to its 2022 relative level — "crazy" sounding, but nearly everything else in crypto has already done that.
10. Bitcoin miners are only performing if they have pivoted to AI data-center capacity, a sobering structural signal; meme coin culture continues to represent a massive misallocation of capital that is a structural headwind for any new crypto bull market.

## Predictions made
- Gold to outperform Bitcoin for the remainder of 2026, continuing the current trend (Cowen)
- Gold to bottom around summer 2026 and rise in H2, following midterm-year pattern (Cowen)
- Bitcoin four-year cycle low expected in Q4 2026, possibly coinciding with eventual Clarity Act passage (Cowen)
- Bitcoin likely to see a near-term correction if the Clarity Act fails to advance (Guy, panel broadly in agreement)
- Energy stocks not yet at cycle top; likely to continue higher until the equity market peaks, historically lagging the equity top by 6-12 months (2000: SPX peaked March, energy peaked May 2001; 2007: SPX peaked October, energy peaked May-June 2008) (Cowen)
- Rate hike by the Fed likely when oil-shortage inflation spikes as strategic reserves are depleted (Guy)
