# Evidence log: Spacex Ipo Post Ipo Pattern

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-06-16 (gFiKyyPXMFo): Cowen documented SpaceX's ~50% post-IPO surge and argued it fits the well-established pattern for successful companies: an initial surge lasting days to weeks, followed by a sharp pullback, then a durable long-term advance. The Tesla parallel is the video's spine — both Musk-led companies IPO'd in June of a midterm year, both surged ~50–60%, and Tesla then fell 51% within a single month before establishing a lasting floor. Rivian is offered as the failure-mode counter-example to distinguish pattern-following from guaranteed success. [00:30] "expect to see the price surge after launch and oftentimes a surge in price after the IPO can last you know between one day to a few weeks."
