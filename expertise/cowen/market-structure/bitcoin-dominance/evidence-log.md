# Evidence log: Bitcoin Dominance

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-12 (Rf-gAQJk5-0): With CPI rising to 3.78% and markets pricing out all rate cuts through 2027 (a hike now more probable than a cut), Cowen argues altcoins are bleeding to Bitcoin because crypto sits further up the risk curve than equities, has no earnings floor, and depends on excess liquidity and rate-cut narratives to sustain speculation — conditions that are now absent. The Total 2 (minus USDT) / BTC ratio is cited as showing relentless BTC dominance gains, framed as a direct consequence of the macro repricing rather than any crypto-specific dynamic. No direct timestamped quote on dominance appears in the brief's quoted section; the case is made through the broader macro-to-crypto transmission argument in point 7.

- 2026-05-20 (mIjviu7Q4ag): Cowen argues BTC dominance excluding stablecoins is still making higher highs, and that the apparent stagnation in the headline figure reflects stablecoin dominance rising sharply — not altcoins gaining ground. He draws a direct structural parallel to the A-B-C-D pattern that drove BTC dominance higher for far longer than expected in prior cycles, treating it as the template for where stablecoin dominance (and the BTC-favoring regime) is headed. When the next bull cycle arrives, he expects rotation out of stablecoins into BTC first, compressing altcoin relative performance before any true alt-season can develop. "The trend lasted far longer than most people thought it was going to." [04:31]
