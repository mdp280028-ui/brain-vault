## Current Thesis

Midterm years are historically the worst year for equities and the typical "reset window" for Bitcoin, and Cowen now applies this seasonal roadmap to both BTC and SPX in parallel. His SPX-specific call: the shallow ~5% June correction he predicted played out on schedule (2018/2022 analog), a drift-higher rally on lower volume is likely through July-August, and a larger 10-20% second correction should hit in the August-September window (per 2014/2018/2022 precedent), with the historical midterm-year low typically landing late September/early October. He expects this larger equity drop to coincide with Bitcoin's market cycle bottom and possibly gold bottoming around the same time, tying the SPX, BTC, and gold cycle-bottom calls into one macro-driven midterm-year framework. The underlying mechanism remains macro rather than partisan — a persistently strengthening dollar (echoing the Trump first-term pattern) is flagged as a headwind feeding the later-year correction across asset classes, and Cowen's presidential-term return-path comparisons (Trump's second term tracking his first term, ahead of Biden's equivalent point, slightly behind Obama's second term) reinforce the cycle as a macro-regime proxy rather than a political one.
