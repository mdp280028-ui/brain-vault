# Lengthening Cycles Thesis

Cowen's thesis that each Bitcoin cycle takes longer to complete than the prior one, pushing the cycle-top further out and prolonging bear-market drawdowns. Frequently discussed in tension with the diminishing-returns thesis.

## Current Thesis

Cowen holds that the current bear market resolves on a time-based rather than price-based schedule, with the bull market not resuming until late 2026 or early 2027 — placing the recovery roughly 2.5 years after the April 2024 halving and consistent with each successive cycle running longer than the last. The bottoming mechanism is attrition ("time-based capitulation, people giving up"), not a specific price level being hit. His midterm-year roadmap — June/July low, July/August counter-trend bounce, Q4 final capitulation — draws directly on the 2018 and 2022 templates, which he treats as the lengthening-cycle analogues closest to the present structure.

## Key Evidence

- The projected bull market resumption of "late 2026 or early 2027" places the next sustained upleg ~2.5 years after the April 2024 halving, longer than the post-halving lead times in prior cycles (slTIiS-Y65k)
- BTC measured against gold has been declining since December 2024 — ~18 months at time of video — demonstrating the bear market is already longer than the USD price chart alone suggests (slTIiS-Y65k)
- Cowen's core rule — "buy at the end of midterm years, sell at the end of post-halving years" — structurally encodes a lengthening cycle: the optimal sell window drifts further from each halving as the cycle extends (slTIiS-Y65k)
- The midterm-year capitulation pattern (June low → multi-month bounce → Q4 final low) has repeated in 2018 and 2022, showing the bottoming process itself spans quarters rather than weeks (slTIiS-Y65k)
- The 200-week MA is now above February's wick low, meaning the bear market must persist long enough in time for price to revisit and interact with that level before the cycle can end (slTIiS-Y65k)
