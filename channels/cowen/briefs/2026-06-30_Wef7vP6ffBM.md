## Key points (the distilled video)
1. A month ago Cowen called for a "shallow June correction" for the S&P 500, timed to line up with prior midterm-year corrections (2018, 2022); that correction materialized at about 5%, within his suggested 5-8% range.
2. His broader midterm-year pattern: an early-year low (seen in 2026, 2022, 2018, 2014), followed by a shallow correction in early summer, then a drift-higher rally in July/August on lower volume (vacation season), then a larger second correction later in the year.
3. Historically the larger second-half drop started in September 2014, September 2018, and August 2022 — leading him to expect a similar August/September window in 2026.
4. He estimates the magnitude of the coming larger correction at 10-20%, based on 2018 (20%), 2022 (20%), and 2014 (~10%) analogs.
5. He expects this larger drop later in the year to "reset" the market and likely coincide with Bitcoin putting in its market cycle bottom, with gold possibly bottoming around the same time or slightly earlier.
6. Presidential term return-path comparison: Trump's second term is tracking fairly closely with his first term, currently outperforming the equivalent point in Biden's term and slightly underperforming Obama's second term; the chart suggests the market is close to a rally point that would then set up the larger back-half-of-year drop.
7. Dollar performance under Trump's first vs. second term looks similar — dropping in year one, bottoming, then "begrudgingly" grinding higher; Cowen suggests a persistently strengthening dollar could be a headwind contributing to the later-year stock market correction.
8. Comparing year-to-date S&P ROI in 2026 against 2014/2018/2022 patterns and the average of all midterm years back to the 1920s, he notes the historical average midterm-year low occurs in late September/early October.
9. Cowen reiterates this is not financial advice and doesn't affect his own strategy — he DCAs into low-expense-ratio index funds monthly regardless of the outlook.
10. He frames the eventual later-2026 reset as something that could help "calm the euphoria" and reset metrics, especially in Bitcoin, ahead of the next bull market.

## Predictions made
- S&P 500 to see a drift-higher rally through July/August, followed by a larger correction of 10-20% starting in the August-September 2026 timeframe.
- This larger equity correction is expected to coincide with Bitcoin forming its market cycle bottom, and possibly gold bottoming around the same time or slightly sooner.
- Historical seasonality suggests the midterm-year stock market low typically lands in late September or early October.
