## Key points (the distilled video)
1. The core thesis: crypto needs looser monetary policy for people to "care again," but there is currently no promise of that — the market is more worried about rate hikes than rate cuts.
2. Cowen frames crypto as sitting at the furthest end of the risk curve. In prolonged high-rate / quantitative-tightening environments, the frothiest assets weaken first.
3. This explains the sequence: altcoins showed weakness first (highest risk), and Bitcoin has followed, showing weakness for the past 8–9 months.
4. Cowen's expected trigger for a Fed pivot: stock market weakness in late Q3 / early Q4 2026. Until equities show pain, the Fed has no reason to cut.
5. The AI trade is identified as the key reason rate cuts haven't materialized — as long as it keeps working, there is no pressure on the Fed to ease.
6. Bottom line: the entire crypto industry remains in a holding pattern until the macro regime shifts, which Cowen pins on an equity drawdown later in 2026.

## Predictions made
