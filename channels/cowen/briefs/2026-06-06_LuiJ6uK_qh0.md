## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin has swept the February 2026 low (~$60K) right on schedule — Cowen's stated base case for weeks. The sweep occurred ~17 weeks after the February low, closely mirroring 2018 (where the next lower low arrived 19 weeks later in June). Price path in both cycles: February low → higher low late March/early April → rally to the 200-day MA → rejection → new lower low in June.
2. 2026 is simultaneously similar to 2018 structurally (same price sequence) and to 2019 thematically (Bitcoin topped on apathy not euphoria, no altcoin rotation, topped two months before QT ended — in 2019 QT ended in August; in 2025 QT ended in December). Cowen's framing: "It's not a carbon copy of 2018. It's not a carbon copy of 2019, but there's elements of both." The lack of a euphoric top explains why the cycle feels different: had Bitcoin gone to ~$200K, a drop to $60K would look like 2018; instead it resembles 2019's apathetic peak.
3. Year-to-date ROI is tracking the historical midterm-year average almost exactly: Bitcoin is currently down ~31–32% from the ~$87.5K yearly open, and the historical midterm-year average at this point in the calendar is also ~31%. Cowen's conclusion: "This is a pretty normal midterm year for Bitcoin."
4. Base case remains intact: June local low → counter-trend rally in July–August → final market cycle low in October 2026. The October low would most likely be a lower low than June at current prices. Exception: if there is a massive capitulation in June, October could be a higher low (analogous to the June 1962 stock market bottom where October was a higher low).
5. Explicit if-then framework: (a) If $60K holds → counter-trend rally, then lower low in Q4; (b) If BTC drifts into the low $50s → still likely a lower low in Q4; (c) If massive capitulation like March 2020 → Q4 would likely be a higher low and could represent the actual cycle bottom. Cowen says he currently has to "defer to time-based capitulation" — the October thesis — unless a price-based capitulation forces a pivot.
6. The midterm-year average plus one standard deviation implies BTC could reach ~45% below the yearly open (~$87.5K) by mid-June, putting a potential level around $48–50K. Cowen explicitly calculates this on-screen and names $50K as the implied level, while noting BTC has periodically outperformed the average so it is not guaranteed.
7. Bitcoin historically goes below the realized price (~$53–54K currently) at some point in every bear market — 2011, 2014, 2018, 2020, 2022 without exception. Cowen expects the same this cycle. Timing varies: in 2014 it took until October; in 2018 until November; in 2022 it happened in June. He does not claim it must happen immediately.
8. Pivot condition stated clearly: if there is a large price drop and all on-chain indicators reset, Cowen would shift from time-based capitulation (October base case) to price-based capitulation (declaring an earlier final low). He does not think a pandemic-scale crash is likely — unemployment is at 4.3% and there is no equivalent macro shock — making the 2019/2020 analog the lower-probability tail.
9. Key uncertainty for the short term: Cowen explicitly says he cannot confirm whether the June low is already in. "I can't sit here with a lot of confidence and tell you whether the low is in or not." He says to allow the June low to form over the next couple of weeks and wait for confirmation before repositioning.
10. DCA framing for viewers: buying Bitcoin in the second half of a midterm year historically produces much better entries than the first half. Cowen recommends DCAing after confirming the June low rather than trying to time the exact bottom; he notes most gains come from riding the subsequent uptrend, not from catching the precise low.
