## Current Thesis

The Clarity Act's stall has hardened into an expectation of outright failure this year: after eight-plus months of "about to pass" framing and a missed White House July 4th deadline, Cowen's panel now says the bill is unlikely to pass in 2026 — and unlikely even under a Democratic House — because the core sticking point (barring officials and family members from crypto trading) has become politically toxic following Trump's ~$1.4 billion in crypto-related profits. This confirms Cowen's prior base case of the bill dragging past midterms, but the tone has shifted from "delayed" to "probably dead for now." Markets remain in the same waiting-game posture described in prior briefs, with passage still seen as the clearest bullish catalyst and continued failure reinforcing sideways/range-bound price action.
