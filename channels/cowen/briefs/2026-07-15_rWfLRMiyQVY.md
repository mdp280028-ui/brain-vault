## TL;DR
Cowen says the cooler-than-expected CPI print is fueling Bitcoin's typical mid-term-year July rally, but he still expects the rally to stall at the bear market resistance band and for a resolution — likely to the downside, in line with the 2018 fractal — to play out between August and Q4.

## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Headline CPI came in at 3.5% (vs. 4.2% last month, consensus ~3.8-3.9%), and core CPI came in at 2.6% (vs. forecast 2.8-2.9%, and 2.9% last month) — both cooler than expected.
2. Cowen says this confirms a call he made weeks ago: a low in late June/early July, a rally, a sell-off around the mid-July CPI report, then the CPI coming in cooler and fueling the rally further into late July/early-to-mid August — matching the 2018 pattern almost exactly.
3. He stresses Bitcoin doesn't need a narrative to move this way — seasonality alone explains it; Bitcoin is already up ~10% this July, versus +20% in July 2022 and nearly +40% in July 2018 (both mid-term years).
4. Category breakdown: housing (weighted ~2/3 of CPI) saw a "pretty substantial drop" and was the largest contributor to the headline decline; food & beverage, apparel, medical care, and "other goods and services" also dropped; transportation dropped but less inflationary than last month (contribution fell from 1.5 to 1.0); recreation rose; education/communication was negative.
5. One caution flagged: oil is showing a bit of a bounce — Cowen says he hopes it's just "back-testing" a prior breakdown level and not a resumption higher, since further oil strength would worsen the inflation picture; he'll be watching this in coming weeks.
6. Technically, Bitcoin is squeezed between the 200-week moving average and the bear market resistance band, mirroring 2018 (Feb low, higher low in late March/April, May rejection off the resistance band, sweep of the Feb low in late June/early July).
7. His expectation: Bitcoin may push back up toward the bear market resistance band again, get rejected, and then resolve — with resolution expected in the August-to-Q4 window, consistent with prior cycles.
8. He frames the current move as a second, smaller countertrend rally (after an already-completed larger rally a couple months ago), analogous to Bitcoin's April 2018 rally followed by a smaller July 2018 rally; in 2018 the next real window of weakness didn't hit until late July/early August.
9. He reiterates that 4-year cycle bear markets aren't "down only" — countertrend rallies are normal — and if this squeeze eventually breaks down, it would align with market cycle theory and produce a low later in the year at the normal market cycle bottom.
10. ITC (Into the Cryptoverse) conference reminder: Miami, November 20-22.

## Predictions made
- Bitcoin's current rally likely extends into late July, possibly as late as early-to-mid August, before giving back gains (based on the 2018 seasonal analog).
- If the bear market resistance band rejection plays out as expected, Bitcoin's resolution/breakdown is expected in the August-to-Q4 timeframe, aligning with a later-in-the-year market cycle bottom.
