# Evidence log: Elon Musk Ceo Track Record

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-06-16 (gFiKyyPXMFo): Cowen's bullish long-term case for SpaceX rests almost entirely on Elon Musk's CEO track record rather than SpaceX-specific fundamentals. He draws a direct parallel to Tesla — same founder, IPO'd in June of a midterm year, same early ~60% surge — and uses Tesla's subsequent 51% drawdown (all within one month) followed by a permanent floor as the template for how to interpret SpaceX volatility. The Rivian example is introduced as a deliberate contrast: an IPO that also surged but lacked the leadership quality to sustain it. Cowen's thesis is summarized in his own words: [04:29] "I'm long-term bullish on SpaceX. I do not think it makes sense to bet against Elon Musk."
