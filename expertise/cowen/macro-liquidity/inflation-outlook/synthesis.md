# Inflation Outlook

CPI, PPI, and forward inflation expectations. Cowen uses inflation trajectory as the primary constraint on Fed cuts and the key signal for whether monetary policy can ease.

## Current Thesis

Inflation is reaccelerating — CPI is trending back up year-over-year and PPI is spiking sharply — making any near-term Fed rate cut practically impossible regardless of political pressure on the new Fed Chair. Cowen views resurging inflation, compounded by structurally higher oil prices through 2026, as the dominant constraint that keeps rates on hold through summer 2026 and raises meaningful risk of hikes beginning in 2027. The 2% inflation target has not been hit in five years, and the failure to do so raises the probability of a hard landing rather than a soft one.

## Key Evidence

- CPI is trending back up year-over-year as of mid-2026, directly undermining the rate-cut case (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- PPI month-over-month is spiking sharply, with a recent print described as approximately 6% — a level that forecloses near-term easing (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Oil prices are structurally higher for the rest of 2026; the 21-week EMA has already provided support once and a second surge later in the year is a material risk, which would add further inflationary pressure (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- The Fed's 2% inflation target has not been achieved in five years, undermining confidence in a clean soft landing (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Prediction markets showing >50% probability of a Fed cut within 12 months are viewed skeptically given the current inflation trajectory (FgxAe_NAh5c)
