## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Gold is currently trading around $4,000/oz, having corrected ~28% from its early-2026 peak — a correction Cowen says he predicted would occur in the first half of 2026.
2. Using midterm-year seasonality, gold on average bottoms on day 187 of the year (currently day 162), which maps to late June or early July. Cowen frames June–October as the likely low window, with June–July being the average.
3. Seasonality works roughly 70% of the time; Cowen runs through individual midterm-year examples (2022, 2018, 2014, 2010, 2006, 2002, 1998, 1994, 1990, 1986, 1982, 1978, 1974, 1970) to show the distribution of lows, noting they cluster in summer but can extend into Q4.
4. The current ~28% drawdown is explicitly compared to prior large-but-healthy corrections within gold bull markets: the 2008 correction (~33%), the 1974 correction (~25%), the 2006 correction (~25%), and the 1973 correction (~28%). In all those cases, the bull market top was not in.
5. The monthly RSI reached ~95 earlier in this cycle, which some use as a topping signal. Cowen counters that the 1973 monthly RSI was similarly elevated before gold corrected 28% and still went on to new all-time highs. He argues the RSI signal to watch is a bearish divergence (RSI lower high while price makes higher high), which has not yet occurred.
6. Gold divided by the stock market saw a large breakout above prior highs, then a sharp sell-off — Cowen frames this as a potential "fakeout" pattern, comparing it to palladium and Bitcoin dominance, which broke out, pulled back, and then continued higher.
7. Cowen's structural thesis: the bull market has three legs. Leg 1 is behind us. The current midterm-year correction is the pause between Leg 1 and Leg 2. A second midterm-year correction will eventually separate Leg 2 from Leg 3, with the final leg extending into end of the decade.
8. The bull market support band (20-month MA and 21-month EMA) is the key structural level. Gold stayed above it for most of the 1970s and 2000s bull markets, with brief exceptions in 2008 and 1974. Gold already dipped below it briefly in 2022; Cowen expects this band to provide support again.
9. For gold to print new all-time highs in 2026, it must bottom within the next few weeks (from June 13). If the low extends into September or October, new ATHs in 2026 become very unlikely. New ATHs in 2027–2028 remain Cowen's base case either way.
10. A stock market correction in late Q3/early Q4 (consistent with the typical midterm-year cadence — weakness at start and end of year) could either drag gold lower with it or, alternatively, cause gold to bottom early and then rally as the safe-haven bid returns while stocks weaken.

## Predictions made
- Gold likely finds its low between June and October 2026; on average in midterm years the bottom occurs in June or July
- New gold all-time highs expected in the 2027–2028 timeframe
- New gold all-time highs in 2026 are possible only if gold bottoms within the next few weeks (by approximately early July 2026)
- A second stock market correction is expected in the back half of 2026, consistent with the midterm-year pattern of weakness at both the start and end of the year
