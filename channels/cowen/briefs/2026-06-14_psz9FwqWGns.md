## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen distinguishes between price-based capitulation (a sharp drop that resets all on-chain indicators, like March 2020) and time-based capitulation (simply enduring the bear market until its historical duration is exhausted, ~50–60 weeks). He argues time-based is the more important framework for this cycle unless a price-based event forces a pivot.
2. The last two midterm-year bear markets each lasted approximately 50–60 weeks: December 2017 to December 2018 (~53 weeks) and November 2021 to November 2022 (~54 weeks). The 2013–2014 cycle was ~59 weeks. Bitcoin is currently at approximately week 35, meaning by historical analogy the bear market is not yet complete.
3. The 2019–2020 bear market is the one precedent for a shorter bear market, but Cowen attributes that compressed duration to the pandemic-driven March 2020 crash — a price-based capitulation that reset all on-chain indicators and justified an early pivot. Without a similar event, he does not think the 2019 analogy alone justifies calling the bottom early.
4. His pivot condition is explicit: if Bitcoin were to drop another ~$20,000 from current levels (~$63K at time of recording) and all on-chain indicators fully reset, he would not stay bearish into Q4. But if Bitcoin holds around 60K — mirroring 2018's pattern of a February low, June low sweep, and then a final Q4 drop — he would remain open to a lower low later in the year.
5. The pattern he expects to play out: Bitcoin finds a June low, rallies in July, and then the market debate shifts to whether a full-year bear market is needed given there was no euphoric rally. Cowen expects that debate will be resolved by a final drop in Q4, accompanied by surging volume and likely a stock market decline.
6. Every historical Bitcoin bear market has ended with a massive volume spike — a "wall" of volume that marks the low. This has occurred in 2014, 2018, and 2020 (pandemic crash). The current bear market has not yet produced such a spike, which is a key reason Cowen does not believe the low is in.
7. The supply in profit/loss crossover signal has historically preceded the cycle low by 1–4 months: June 2022 crossover → November 2022 low (5 months), November 2018 crossover → December 2018 low (1 month), September 2014 crossover → January 2015 low (4 months), September 2011 crossover → November 2011 low (2 months). The current crossover occurred in June, pointing to a low somewhere between July and October 2026.
8. Cowen explains why he called the top time-based rather than price-based: Bitcoin never reached the terminal price (the euphoric threshold), it topped on apathy with no altcoin rotation, and the apathetic top coincided with the end of the four-year cycle rather than the middle (as in 2019). These structural differences meant waiting for price euphoria would have caused him to miss the entire bear market.
9. He states he still holds some Bitcoin and believes it makes sense to begin accumulating around the June low, with continued DCA through the end of the midterm year even as prices may go lower. His explicit warning: the biggest mistake sellers make is never buying back.
10. A Bank of England rate hike (described as upcoming at time of recording) is flagged as a potential catalyst that could push Bitcoin below the realized price (~$54K), which would cause Cowen to question whether the June low is in — though he notes even at $54K, the MVRV-Z score would likely not be fully reset.

## Predictions made
- Bitcoin base case: cycle low in October 2026, plus or minus a few months
- Bitcoin likely finds a June low, rallies in July, then faces a final lower low in Q4 accompanied by surging volume and a stock market decline
- If Bitcoin drops ~$20K from ~$63K (i.e., to ~$43K) and all on-chain indicators reset, Cowen would pivot away from the October base case
- If Bitcoin goes to ~$54K (below realized price), it would raise questions about whether the June low is still intact
- Accumulation from the June low through end of midterm year is expected to produce good outcomes for investors
