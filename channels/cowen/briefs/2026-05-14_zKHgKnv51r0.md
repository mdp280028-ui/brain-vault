## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin is pressing against its 200-day moving average in May of a midterm year — a recurring pattern across all prior bear market cycles (2014, 2018, 2019, 2022). In 2018 and 2022 it stalled there; in 2014 and 2019 it briefly broke above before ultimately failing.
2. Monthly Heikin-Ashi candles turned briefly green in the 2014 and 2019 bear markets (the two cycles where Bitcoin pierced the 200-day MA mid-bear); they never turned green in 2018 or 2022. If the current month closes green, it increases the resemblance to the 2014/2019 path.
3. Using the Fibonacci retracement tool measured from the cycle high to the most recent low, the 0.382 level has been the consistent stall point for countertrend rallies across all prior bear markets (2014, 2018, 2022). In the current cycle, that level sits around $85K — which also converges with a trendline channel by early June.
4. The 2019 analog is Cowen's primary comparison: Bitcoin topped 2 months before QT ended in both 2019 (topped June, QT ended August) and the current cycle (topped October 2025, QT ended December 2025). In both cases, a ~52–53% decline followed before the countertrend rally. In 2019, BTC tagged the 200-day MA, pulled back to the 21-week EMA, then broke through and extended to the 0.5 Fibonacci level before rolling over — the current price sequence has replicated the early steps of this pattern.
5. June is historically a pivot month for Bitcoin: when BTC is rallying into June (2014, 2019), that month tends to mark the high; when it is falling into June (2018, 2022), June tends to mark the low. Since Bitcoin is still rallying in mid-May, Cowen infers it is more likely to top in June than to put in a June low.
6. A repeating ~2-week intraday pattern on the daily chart (up Mon–Wed, stall through the weekend, down Mon–Wed, then a new leg up) has been cycling through and suggests Bitcoin may be approaching another near-term push attempt at the 200-day MA.
7. Cowen's macro narrative for the eventual rollover: in 2018 (Trump presidency, midterm year), a second late-year stock-market correction dragged Bitcoin into its final bear market leg. He expects the S&P 500 to follow Bitcoin's 2025 playbook roughly one year behind — a second Q4 2026 SPX correction serves as the most likely catalyst for Bitcoin's next leg down.
8. BTC has underperformed all major asset classes YTD in 2026: –14–15% vs. S&P 500, –13–14% vs. gold, –20–21% vs. silver, –27–28% vs. energy. Cowen concludes that fading Bitcoin in midterm years and rotating into those outperforming assets has been the correct trade, and that countertrend rallies make this feel wrong near their peaks but historically the back half of the year resolves it.
9. Cowen's base-case scenario: BTC tops somewhere between the 200-day MA and ~$85K by early June (with a non-trivial chance of extending into the 90s if the 2019 analog plays out fully to the 0.5 Fibonacci level), then declines into Q4 2026 with a potential cycle low around October — mirroring the 2014 path where the rally peaked in June and the durable low arrived in October.
10. Personal positioning: Cowen has reduced his Bitcoin holdings from bull-market levels but still holds some; he has not sold his index funds despite believing the business cycle is late and wanting more evidence of a durable SPX top. He would only consider re-entering Bitcoin aggressively when multiple indicators simultaneously signal a durable bottom, which he does not expect until late Q4 2026 at the earliest.

## Predictions made
- If BTC clears the 200-day MA, the 0.382 Fibonacci retracement (~$85K) is the next resistance level, potentially reached by early June 2026.
- BTC most likely declines into Q4 2026, with a potential cycle low around October 2026 based on the 2014 analog.
- A second late-year S&P 500 correction is the probable catalyst for Bitcoin's next bear-market leg, mirroring the Q4 2018 pattern.
