# BTC Cycle Theory

Cowen's framework that Bitcoin and broader risk assets follow an approximately four-year cycle of bull-top, bear-bottom, and accumulation phases. He frames the cycle as macro-driven rather than halving-driven, citing parallel equity-market cycle precedents going back to the 1950s.

## Current Thesis

Cowen's Q4 2026 cycle-low thesis is fully intact and now carries an additional near-term bearish shift: the short-term outlook flipped from a counter-trend bounce expectation to outright caution (BTC weeks ↓2), with the panel broadly favoring profit-taking over fresh longs. The macro bear pillar deepens further — oil-shortage inflation driven by SPR depletion makes Warsh rate hikes increasingly likely, 30-year yields are approaching or exceeding their October 2023 highs, and 2026 rate-cut expectations remain entirely priced out. Midterm-year seasonality is now explicitly framed as crypto's structural headwind — "cash is king over crypto until Q4" — and the gold/BTC relative trade is an active call: gold typically bottoms around summer and rallies H2 while BTC sells off, requiring BTC to drop ~40% vs. gold just to return to its 2022 relative level. Cowen adds an ironic timing overlay: the Clarity Act may finally pass in Q4 2026, coinciding with the expected cycle low — too late to matter as a bullish catalyst.

## Key Evidence

- The 2019 QT-end analog is Cowen's primary cycle comparison: BTC topped 2 months before QT ended in both 2019 (June peak, August QT end) and the current cycle (October 2025 peak, December 2025 QT end), with a ~52-53% decline preceding the countertrend rally in each case. (zKHgKnv51r0)
- June is a recurring seasonal turning-point in BTC bear markets — a significant low in the falling cycles of 2018 and 2022, and a significant high in the rising cycles of 2014 and 2019; the 2018 seasonal analog (Feb low → April higher low → May 200-day rejection → June sweep of Feb low) maps directly onto the 2026 sequence. (zKHgKnv51r0, WHK1S6MrlCA, mIjviu7Q4ag)
- BTC confirmed rejection off the 200-day MA (~$78K) in May 2026, consistent with bear-market capping behavior in 2018 and 2022; this rejection coincided with stablecoin dominance sweeping just below the 21-week EMA before recovering — Cowen frames the confluence as structural confirmation of ongoing bear market structure. (Ox0mi5n3Ju4, RxD-bxH6tMg, M5d5fbiSfhc, mIjviu7Q4ag)
- Stablecoin dominance (USDT + USDC combined) formed a 2+ year base and broke out above it; Cowen argues bases of this size almost never produce false breakouts, making the trend structurally durable and pointing to continued capital sitting out of risk assets. (mIjviu7Q4ag)
- On-chain levels anchor the Q4 $40K target: realized price (~$54K) and balanced price (~$39K) have both been breached to the downside in every historical BTC bear market (2011, 2014, 2015, 2018, 2020, 2022), making a Q4 print near $40K consistent with all prior cycle lows. (WHK1S6MrlCA)
- BTC midterm years (Year 2 of the presidential cycle) have averaged −65% historically; cash outperforms crypto until Q4 while gold typically bottoms in summer and rallies H2 — BTC would need to fall ~40% vs. gold just to return to its 2022 relative level, and the current Republican sweep is one of the historically weaker configurations for Bitcoin. (9a-LA7tm8Mo, M5d5fbiSfhc, 8PQee-onXrU)
