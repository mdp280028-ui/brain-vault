# BTC Cycle Theory

Cowen's framework that Bitcoin and broader risk assets follow an approximately four-year cycle of bull-top, bear-bottom, and accumulation phases. He frames the cycle as macro-driven rather than halving-driven, citing parallel equity-market cycle precedents going back to the 1950s.

## Current Thesis

BTC is in Stage 3 of the bear market (June–October 2026), with October 2026 as the base-case cycle bottom defended via time-based capitulation: the bear is at approximately week 35 against a historical 50–60 week midterm-year duration, making the low structurally premature regardless of current price. Multiple on-chain conditions remain unsatisfied — Bitcoin has not broken below realized price (~$54K), no volume capitulation spike has occurred, and the MVRV Z-Score (currently ~0.35) has not yet crossed below zero, a threshold that in every prior cycle has marked an extended accumulation window preceding the next bull market. The pivot condition for early exit remains explicitly defined: if BTC drops ~$20K from ~$63K and all on-chain indicators fully reset, Cowen would abandon the October timeline; absent that, he is actively accumulating from the June low through year-end, framing that window as historically the best re-entry period and the MVRV reset as the setup for the next bull leg.

## Key Evidence

- BTC topped on cycle day 1,162 — within one week of the prior two cycles' tops (days 1,059 and 1,168) — pinning the cycle bottom at approximately Oct 19, 2026 (1,428 days from Nov 21, 2022); the bear market is currently at ~week 35 against a historical 50–60 week midterm-year duration. (7byCfkDQxq8, QMn7cm4nfYU, psz9FwqWGns)
- Every midterm-year bear market has lasted approximately 50–60 weeks: Dec 2017→Dec 2018 (~53 wks), Nov 2021→Nov 2022 (~54 wks), 2013–14 (~59 wks); the only shorter precedent (2019–20) was caused by a pandemic-driven price-based capitulation — a structural exception explicitly dismissed as the base case for this cycle. (psz9FwqWGns, hXlR5xu0HK4)
- The MVRV Z-Score is currently ~0.35, approaching zero; every prior cycle has seen it cross below zero for 5 months to over a year before the next bull market began (2014–15: ~1 year; 2018–19: ~5 months; 2020: briefly, pandemic exception; 2022–23: ~6 months) — and that reset has not yet occurred. (hXlR5xu0HK4)
- In every prior bear market (2011, 2014–15, 2018, 2020), Bitcoin dipped below the realized price (~$53–54K currently) before the cycle bottom was confirmed; BTC has not yet broken below that level, and even a drop to ~$54K would likely not fully reset the MVRV Z-Score. (R9vEpIpyj2s, psz9FwqWGns)
- Every historical Bitcoin bear market has ended with a massive weekly volume spike; 2014, 2018, and 2020 each produced a clear capitulation "wall" of volume that has not yet appeared in the current cycle. (psz9FwqWGns)
- Cowen's distilled rule — buy BTC at the end of midterm years, sell at the end of post-halving years — is explicitly stated as the only indicator that ever worked; accumulating after the June low through year-end aligns directly with the MVRV reset window and has historically been the best-performing approach. (slTIiS-Y65k, Tj7mvTHDe1U, dIa8HUYDNEY, psz9FwqWGns, vTcABO_0Exg, hXlR5xu0HK4)
