## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Crypto is in a waiting game, largely because the Clarity Act has been "about to pass" for eight months and still hasn't — market momentum is stalled pending regulatory clarity.
2. Guy argues a structural shift is underway: digital assets no longer just mean cryptocurrencies. Coinbase's rollout of tokenized stocks means users can soon hold Bitcoin, altcoins, and their equity portfolio all on one platform — crowding out altcoin demand.
3. Europe's MiCA regulations are tightening, with Binance being shut out of Greece at the direction of the European Central Bank — a signal that powerful institutional forces still actively oppose crypto.
4. Bitcoin is currently tracking the S&P 500 more closely, reacting to macro headlines (e.g., Iran geopolitics) in the same way stocks do; Guy sees this correlation continuing.
5. Rob frames the current negativity as seasonal, drawing a direct parallel to June 2022's FUD (Voyager/Celsius collapse, macro fears, quantum computing scares) — his view: accumulate in bear markets, not blow-off tops.
6. On the SpaceX IPO: all three agree the entry point is not now. The IPO launched at ~$130, ran to an all-time high near $220, and the first major insider unlock hits August 11th (20%+ of float). They expect meaningful selling pressure then.
7. Cowen draws a historical parallel: Tesla's IPO in June of a midterm year rallied ~60% then crashed back below its launch price; SpaceX already rallied ~50% from open despite a valuation many orders of magnitude higher than Tesla's was. He notes ~90% of IPOs eventually trade below their IPO-day price.
8. Guy's view on the SpaceX longer term: the 130x revenue valuation is "insane" and based on optimistic assumptions, but he's eyeing it as a genuine long-term hold once the post-unlock dip materializes — possibly as soon as August.
9. On Kevin Warsh/FOMC: Guy characterizes it as a definitive regime change at the Fed. Warsh is abandoning forward guidance, leaned hawkish, declined to give his own dot plot call, and the dot plot itself was roughly 50/50 on rate hikes by year-end. Guy personally thinks rate hikes before year-end are still more likely than not.
10. On AI: Rob showcases practical use cases — rebuilding websites for ~$1/month (vs. $2,500/year), using Claude Code + Firebase for custom business tools, automating nonprofit grant applications via Claude browser control, replacing a $2,100/year email service for $6/month. Guy adds a caution: the current "subsidized token model" won't last; AI companies will need to generate real revenue to justify their IPO valuations, so pricing will rise.

## Predictions made
- SpaceX stock will likely face meaningful selling pressure around August 11th when the first major insider unlock (20%+ of float) hits
- Rate hikes before year-end are more likely than not, per Guy's reading of the Warsh Fed
