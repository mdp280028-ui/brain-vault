## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen defines a three-stage bear market framework: Stage 1 — only a few believe it's a bear market (initial drawdown after the high); Stage 2 — roughly half believe; Stage 3 — the majority believe. Each stage in this cycle lasts approximately four months.
2. Stage 1 was October 2025 to February 2026. Stage 2 was February 2026 to June 2026 — confirmed complete when Bitcoin broke below the February low, producing a lower high followed by a lower low and forcing even prior bulls to acknowledge the bear market.
3. We are now in Stage 3, which Cowen expects to run from June 2026 to October 2026. October (or sometime in Q4) is where he expects the final bear market low to form.
4. The June low may already be in — Bitcoin bottomed early in February (around Feb 6) and the video was recorded June 6 — but Cowen says it is too early to confirm. He is watching for the June low to form before repositioning.
5. Conditional framework for the June–October relationship: if BTC bottoms around current prices (~$60K) and trends back up, the October low is likely to be lower; if BTC drops significantly in June (a more capitulative move), October may not need to be as bad.
6. Historical analog: in 2018, the June sweep of the February low was only marginally lower (~$5,700–5,800). Cowen says not going much below $57,000–58,000 would fit the 2018 sweep pattern and would set up a short-term higher low before a July rally. In 2014, the drops between lows were much deeper (15–18%), suggesting that cycle was more severe.
7. Investor psychology theme: people who convert to bearishness late in a bear market tend to stay bearish too long — they get hurt as bulls in the bear, then stay bearish into the bull. This is why bear markets are so psychologically difficult: timing the mental shift matters as much as timing the trade.
8. Crowd polls as a contrarian indicator: Cowen argues the majority is not always wrong — when sentiment is only 60/40, the majority can be correct. The signal flips when consensus reaches 75–80%. Current reading: roughly 60–65% of respondents are bearish on Bitcoin, which Cowen interprets as the majority correctly recognizing the bear market but not yet at the extreme that would signal a reversal.
9. The bear market ends when everyone believes in it. Stage 3 completing in October would bring Bitcoin to that point of maximum consensus pessimism — historically the precondition for the market turning.

## Predictions made
- Bitcoin sets its final bear market cycle low in October 2026 (or sometime in Q4)
- Bitcoin's June 2026 low is either already in or forming in the very near term
- If the June low is shallow (~$60K area), the October low is expected to be lower; if June sees a larger drop, October may form a higher low relative to June
