## Key points (the distilled video)
1. ETH was rejected at its bear market resistance band after a rally, and Cowen expects a move down to the lower logarithmic regression band — a level that corresponds exactly to the April 2025 lows — which he has been calling for since early in the cycle.
2. In 2021 Cowen noted that the fair value of ETH on the regression model would not cross $2,000 until 2026, which he offers as sobering long-term context for why he has consistently expected ETH to "go home" before any new all-time high; ETH is now trading near that fair-value line.
3. The ETH/BTC ratio is bleeding because tighter monetary policy expectations shift capital from higher-risk assets (ETH) to lower-risk assets (BTC). Rate cut expectations priced in at the start of 2026 have been entirely priced out, and the market is now beginning to price rate hikes instead, driven by Middle East geopolitical conflict pushing oil and energy prices higher.
4. Concrete scenario: if BTC returns to ~60K (where it was in February), ETH could reach the April 2025 lows because the ETH/BTC ratio is already below where it was when BTC was last at 60K — meaning ETH can fall further in USD terms even on an unchanged BTC valuation.
5. Fundamentally, Cowen states he believes Bitcoin is a better asset than Ethereum, citing ETH's pattern of lower highs since 2017 and its underperformance versus cash over a five-year holding period. The ETH/BTC bleed is therefore both cyclical (monetary policy) and structural (asset quality).
6. The current setup mirrors 2019: after ETH "went home" it rallied (~240% in 2019, ~350% this cycle), was then rejected at the bear market resistance band, and bled down to the lower regression band before staging a sizable rally. Cowen is tracking this analog explicitly.
7. In 2019, ETH bottomed in June while BTC bottomed in November. Cowen explicitly flags the possibility that ETH forms a low as early as June 2026, even though his base case for the broader crypto cycle bottom remains Q4 2026.
8. If ETH reaches the lower regression band without a recession materializing, Cowen would begin to look for a "sizable counter-trend move" off that level. Going durably below the regression band — as briefly occurred in March 2020 — would signal recession territory.
9. Bank of Japan catalyst: Cowen observes that historically, major ETH capitulations have coincided with BoJ rate hikes, and the BoJ is potentially set to raise rates again in June — a possible trigger for a large liquidation flush down to the lower regression band.
10. Long-term, Cowen draws a parallel to 2018–2019: during Trump's first term ETH spent significant time inside the regression band and did not break out until the late election year. By analogy, a durable ETH breakout may not occur until around 2028.

## Predictions made
- ETH to fall to April 2025 lows (lower regression band) within the next few weeks, potentially by June 2026
- ETH/BTC ratio to fall back toward June 2025 levels over the same period
- Bank of Japan rate hike in June 2026 likely to trigger a large ETH liquidation event
- Bitcoin to bottom in Q4 2026; ETH may bottom earlier, potentially June 2026
- If ETH reaches the lower regression band without a recession, a sizable counter-trend rally likely follows (conditional on no recession)
