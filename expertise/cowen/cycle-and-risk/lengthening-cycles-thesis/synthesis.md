# Lengthening Cycles Thesis

Cowen's thesis that each Bitcoin cycle takes longer to complete than the prior one, pushing the cycle-top further out and prolonging bear-market drawdowns. Frequently discussed in tension with the diminishing-returns thesis.

## Current Thesis

Cowen holds that the current bear market resolves on a time-based rather than price-based schedule, with the bull market not resuming until 2027–2028 — placing the recovery well over two years after the April 2024 halving and consistent with each successive cycle running longer than the last. The bottoming process itself spans quarters via a three-phase structure (June local low → July–August counter-trend rally → Q4 final low) that directly mirrors 2018 and 2022; two independent day-count metrics converge on October 5–19, 2026 as the specific cycle-bottom window. A new on-chain confirmation has now arrived: the Bitcoin supply-in-profit/supply-in-loss lines crossed in June 2026, a condition that has historically preceded every cycle low by 1–4 months — making the October window consistent with both the time-based thesis and this indicator. Cowen treats the June crossover as the permission condition to begin DCA accumulation, but not as confirmation that the bear market is over, preserving the view that time rather than price governs the resolution.

## Key Evidence

- The projected bull phase of 2027–2028 places the next sustained upleg ~2.5+ years after the April 2024 halving, longer than post-halving lead times in prior cycles (slTIiS-Y65k, vxnpP3EOl-8)
- Two independent four-year-cycle timing metrics converge on October 2026: the last two cycle bottoms occurred at days 1,424 and 1,432 from the prior low (= ~October 19, 2026 from November 21, 2022), and MicroStrategy's 98-week bottoming pattern points to the week of October 5, 2026 (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- The supply-in-profit/supply-in-loss crossover, which has preceded every prior cycle low by 1–4 months, occurred in June 2026 — placing the historical lag window squarely over the October target and adding an independent on-chain confirmation of the time-based thesis (vxnpP3EOl-8)
- The 2026 bear market sequence has replicated the 2018 analog exactly — February low → late March/April higher low → May lower high → June capitulation — demonstrating the lengthening-cycle playbook is tracking on schedule (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- The midterm-year capitulation pattern (June low → multi-month bounce → Q4 final low) has repeated in 2018 and 2022, showing the bottoming process itself spans quarters rather than weeks (slTIiS-Y65k)
- Cowen's core rule — "buy at the end of midterm years, sell at the end of post-halving years" — structurally encodes a lengthening cycle: the optimal sell window drifts further from each halving as the cycle extends (slTIiS-Y65k)
