## TL;DR
Cowen presents a new asymmetric quantile model for Bitcoin in which the lower and upper price tails are fitted with different curvature; with BTC currently at the 9.4th historical percentile and Q1 support at ~$62K, historical dislocation analogs place conditional bear-case floors at $57–58K (2022), $51–52K (pandemic), and $48–49K (2015). The model also explains the absent alt season: both 2019 and 2025 topped at approximately the 75th percentile, short of the 95th+ threshold historically required to trigger durable rotation.

## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen introduces a new "asymmetric tail curvature" quantile model that fits Bitcoin's lower and upper price tails with mathematically distinct curvature — unlike prior rainbow charts and his own earlier regression rainbow, which applied a single curve to both ends. The fit uses post-estimation rearrangement with no constraints.
2. The model traces a lineage of prior Bitcoin price frameworks: original rainbow chart (Trolo) → power law model (Giovani) → Cowen's earlier non-rigorous "non-bubble data" quantile attempt and regression rainbow → Real Plan C's quantile model with exponential decay in the upper tail → this new rigorously fitted version. Cowen explicitly credits Giovani and Real Plan C.
3. At the lower tail, this model is no better than the simple power law, which has reliably found structural lows. The claimed advantage is at the upper tail, where prior models either overshoot (stock-to-flow) or fail to capture the converging, diminishing curvature of successive cycle peaks.
4. Bitcoin is currently at the 9.4th historical percentile — it has spent only ~9% of its time at or below this level. The Q1 (1st percentile) structural support sits at ~$62K today; Bitcoin has broken below Q1 in only four main historical events.
5. Those four Q1 dislocation events, mapped to equivalent prices at today's quantile levels: the 2022 bear market (~5–6% below Q1) → ~$57–58K; the March 2020 pandemic crash → ~$51–52K; the 2015 double bottom during a recession scare (~22% below Q1) → ~$48–49K; an August 2010 illiquid wick (~50% below, explicitly discounted as probably meaningless) → ~$40K.
6. In the upper tail, Bitcoin topped at approximately the 75th percentile in both the 2019 mid-cycle peak and the 2025 cycle peak — never reaching the 95th–99th percentile range that historically coincides with full euphoria. In both cases, Bitcoin topped roughly two months before quantitative tightening ended and the Fed balance sheet began expanding.
7. The absence of a durable alt season in 2019 and 2025 follows directly from this: without 95th+ percentile price action, Bitcoin never became euphoric enough to generate sustained rotation into altcoins. Cowen attributes the pattern jointly to monetary policy (QT timing) and the structural ceiling imposed by the upper quantile.
8. Every Bitcoin cycle has at some point seen a sub-Q1 dislocation; in 2022 it happened in the midterm year, in the 2018 cycle it didn't occur until the 2020 pandemic, and in the prior cycle it occurred in the pre-halving year. Midterm years are the typical reset window but the exact timing within any cycle is uncertain.
9. The "golden pocket" (sub-Q1 price territory) has historically been an exceptional accumulation zone. Time between the 2015 and 2020 Q1 events was ~238 weeks; between the 2020 and 2022 events ~139 weeks. At ~185 weeks since 2022, we are within the historical range, making a late-2026 Q1 dislocation plausible if the midterm weakness thesis holds.
10. The model's end-of-year price table (projecting to 2050) shows: Q1 (1st percentile) reaches $1M around 2041; the 50th percentile around 2036; the 99th percentile at earliest around 2035. Cowen concludes Bitcoin hitting $1M in the next cycle (~2029) or the one after (~2033) is unlikely under any percentile, consistent with his ~2040 estimate from a 2020 video.
