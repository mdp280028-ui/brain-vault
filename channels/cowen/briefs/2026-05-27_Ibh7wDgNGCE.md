## Key points (the distilled video)
1. In February, Cowen called for a ~10% SPX drop based on fading momentum and inability to put in new highs — the drop materialized around the late January/early February top, confirming the call.
2. The post-drop recovery was expected: as long as there is no feedback loop in the labor market (layoffs low, initial claims low), the market climbs the "wall of worry" and returns to all-time highs. That condition still holds.
3. The unemployment map shows rising unemployment in many states but not yet nationwide — prior recessions showed the entire country affected. The number of states with rising unemployment over 6 months has been elevated but has not broken into the "whole country" range.
4. Cowen's core SPX framework is the S&P 500 divided by M2, overlaid as a fractal going back to 1996. The 2023 correction mapped to the 1997 correction (~10%), and the recent 10% drop also mapped correctly to the expected fractal point.
5. He does NOT expect the full fractal to play out to the downside — he explicitly states there is a "really, really good chance" it just breaks to the upside until bears are wiped out before any larger drop materializes.
6. Base case for SPX: small correction in the June-July window (probably June), then a rebound, then a larger drop beginning in September (possibly August) extending into early Q4, then another rebound. The S&P/M2 fractal in time specifically points to September for the larger drop.
7. Midterm year historical pattern supports the two-drop structure: 2022 had a big early-year drop then a second major low in late year; 2018 had a large February drop then an even larger Q4 drop; late 2014 also had a notable drop.
8. Despite SPX making new all-time highs repeatedly, Bitcoin keeps lagging — Cowen frames this as BTC's failure to validate a supercycle thesis. Bitcoin has had the time and conditions to go to new ATHs and hasn't.
9. BTC is now further down the risk curve than SPX: when SPX rallies, BTC doesn't participate fully; when SPX drops, BTC gets hit harder. The 2018 analog is instructive — SPX's second drop was not dramatically lower than the first, but Bitcoin went substantially lower on the second SPX leg down. Same pattern played out in 2022.
10. If SPX tops in September and begins the larger drop, Cowen thinks BTC could react faster (being further up the risk curve) and find a cycle low in Q4 2026, potentially as early as October — consistent with his four-year cycle projection from prior videos.

## Predictions made
- Small SPX correction in June-July 2026 (probably June)
- Larger SPX drop beginning approximately September 2026, extending into early Q4
- BTC cycle low in Q4 2026, possibly as early as October 2026
