## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin has fallen back below the bear market resistance band on the daily chart; the 20-week SMA (bear market resistance band on the weekly) sits at ~74K with BTC roughly $1,000 below it — whether the weekly closes below remains to be seen.
2. The recent rally had very little follow-through before retesting the band. In 2023 there was significantly more follow-through before retesting; this time the minimal follow-through matches the classic midterm-year fake-out pattern where Bitcoin briefly pokes above the resistance band, bulls dunk on bears, and then the air comes out.
3. The standard midterm bear market pattern Cowen is tracking: rally above 21-week EMA → push to 200-day MA → retrace to 21-week EMA → one more bounce → roll over lower. He documented this in 2018 and 2022 and sees it repeating now.
4. Why Bitcoin keeps falling while stocks rise: Bitcoin is further up the risk curve than equities. Inflation creeping back has caused priced-in rate cuts to get priced out, and now rate hikes are being priced in — a macro headwind that persists for at least a few more months.
5. A plausible scenario: Bitcoin bottoms just before rate hikes actually begin (pricing it in ahead of time), then re-enters its bull market late 2026 or early 2027.
6. Historical midterm timing: weak points cluster around June (confirmed in both 2018 and 2022), followed by a counter-trend bounce in July–August, then the major cycle low forms in Q4. Current structure resembles 2018 more than 2022 — a sweep of the prior low rather than a dramatically lower collapse.
7. The 200-week MA is "our date with destiny" — it will come into play before the bear market ends, and notably it is now above February's wick low, meaning even a higher low could tag it.
8. Bitcoin measured against gold has been declining since December 2024 — roughly 18 months — illustrating that the bear market is longer than the USD price chart suggests.
9. Cowen says he will not turn constructive on any rally unless prices move "much higher than current prices"; by Q4 his tone will shift and he will become more openly optimistic as the cycle timing window opens.
10. The only signal that ever mattered, in Cowen's view: buy at the end of midterm years, sell at the end of post-halving years. All other indicators — regression bands, power law, stock-to-flow, on-chain metrics, social interest — added noise, not edge. The bottom is time-based capitulation, not a price-level event.

## Predictions made
- BTC low in June or possibly July 2026, counter-trend bounce in July–August 2026, final cycle low in September–October 2026 (midterm-year pattern, historical precedents 2018 and 2022)
