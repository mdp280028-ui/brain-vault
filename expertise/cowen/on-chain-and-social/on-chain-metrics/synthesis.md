# On-Chain Metrics

Bitcoin and Ethereum on-chain indicators including HODL waves, supply-shock metrics, exchange flows, miner behavior, and realized-price bands. Cowen integrates on-chain data with macro and price signals rather than treating it in isolation.

## Current Thesis

On-chain metrics confirm Bitcoin is mid-bear-market but not yet at a cycle low. MVRV Z-score remains above zero, the composite on-chain risk indicator sits at 0.216 (vs. a target near 0.1), and running 1-year ROI at 0.589 needs to fall below 0.3–0.4 before a bottom signal fires. Cowen treats these readings as confirming his macro/cycle timing view rather than driving it independently — the on-chain picture must align with price action below key levels (realized price ~$53.8K, balance price ~$38.65K) before he would declare the bear market over.

## Key Evidence

- MVRV Z-score still above zero — historically Bitcoin cycle lows occur below this line, so the current reading rules out an immediate bottom (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- Composite on-chain risk indicator at 0.216, needs to approach 0.1 to signal cycle-low conditions; components include pure multiple, MVRV score, MVRV Z-score, transaction fees, terminal price, and thermocap ratios (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- Running 1-year ROI at 0.589; prior cycle lows have required a reading below ~0.3–0.4 (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- Realized price (~$53.8K) as a mid-bear confidence level: trading below it would strengthen conviction that the low is in; balance price (~$38.65K) historically marks cycle-low territory though each cycle has breached it less severely (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- Bitcoin already crossed below Q1 (1st percentile floor) on the asymmetric quantile regression model, with key dislocation levels at ~$58K, ~$52K, ~$48K, and ~$40K corresponding to prior crisis events (QMn7cm4nfYU)
