# Post-Halving Behavior

Bitcoin price and on-chain behavior in the 12-18 months following each halving event. Cowen analyzes whether halving-narrative price action is genuine causal effect or coincident-cycle artifact, generally favoring the latter view.

## Current Thesis

Cowen is focused on whether the current post-halving cycle resolves like 2018 (continued decline after a bounce to the 200-day MA) or 2019 (recovery), and does not yet commit to either outcome. The structural price action — February low, April higher low, May rally to the 200-day MA — has repeated with striking fidelity at 10x the price levels, which Cowen treats as evidence that cycle dynamics are recurring but not as a directional signal. His framing implies skepticism that the halving itself is causal: the repeating fractal predates and spans multiple halving cycles, suggesting broader market structure drives the pattern.

## Key Evidence

- The 2018 post-halving cycle followed a precise three-step structure (Feb low → April higher low → May rally to 200-day MA) that the current cycle has replicated in shape and relative magnitude (Li3m6eEWECA)
- Price levels are approximately 10x higher this cycle: 2018 first low ~$6K vs. ~$60K now; 2018 higher low ~$6,400 vs. ~$65K now — the fractal holds across an order-of-magnitude scale difference (Li3m6eEWECA)
- The 200-day moving average functions as a key resistance/reference level at the peak of this recurring bounce structure, consistent across cycles (Li3m6eEWECA)
- No directional resolution is offered — Cowen explicitly leaves the 2018 vs. 2019 outcome question open, reflecting genuine uncertainty rather than a suppressed bearish or bullish lean (Li3m6eEWECA)
