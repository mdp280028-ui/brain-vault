# Evidence log: Gold vs Crypto

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-13 (9a-LA7tm8Mo): Using historical presidential-cycle data (1953–present for gold, 2013–present for BTC), Cowen demonstrates that gold and Bitcoin have opposite political-regime profiles: gold peaks under Republican sweeps while Bitcoin's median return is negative under any sweep configuration. He holds gold and expects continued strength: "Gold does the best under Republican sweep, which is exactly where we are right now." [25:03] Bitcoin's midterm-year historical average of −65% further widens the near-term performance gap between the two assets, weakening the "digital gold" equivalence thesis on a regime-adjusted basis.

- 2026-05-17 (WHK1S6MrlCA): Cowen quantifies the BTC/Gold divergence with a specific ratio target, stating "Bitcoin drops another 45% against gold. Seems like a likely outcome to me." [21:30] The BTC/Gold ratio chart shows a well-defined high and an emerging low, reinforcing the structural case that these assets are moving in opposite directions — not correlated stores of value. Year-to-date 2026 cross-asset data is cited directly: gold, SPX, international/EM equities, and silver are all outperforming Bitcoin, consistent with Cowen's prior midterm-year framework.

- 2026-05-21 (8PQee-onXrU): In a three-host panel, Cowen reinforced his gold-over-Bitcoin stance with an explicit midterm-year seasonal argument: gold tends to bottom around summer and rally through H2 while Bitcoin sells off, and Bitcoin would need to fall roughly 40% against gold just to revisit its 2022 relative level. Cowen is positioned in cash and gold over Bitcoin through the expected Q4 2026 cycle low, with the outlook table showing Gold ↑3/↑2 vs. BTC ↓3/↓2/↓2 across year/months/weeks timeframes. His framing was direct: "a lot of times in midterm years, gold bottoms around the summer and then starts heading back up. So, imagine if gold goes up in the second half of the year and Bitcoin goes down." [27:25]

- 2026-06-04 (QMn7cm4nfYU): With Bitcoin live at the 200-week exponential MA (~$61.8K), Cowen notes that BTC was rejected at bare market resistance against gold and states that Bitcoin will likely drop further against the metal through the rest of 2026. He gives a specific magnitude — "Bitcoin will likely drop about another 30% against gold this year" [32:12] — through a combination of BTC falling and gold recovering after finding a low within the next 1–2 months. The outlook table rates Gold ↑2 (months) while BTC is rated ↓4 (months) and ↓3 (year), sustaining the structural preference for gold that has been consistent across prior briefs.

- 2026-06-13 (DoteyQFNfnE): Cowen explicitly frames gold's ~28% correction from its early-2026 peak as a healthy midterm-year pause within an ongoing bull market, not a top, citing comparable drawdowns in 2008, 1974, and 2006 that all resolved higher. Midterm-year seasonality places the average gold bottom on day 187 (currently day 162 as of June 13), pointing to a late June/early July low with the full window extending through October — after which Cowen expects gold to recover and ultimately reach new all-time highs in 2027–2028, further widening its advantage over Bitcoin across that timeframe. As he put it at [08:19]: "for me, I remain still bullish on gold. And having a correction in the first half of 2026 is what we said would likely happen."

- 2026-07-13 (sjt1LV2iDog): Cowen refines the seasonal-low case for gold, noting 2026's YTD drawdown (~7-8%) is milder than the 2018/2022 midterm-year analogs (10-11%) and that a recent death cross has historically preceded short-term rallies; he pinpoints gold's bull market support band (~$3,800-3,841, untested since 2023) as the level to watch for a bounce, with the low likely in by the November ITC conference. He also stresses gold's much faster historical recovery from recession-driven drawdowns versus equities: "back then, gold did drop about 26%. If you compare that to what it's dropped right now, it's about 28%." [10:57]
