## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin had a rough week but managed to close back above the 200-week EMA after sweeping the February low — Cowen treats this as a mild positive for bulls, showing they haven't given up on that level.
2. The setup closely mirrors 2018: February low, May rally to bear market resistance band (slightly exceeded), then a June low sweep. In 2018, that pattern led to a rally into late July / early August.
3. Cowen's base case is that June forms a meaningful low, but he repeatedly emphasizes this is "dubious speculation" — it is too early in June to confirm, and the mid-June timing of the 2018 low suggests the current early-June low could be retested.
4. Even if the June low is in, Cowen expects summer volatility to dry up significantly — counter-trend rallies back toward the bear market resistance band are possible but no major move is expected.
5. The final bear market low is still expected in Q4, consistent with his prior stage-three framework. A drop later in the year remains the base case regardless of whether a June low holds.
6. Historical pattern across 2018, 2022, and the current cycle: down May/June, then green July. Cowen's "dubious" call is that July 2026 will likely be a green month.
7. Year-to-date ROI data for 2018 and 2022 show that mid-June drops took Bitcoin roughly 55–60% down from the yearly open — he uses this as a data anchor for where current weakness fits historically.
8. DCA strategy advice: Cowen says ignoring Bitcoin in the first half of the midterm year and starting to DCA in the second half (post-June low) has historically worked, even though Q4 drops in 2018 and 2022 temporarily hurt those positions before they recovered.
9. The key near-term signal to watch: whether Bitcoin can hold the 200-week EMA for a second consecutive weekly close. If it does, odds of a counter-trend rally into July increase meaningfully.
10. Cowen acknowledges the June low price level as approximately $59K and frames the open question as whether that holds or whether a slightly lower low prints later in June.

## Predictions made
- Bitcoin likely forms a low in June 2026 (possibly already at ~$59K), with the final bear market low expected in Q4 2026
- July 2026 is likely a green month for Bitcoin, based on the 2018/2022 pattern of down May–June followed by an up July
- A counter-trend rally back toward the bear market resistance band is possible in late July / early August if the 200-week EMA holds
- Summer volatility expected to dry up significantly even if a counter-trend rally occurs
