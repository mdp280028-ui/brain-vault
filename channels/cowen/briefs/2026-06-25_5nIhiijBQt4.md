## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Guy (Coin Bureau) identifies the current bull market as memory and chips — specifically Micron and SanDisk — which are downstream beneficiaries of hyperscaler AI spending. Nvidia and Alphabet have pulled back while these names rip.
2. Micron reported fiscal Q3 revenue of $41.5B against a $35.5B estimate; EPS of $25 vs. $20.50 expected. Forward guidance calls for $50B revenue and $31 EPS next quarter — described as "insane."
3. Micron noted semi-autonomous cars use 5x the memory of average vehicles, and humanoid robots could use ~10x the memory of those cars — supporting a long-term structural demand thesis for memory even beyond AI data centers.
4. Ben's near-term equity view: a shallow 5–10% correction in stocks in June is largely done; a rally back into August/September is the base case before a larger drop at the end of the midterm year.
5. Ben's crypto rotation thesis: crypto bull market is probably several months away — likely returning in Q4 2026 when a stock market correction pushes capital back into Bitcoin. "The time to sit out was eight months ago."
6. Both agree the four-year cycle remains the single most reliable Bitcoin indicator — buy at end of midterm year, sell in Q4 of the post-halving year. Neither has seen it fail and neither sees strong reason it would break now.
7. Cowen references Bitcoin's 200-week MA (~$62K) as a current support marker; a deeper flush could reach the 250-week MA (~$57K) or the 350-week MA (~$47K). He frames any move to those levels as a buying opportunity, not a crisis.
8. M2 money supply is trending up and to the right in 2026 — Cowen presents this as a supportive macro backdrop. By contrast, 2022's bear market coincided with M2 declining.
9. Macro headwinds noted: a potential Fed rate hike at the July 29 FOMC (60/40 or 65/35 odds cited), US-Iran geopolitical tension, and real disposable income turning negative for the first time in ~2.5 years.
10. Both panelists agree on the anti-cash thesis: sitting 100% in cash is a losing strategy because inflation erodes purchasing power continuously. The right move is to hold quality assets with a long time horizon and keep some cash for opportunistic deployment.

## Predictions made
