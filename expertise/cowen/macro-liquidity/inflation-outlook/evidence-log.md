# Evidence log: Inflation Outlook

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-15 (FgxAe_NAh5c): With Powell's departure, incoming Fed Chair Kevin Warsh faces resurging CPI (trending back up year-over-year) and a sharply spiking PPI (approximately 6% month-over-month), conditions that make rate cuts practically impossible regardless of political pressure. Oil prices add a further inflationary tail risk — the 21-week EMA has already supported prices once and a second surge later in 2026 remains a material risk. As Cowen puts it: [14:24] "The primary way that I see at this moment is he comes in and there's pressure to cut, but he's looking at the energy market and he's like, 'How the hell do I cut?'" Rates are expected to hold through summer 2026, with meaningful risk of hikes beginning in 2027.
