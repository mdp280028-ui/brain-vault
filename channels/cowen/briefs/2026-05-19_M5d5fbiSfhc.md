## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin rallied to the 200-day SMA and was rejected — consistent with bear market behavior in May 2018 and 2022, when that same level capped counter-trend rallies before further declines.
2. Cowen's base case: BTC weakness persists into Q3 and at least into early Q4 2026, with the most likely cycle low in October 2026 — which he frames as the *optimistic* scenario, not the bear case.
3. Two forward paths outlined from the current rejection: (a) Bearish — drops into May/June, sweeps the February low, countertrend rally in July–August, then new low in October; (b) Less bearish — one more attempted rally to the 0.382 Fibonacci level (mid-80s) before the final Q4 sell-off.
4. The 0.382 Fibonacci retracement has been tagged before the final flush in every prior mid-term bear cycle (2014, 2018, 2022), but BTC has not tagged it yet this cycle — leaving open the possibility of one more push higher before the final leg down.
5. After rejection from the 200-day SMA, BTC historically retreats to the 20-week SMA (~75k currently), which provides temporary support before the trend resumes lower.
6. Mid-term year seasonal pattern: weakness tends to cluster in February, April, June, and October, with the cycle trough typically landing in Q4 approximately one year after the cycle top (Nov 2021 top → Nov 2022 low; Dec 2017 top → Dec 2018 low).
7. The pessimistic scenario: if the broader business cycle ends in late 2026, Bitcoin's expected bull market could be choked off by a stock market bear market — the same mechanism by which altcoin season was cut short in Q4 2025 when Bitcoin entered a bear market before euphoria could build.
8. Extended altcoin analogy: Cowen draws a direct parallel between his years-long bearish alt/BTC call (which played out) and his current BTC bear view — he argues that calling for alt/BTC pairs to merely reach range lows was always the *optimistic* case, because the more rational read was a structural downtrend with lower highs and lower lows. He applies this same framing to BTC now.
9. According to the average of all prior mid-term years with one standard deviation overlay, BTC should currently be ~40% below the yearly open — roughly 51–52k — meaning the current price is significantly elevated relative to historical mid-term norms.
10. Stablecoin dominance has dipped just below the 21-week EMA and is now rallying back up — Cowen flags this as a bearish secondary signal consistent with prior bear market behavior, where stablecoin dominance bottoms near that EMA before trending higher.
