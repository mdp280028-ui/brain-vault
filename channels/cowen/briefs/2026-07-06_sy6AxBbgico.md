## TL;DR
Cowen argues 2026's BTC price action is closely tracking the 2018 four-year cycle pattern (just less volatile and 10x the price level), and expects a likely detour where Bitcoin breaks below this year's local low and forms its cycle bottom in October, possibly as late as December.

## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen argues 2026's Bitcoin price action is a "less volatile version" of 2018: both years show a February low, a higher low in late March/early April, a lower high at the 200-day moving average in May, and a sweep of the February low in June.
2. The June 2026 low was $57,000, which he notes is almost exactly 10x the 2018 June low of $5,700 — reinforcing his four-year cycle comparison.
3. He expects Bitcoin to follow the 2018 script further: a countertrend rally starting in early July, a brief pullback around mid-July (possibly tied to an inflation report), then a larger move up toward the 200-day moving average in late July/early August, before giving it back in August/September.
4. He expects this pattern to eventually "take a detour" from 2018, with Bitcoin breaking below the current pattern before November, and thinks October is a strong candidate month for the cycle low (versus December in 2018), though December remains possible given the 2014 cycle's bear market ran longer than a year.
5. Normal strategy recommendation: ignore Bitcoin in the first half of midterm years, then DCA in the second half; he says finding a new low on July 1st validated that approach.
6. He contrasts 2026 with 2018 in terms of market psychology: 2018 topped in euphoria (~$20k top, rotation into altcoins), while 2026 topped in apathy (~$126k top, no altcoin rotation) — which he says is why this cycle "feels worse" even at higher absolute prices.
7. He critiques macro-data-driven Bitcoin forecasting (e.g., ISM, money supply correlations), citing that in 2014 Bitcoin fell while ISM rose, and in 2015 Bitcoin rose while ISM fell — arguing these relationships aren't reliable for Bitcoin.
8. He frames the year through "windows of strength and weakness": mid-May to end of June was a window of weakness, July is typically a window of strength (with possible pullback to a higher low), followed by give-back in August/September and a potential final low in October.
9. He reiterates that the four-year cycle isn't inherently bearish — it just describes a recurring cadence of when lows tend to occur, typically in the second half of midterm years.
10. He states that if the four-year cycle eventually breaks, he will change his strategy, but until then he continues to defer to it.

## Predictions made
- Bitcoin likely to rally toward the 200-day moving average in late July/early August 2026, mirroring the 2018 pattern.
- Bitcoin expected to give back gains in August/September 2026, then break below the current pattern before November.
- Cycle low most likely to form in October 2026 (candidate month), with December 2026 as a possible later alternative.
