## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Structurally, Cowen says 2026's Bitcoin bear market is a "less volatile version" of the 2018 bear market: both had a February low, a higher low in late March/early April, a lower high in May, and a sweep of the February low in late June/early July.
2. From a liquidity/business-cycle perspective, however, he says this period feels more like the 2019-2020 "digestion phase" after the 2019 apathetic top — both his four-year-cycle view and his business-cycle view, he argues, can be true simultaneously ("why not both?").
3. He expects the year-to-date ROI fractal matching 2018 to eventually break, but not in the way bulls who "faded" the four-year cycle for the past 8 months expected — he still expects a rally into a brief mid-July bounce/CPI-related higher low, then most gains given back by late July/early August, similar to both 2018 and 2022 seasonal patterns.
4. He warns specifically about altcoins (not Bitcoin): in 2018, Bitcoin held ~$6K support into November while altcoins (TOTAL3/BTC) bled out hard from August through October — he expects a similar altcoin underperformance this cycle even while BTC holds up.
5. His base case for the market cycle bottom timing is late September/early October 2026, based on measuring ROI from the prior low to the next low; he does not think the bear market needs to extend to December like the last two cycles (2018, 2022) did.
6. He notes one historical exception (2013-2014) where the bear market ran ~13-14 months into January; if this cycle mirrors that, Bitcoin could bottom as late as December, but he calls that his non-base case ("getting a little greedy").
7. Near-term technical setup: Bitcoin is being squeezed between the 200-week moving average and the bear market resistance band; he expects a possible retest of the resistance band before resolving to the downside, which he says is needed to "fully reset all the onchain indicators" ahead of the actual market cycle bottom.
8. He expects a stock market correction to begin in August or September, which he ties to triggering Bitcoin's final drop to the market cycle bottom, rather than Bitcoin drifting sideways until November as it did in the 2018 analog.
9. Cowen distinguishes "being right" from "making money" — he says he doesn't wait for a confirmed bottom to buy (he started buying in summer in both 2018 and 2022) and frames this analysis as academic/probabilistic rather than a timing instruction.
10. Reminder: ITC (Into The Cryptoverse) conference is November 20-22, 2026 in Miami; Cowen designed the timing hoping it falls after the market cycle bottom, though he notes it might land close to, rather than fully after, the low.

## Predictions made
- Bitcoin market cycle bottom most likely around late September/early October 2026, based on ROI-from-low measurement.
- Possible tail scenario: Bitcoin bottoms as late as December 2026 if this bear market extends to 13-14 months like 2013-2014 (explicitly stated as not his base case).
- Bitcoin likely to see a brief bounce/higher low around mid-July (near the CPI report), then give most gains back by late July/early August.
- Stock market (S&P) correction expected to start in August or September 2026, which would trigger Bitcoin's drop to its market cycle bottom.
- Altcoins (TOTAL3 relative to BTC) expected to continue underperforming/bleeding even while Bitcoin holds up, similar to August-October 2018.
