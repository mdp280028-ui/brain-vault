# Bull Market Support Band

A combined indicator (20-week SMA + 21-week EMA) Cowen treats as the bull/bear regime line for Bitcoin. Sustained closes below it signal bear-market continuation; reclaims signal regime shifts. Also applied to SPX and select altcoins.

## Current Thesis

The bull market support band concept applies across assets but with opposite roles: for Bitcoin (weekly timeframe), the 20-week SMA / 21-week EMA band remains bear-market resistance following May 2026's rejection, and any rally back to it is treated as a low-conviction counter-trend move within the ongoing bear. For gold (monthly timeframe, 20-month MA / 21-month EMA), the same band structure plays its intended bull-market support role — gold briefly dipped below it in 2022 but is expected to find support there again during the current midterm-year correction, consistent with how the band behaved throughout the 1970s and 2000s gold bull markets. The timeframe difference is critical: the band signals bear continuation for BTC on the weekly chart while simultaneously signaling bull-market support for gold on the monthly chart.

## Key Evidence

- Bitcoin rejected at the 20-week SMA / 21-week EMA band in the current midterm year, matching the pattern from 2014, 2018, 2019, and 2022 midterm bear cycles (dy3E7Jzte88)
- The BTC/SPX ratio was also rejected at the same 20-week SMA / 21-week EMA level, extending the bear-regime signal beyond spot BTC to relative performance (dy3E7Jzte88)
- The recent BTC rally produced minimal follow-through before retesting the band — far less than 2023 — matching the classic midterm fake-out pattern (slTIiS-Y65k)
- For gold, the 20-month MA / 21-month EMA band held as support throughout the 1970s and 2000s bull markets, with only brief exceptions in 1974 and 2008 — both of which preceded continued bull market advances (DoteyQFNfnE)
- Gold dipped below the monthly band briefly in 2022 but recovered; Cowen expects the band to provide support again during the current ~28% midterm-year correction (DoteyQFNfnE)
- A counter-trend BTC rally back toward the weekly band remains conditionally possible if the 200-week EMA holds, but Cowen frames it as a low-conviction within-bear move, not a regime shift (SG3tuA8zqs8)
