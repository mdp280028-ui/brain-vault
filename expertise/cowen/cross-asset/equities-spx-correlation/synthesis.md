# Equities & SPX Correlation

SPX (S&P 500) price action, structural corrections, and its correlation with Bitcoin. Cowen treats SPX as the dominant macro asset crypto correlates with during risk-off events, and the four-year cycle as shared across both; SPX cycle-tops and major corrections often coincide with crypto-cycle inflections.

## Current Thesis

SPX is expected to suffer another significant drop later in 2026, consistent with the 2018 midterm template and the S&P/M2 fractal, which continues to point to a September 2026 top followed by a deep secondary correction in late Q3/early Q4. The four-year cycle, resurging inflation foreclosing any Fed pivot, and the 2018 analogue all sustain the structural case, with the second-half SPX decline framed as the macro catalyst for Bitcoin's decisive Q4 leg down. A new pessimistic overlay introduced in May 2026 goes further: if the broader business cycle ends in late 2026, an SPX bear market could choke off Bitcoin's bull entirely rather than merely triggering a corrective leg — the same mechanism by which altcoin season was cut short in Q4 2025 when Bitcoin entered its own bear market before euphoria could build. Cowen's yearly SPX conviction softens marginally to ↓ 2, but the directional call is unchanged.

## Key Evidence

- SPX has bottomed approximately every four years since at least 1958 (1958, 1962, 1966, 1970 … 2014, 2018, 2022), establishing the four-year cycle as a macro-structural feature independent of any crypto-specific driver. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- The 2018 analogue is explicit: SPX topped in September 2018, crashed into December, and Bitcoin simultaneously collapsed to ~$3,100 — Cowen maps the 2026 SPX double-correction onto this same structure. (FgxAe_NAh5c, APCjkL5gfFI)
- The S&P/M2 fractal is still tracking the prior-cycle pattern and points to an SPX top around September 2026; it remains an active tool in Cowen's framework as of May 2026. (APCjkL5gfFI, M5d5fbiSfhc)
- CPI is trending back up YoY and PPI month-over-month is spiking (~6%), removing the Fed's room to cut and sustaining the macro headwind for risk assets. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- BTC found support at $60K in 2026, mirroring $6K support in both 2018 and 2019; in 2018 the eventual breakdown was triggered by a second-half SPX correction — Cowen cites this as the more likely 2026 analog over the pandemic path. (WHK1S6MrlCA)
- Cowen introduces a pessimistic scenario in which the broader business cycle ends in late 2026, producing an SPX bear market that extinguishes Bitcoin's bull cycle entirely rather than merely triggering a corrective leg — mirroring how BTC's own bear market ended altcoin season in Q4 2025. (M5d5fbiSfhc)
