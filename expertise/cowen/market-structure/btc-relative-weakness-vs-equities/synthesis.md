## Current Thesis

BTC is structurally further down the risk curve than SPX — underparticipating on equity rallies and absorbing harder hits on declines — a dynamic Cowen explains through two reinforcing mechanisms: the mechanical midterm-year bleed pattern (through June, counter-trend bounce July–August, capitulation low in Q4) and the monetary-policy sensitivity gap between crypto and equities. The June 19 social-risk video sharpens the macro narrative: the AI trade is keeping equities at new highs, which is actively suppressing any Fed pivot, meaning the very force lifting SPX is simultaneously blocking the looser-policy catalyst that BTC needs to recover — as Cowen states, "we're more worried about rate hikes than rate cuts at this point." Retail never returned in this cycle (an "apathetic top" mirroring 2019, not the euphoric 2021 top), and without a monetary catalyst, crypto remains stuck while SPX grinds higher on AI-driven earnings. Outperformance versus equities is not expected to resume until equity weakness in late Q3/early Q4 2026 forces a Fed pivot, with the cycle low still projected for Q4 2026 and BTC outperformance not resuming until 2027 or later.
