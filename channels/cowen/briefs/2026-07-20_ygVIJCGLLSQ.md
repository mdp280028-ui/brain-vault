## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Cowen frames the core question for ETH as whether the current cycle resembles 2022 or 2018.
2. In 2018, Bitcoin rallied in July and then gave back the gains.
3. In 2022, Bitcoin also rallied in July and gave back the gains, but the reversal came in August or September.
4. In both 2018 and 2022, Bitcoin's price behavior after the July rally was essentially the same.
5. Ethereum behaved very differently from Bitcoin across the two cycles when overlaid on the chart.
6. In 2018, Ethereum failed to hold support and dropped to a new cycle low in August, and again later at the end of the cycle.
7. In 2022, when Bitcoin dropped, Ethereum instead printed a higher low rather than breaking to new lows.
