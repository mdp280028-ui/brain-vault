# Bitcoin Risk Metric

Cowen's proprietary 0-to-1 scaled composite indicator used to identify zones of long-term accumulation (low risk) and distribution (high risk). Buy zones cluster below 0.3 and distribution zones above 0.7; the metric is central to his long-horizon allocation framework.

## Current Thesis

The composite on-chain risk metric — built from ~9 normalized indicators (MVRV Z-score, Puell Multiple, RHODL ratio, supply in profit/loss, thermocap ratios, terminal price, transaction fees) — currently reads ~0.18–0.198, placing Bitcoin in a historically rare sub-0.2 accumulation zone. Cowen argues this reading confirms Bitcoin is in the third and final stage of the bear market, with the cycle bottom most likely in October 2026. The metric's signal is deliberately insulated from news, macro, and geopolitics — its value lies entirely in on-chain behavior, making it Cowen's primary tool for long-horizon allocation decisions.

## Key Evidence

- Current composite reading of ~0.18–0.198 is below the 0.2 threshold, a level Bitcoin has historically spent very little time at, marking it as a rare accumulation zone (Di8YR9nX8Q8).
- Sub-0.2 readings cluster most frequently during midterm years in the 4-year cycle; 2026 is a midterm year, consistent with the pattern (Di8YR9nX8Q8).
- In every prior bear market, the composite risk metric carved out a multi-month low before recovering — Cowen expects the same structure this cycle (Di8YR9nX8Q8).
- The supply-in-profit vs. supply-in-loss crossover has occurred in the current cycle; historically this event precedes a bottom by 1–4 months, corroborating the metric's accumulation signal (Di8YR9nX8Q8).
- The metric aggregates ~9 individual on-chain indicators, each normalized 0–1, giving it breadth that single-indicator signals lack (Di8YR9nX8Q8).
