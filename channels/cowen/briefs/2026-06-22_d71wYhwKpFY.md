## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin is currently below the bear market resistance band, which sits at roughly $70,000–$74,000. In bear markets, BTC repeatedly tests this band but struggles to sustain above it; the only exception was briefly in 2014.
2. BTC is now caught between two converging levels: the bear market resistance band trending down from above, and the 200-week moving average trending up from below. It must eventually break one way or the other.
3. Cowen acknowledges the four-year cycle history means a breakdown — like those seen at the end of 2014, 2018, and 2022 — is a real possibility that cannot be dismissed, even if it isn't certain.
4. The realized price is flagged as an unmet bear market condition: Bitcoin has not yet traded below its realized price, which historically happens in bear markets. This makes a further leg lower seem likely at some point in 2026.
5. The historical seasonal pattern shows Bitcoin tending to form a low in early summer (June), with weakness persisting into early July, followed by a countertrend rally in late July/August — a pattern seen in both 2018 and 2022.
6. Cowen reiterates his earlier guidance from late 2025: treat the first half of 2026 as noise, assume countertrend rallies get faded, and only start watching more seriously in the second half. H2 2026 begins in about a week from the video date.
7. Some on-chain indicators have reset but not all, leaving the setup incomplete for a confirmed bottom.
8. Altcoins face a specific risk: in July 2018, the altcoin market was "annihilated" while Bitcoin moved sideways. Declining social interest and fewer retail participants buying alts make a repeat of that dynamic plausible.
9. If Bitcoin can hold the 200-week MA here (as it has historically found support there), it would raise the odds of a brief countertrend rally into July, similar to the 2022 analog.
10. Bottom line: Cowen's base case is that BTC will eventually break lower, but countertrend rallies remain possible before that happens, making the near-term path difficult to time precisely.

## Predictions made
- BTC likely trades below its realized price at some point later in 2026
- A countertrend rally in late July is possible if the 200-week MA holds, consistent with 2022 and 2018 analogs
- Altcoins may underperform or get "annihilated" relative to BTC, particularly in July, if the 2018 pattern repeats
