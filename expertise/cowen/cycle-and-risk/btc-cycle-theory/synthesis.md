# BTC Cycle Theory

Cowen's framework that Bitcoin and broader risk assets follow an approximately four-year cycle of bull-top, bear-bottom, and accumulation phases. He frames the cycle as macro-driven rather than halving-driven, citing parallel equity-market cycle precedents going back to the 1950s.

## Current Thesis

BTC remains in the late stage of the 2026 bear market, with Cowen saying there's an "increasing probability" the early-summer ~$57K low is the cycle low, reinforced by the near-exact 2018 analog ($5,743 low, a 10x scaled match) sharing the same February-low → higher-low → 200-day-MA-rejection → lower-low structure. His base case is still a stock-market-driven correction ahead: a relief bounce followed by one more rejection at the 200-day MA around late July/early August (echoing 2018), then renewed weakness into a final bottom roughly 100 days (~3 months) from the recent low — landing around late September–October 2026, possibly slipping into Q4 but "not much later." He continues to frame this same path in fair-value-trendline terms: Bitcoin and the broader crypto asset class will spend more of this year below the fair value log regression trendline, trying to find support at that line in the coming months, with the entire 2023-2025 move recast as analogous to 2019 (rising BTC dominance, three rate cuts, QT ending, no altcoin rotation) — meaning no durable overvalued move was ever justified. He expects the rest of 2026 to be "somewhat sluggish," forming a low later in the year after a summer turnaround, with a trend back upward only starting in 2027-2028; he continues to treat the four-year cycle as undefeated and more reliable than macro narratives, and his strategy remains unchanged: DCA through the second half of midterm years rather than trying to time the exact bottom.

## Key Evidence

- The July 1, 2026 low of $57.7K is almost identical to the 2018 cycle's $5,743 low (a 10x scaled match), with both cycles sharing a February low → higher low (late March/early April) → 200-day MA rejection (May) → lower low (late June/early July) structure. (8TmOvGK235I, oj2n8nkTTTE)
- Using ROI-measured-from-low-to-low, BTC is on cycle day 1,331, with the last two cycles bottoming roughly 100 days (~3 months) from a comparable point — implying a bottom around late September/October, "not much later" into Q4. (oj2n8nkTTTE)
- The crypto asset class remains below its fair value logarithmic regression trendline, with 2023-2025 framed as analogous to 2019 (rising BTC dominance, rate cuts, QT ending, no altcoin rotation); total crypto market cap (~$2.125T) is now slightly below its 2021 level, underscoring how far the asset class sits from prior highs. (uoF9yzXHmt4, BoSW-PLx1Ms)
- Every prior bear market bottom (2014, 2018, 2022) featured a massive volume capitulation spike that reset all on-chain metrics; that spike has not occurred yet, which remains Cowen's primary reason for rejecting any current bottom call. (psz9FwqWGns, g4FiHz14W14, 1LI9wfqxOmc, 8TmOvGK235I, prior)
- The MVRV Z-Score (0.251, approaching zero) and realized price (~$53K, not yet breached) remain unsatisfied bottom conditions; every prior cycle saw MVRV cross below zero and BTC dip below the realized price before the bull market began. (hXlR5xu0HK4, g4FiHz14W14, prior)
- Cowen's distilled rule — buy BTC at the end of midterm years, sell at the end of post-halving years — remains undefeated across three cycles; his stated strategy is to DCA through the second half of midterm years rather than time the exact bottom or trade countertrend rallies. (psz9FwqWGns, vTcABO_0Exg, hXlR5xu0HK4, 5nIhiijBQt4, g4FiHz14W14, OYcGIifaO4Q, 1LI9wfqxOmc, 8TmOvGK235I, oj2n8nkTTTE)
