## Current Thesis

Cowen holds that every Bitcoin bear market in history has ended with a massive volume spike — a visible "wall" of volume on the weekly chart — and the absence of such a spike is his primary reason for not calling a bottom now, even after Bitcoin's first weekly close below the 200-week moving average this cycle. He distinguishes two capitulation paths: time-based (the cycle grinds out gradually to a late Q3/early Q4 2026 low) versus price-based (a sudden, extreme volume spike that resets all on-chain metrics and would cause him to pivot immediately). Separately, a touch of the balance price (~$38K) remains his definitive price-based signal, as it would simultaneously reset all on-chain indicators. His default assumption is time-based capitulation, but he explicitly warns against marrying any single prediction — if price-based capitulation occurs and on-chain metrics fully reset, he would remove the expectation of a lower low.

- Cowen explicitly frames the two capitulation types: time-based (gradual multi-month cycle resolution) vs. price-based (sudden massive volume spike resetting all on-chain metrics); his default is time-based (1LI9wfqxOmc).
- The March 2020 pandemic crash is the clearest modern example of price-based capitulation — surging volume that reset all on-chain indicators and allowed Cowen to pivot away from a longer bear market timeline early (psz9FwqWGns).
- The absence of a volume capitulation spike is cited alongside the incomplete supply in profit/loss crossover timeline as dual confirmation that the bottom is not yet in (psz9FwqWGns).
