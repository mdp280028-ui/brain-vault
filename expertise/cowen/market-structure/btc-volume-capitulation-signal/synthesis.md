## Current Thesis

Cowen holds that every Bitcoin bear market in history has ended with a massive volume spike — a visible "wall" of volume on the weekly chart — and the absence of such a spike is one of his primary reasons for believing the current cycle low is not yet in. This volume capitulation signal is a necessary but not sufficient condition for calling a bottom; it must coincide with on-chain resets to confirm the low. Separately, Cowen identifies a touch of the balance price (~$38K) as the definitive price-based capitulation signal: historically Bitcoin spends only days below the balance price, and reaching it would simultaneously reset all on-chain indicators, removing ambiguity about whether the bear market has ended. Until either the volume spike or the balance price touch occurs, Cowen defers to his time-based framework and an October 2026 base-case low.
