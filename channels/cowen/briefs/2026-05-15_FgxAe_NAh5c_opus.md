## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Powell officially steps down as Fed Chair and Kevin Warsh takes over; Cowen compares the celebration to Gensler's departure, where Bitcoin actually corrected before going higher.
2. Warsh faces structural pressure to cut rates, but PPI is spiking (cited ~6% print) and headline CPI is moving back up, making cuts very difficult — Cowen "does not envy" the job.
3. Oil looks structurally higher for the rest of the year; price found support at the 21-week EMA and longer-term moving averages are catching up, risking another surge later in the year.
4. The four-year cycle is not Bitcoin-specific — the S&P 500 has bottomed approximately every four years going back to the 1950s (1958, 62, 66, 70, 74, 78, 82), so it isn't caused by the halving.
5. Cycle disruptions tend to come from recessions (2020) or prior extended bear markets (2002→2006 skip); March and October are the typical bear-market low windows.
6. Cowen expects a second SPX correction in Q3 or Q4 2026 (one already happened in Q1), mirroring the 2018 setup where the second equity drop dragged Bitcoin down hard.
7. Bitcoin underperformance is "fair" because BTC ran 8x (700%) from late 2022 to October 2025 while SPX roughly doubled; bear-market pain is the symmetric payback.
8. Primary scenario: counter-trend BTC rally, then secondary SPX correction takes BTC lower into a Q4 2026 low; people will declare the four-year cycle dead just before the next bull run.
9. Rate hikes are a real risk in 2027 — Cowen frames it as a possible repeat of the post-COVID sequence (cut → reignite animal spirits → hike → something breaks → cut again).
10. Powell shares blame for inflation (Fed should have hiked in 2021), and with inflation not at the 2% target for 5 years, the attempted soft landing may yet become a hard landing within the next couple of years, with a business-cycle bottom no later than early 2029.

## Predictions made
- Bitcoin likely to make a lower low in Q4 2026 as part of the four-year cycle.
- A second major SPX correction in Q3 or Q4 2026 (after the Q1 one already booked).
- Oil structurally higher for the rest of 2026 with risk of another surge later in the year.
- Rates likely to hold steady through summer 2026, with risk of rate hikes starting in 2027.
- BTC counter-trend rally could reach the 0.382 retrace in the mid-80Ks; sustained hold above $100K for a few weeks would invalidate his bearish view.
- Business cycle bottom by early 2029 at the absolute latest (top occurs before then).
