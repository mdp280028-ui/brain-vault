# Evidence log: Btc Relative Weakness Vs Equities

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-08 (APCjkL5gfFI): With SPX and NASDAQ at all-time highs, Cowen highlights that BTC has merely "gone up" without approaching its own prior peak — a configuration he reads as bearish rather than as a lagging opportunity. He states directly: [05:22] "Bitcoin has gone up, but it's still nowhere near all-time highs. And that's not a declaration that you're about to have all-time highs. It's more so a declaration that Bitcoin is weak relative to the assets that are putting in all-time highs." This relative weakness is the setup for BTC to follow the anticipated late-Q3/early-Q4 2026 SPX secondary correction lower and print a lower low.

- 2026-05-25 (dy3E7Jzte88): Cowen points to the BTC/SPX ratio being rejected at the 20-week SMA / 21-week EMA bear market resistance band as direct confirmation of relative weakness — a pattern that has repeated in every midterm bear year (2014, 2018, 2019, 2022). He explains the divergence structurally: SPX is being lifted by AI-sector earnings while crypto has no equivalent catalyst, leaving BTC entirely at the mercy of monetary policy and liquidity conditions. With the market now pricing rate hikes rather than cuts, he sees the underperformance as likely to persist for months. [05:44] "If you look at midterm years, Bitcoin's valuation against the S&P frequently gets rejected at that 20we SMA, 21week EMA, and that's exactly where it just got rejected at."

- 2026-05-27 (Ibh7wDgNGCE): Despite SPX making repeated new all-time highs, BTC has continued to lag — Cowen uses this to declare the supercycle thesis dead on its own terms: "despite the fact that the S&P keeps putting in new all-time highs, Bitcoin keeps lagging" [05:00]. He frames BTC as further down the risk curve than SPX, citing the 2018 and 2022 analogs where the second SPX leg down was not dramatically lower than the first but BTC went substantially lower, and applies this to his two-drop SPX framework: BTC will underperform on both the June-July and September drops, with a possible cycle low in October 2026.

- 2026-05-28 (OuHBTQ6fkfQ): Cowen points out that BTC has had ample time to reach new all-time highs if the supercycle thesis were valid, but has continued to lag even as the S&P 500 put in seven to eight consecutive weekly gains — making the supercycle case increasingly untenable. He argues that when the overdue SPX correction arrives, BTC will be hit disproportionately harder precisely because it has already been the weaker asset on the way up, illustrating this with the altcoin dynamic: "When Bitcoin would go up, it would lift the altcoin market with it, but the minute Bitcoin started dropping, altcoins would get annihilated." [00:37] BTC now occupies that same subordinate position relative to equities.

- 2026-05-28 (Tj7mvTHDe1U): Ben explicitly frames BTC's underperformance versus equities as a mechanical midterm-year pattern compounded by monetary policy: crypto is more rate-sensitive than stocks, and with rate cuts fully priced out and hikes now being priced in, there is no tailwind. At [08:18] Ben states verbatim: "I think Bitcoin's going to keep bleeding against both the stock market and gold and energy and manufacturing. This is what it does in midterm years." The panel consensus is that BTC continues underperforming through June, with a possible counter-trend bounce in July–August before a final Q4 capitulation low — a view consistent with Cowen's prior structural-weakness framing.
