# Diminishing Returns Thesis

Cowen's thesis that each successive Bitcoin bull cycle delivers a lower percentage return than the prior cycle. Used to set realistic cycle-top targets and ground long-horizon expectations.

## Current Thesis

Cowen holds that the current Bitcoin cycle is underdelivering relative to prior cycles and relative to competing asset classes, consistent with a diminishing-returns pattern. BTC's year-to-date performance has trailed the S&P 500, Nasdaq, gold, and energy, and its nominal recovery has only brought it back to levels from a couple of months ago — no net new gains. Cowen does not expect the current cycle to close that performance gap; meaningful recovery is deferred explicitly to the next cycle.

## Key Evidence

- BTC is underperforming every major asset class (S&P 500, Nasdaq, gold, energy) on a YTD basis as of May 2026, widening the relative gap versus traditional markets (PnveYgxtd1w).
- BTC's recent price recovery has only returned it to levels from ~February 2026, producing zero net new gains for the year — a hallmark of a cycle with weakening momentum (PnveYgxtd1w).
- Cowen explicitly does not expect a catch-up trade within the current cycle, framing any recovery as a next-cycle event: "perhaps in the next cycle we'll see it go back up" (PnveYgxtd1w).
- The "undervalued" framing Cowen uses is comparative, not bullish — BTC is cheap relative to peers but that cheapness is expected to persist through end of 2026, suggesting the market will not re-rate it within this cycle (PnveYgxtd1w).
