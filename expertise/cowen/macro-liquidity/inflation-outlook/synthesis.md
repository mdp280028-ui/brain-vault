# Inflation Outlook

CPI, PPI, and forward inflation expectations. Cowen uses inflation trajectory as the primary constraint on Fed cuts and the key signal for whether monetary policy can ease.

## Current Thesis

Inflation has reaccelerated to 3.78% headline CPI — driven by a supply-side energy shock — with core CPI at 2.74% reversing its prior convergence toward 2%, making Fed cuts impossible through summer 2026 and a 2027 hike more likely than a cut. The tightening regime is the settled structural backdrop; Cowen's active attention has shifted to the BoJ as the more actionable near-term vector, with a June 2026 BoJ hike assigned a "good chance" of occurring and treated as the key mid-June catalyst — explicitly more important to watch than the Fed. Oil/energy prices remain the key variable: another leg higher in Q3 would drive additional rate-hike pricing and extend crypto bear headwinds through year-end.

## Key Evidence

- Headline CPI rose to 3.78% YoY, above the 3.6–3.7% consensus, with housing (3.37%→3.63%) and transportation (6.89%) as primary drivers (Rf-gAQJk5-0)
- Core CPI is at 2.74%, up from a February trough of 2.47%, suggesting inflation is reversing rather than converging to the 2% target (Rf-gAQJk5-0)
- PPI month-over-month spiked sharply (~6%), a level that independently forecloses near-term easing (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Markets have fully priced out cuts through 2026 and 2027 and now assign higher probability to a 2027 hike than a cut — a 180° reversal from the multi-cut path priced at the start of 2026 (Rf-gAQJk5-0, mwrgOz852wI, dy3E7Jzte88)
- Oil/energy prices are structurally elevated; Cowen sees Q3 risk of another leg higher, which would drive additional rate-hike pricing and extend crypto bear headwinds through year-end (FgxAe_NAh5c, Rf-gAQJk5-0, dy3E7Jzte88)
- Bank of Japan June 2026 meeting is flagged as the key mid-June catalyst — Cowen assigns a "good chance" of a BoJ hike and frames it as more important to watch than the Fed, reinforced across multiple clips (mwrgOz852wI, dy3E7Jzte88, dIa8HUYDNEY, JAQfs3rxFz4)
