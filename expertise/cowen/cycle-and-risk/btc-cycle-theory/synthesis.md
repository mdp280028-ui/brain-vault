# BTC Cycle Theory

Cowen's framework that Bitcoin and broader risk assets follow an approximately four-year cycle of bull-top, bear-bottom, and accumulation phases. He frames the cycle as macro-driven rather than halving-driven, citing parallel equity-market cycle precedents going back to the 1950s.

## Current Thesis

The June capitulation is now live: BTC touched the 200-week exponential MA (~$61.8K) during the June 4 stream, with Cowen calling the "date with destiny" actively in progress and flagging the week of June 13–18 as the likely local-low window, consistent with the 2018 (June 18) and 2022 (June 13) analogs. The full three-phase bear map remains: phase one (late 2025 top) → phase two (extended counter-trend rally March–May 2026 that convinced bulls "this time is different") → phase three (June capitulation now underway), followed by a July–August counter-trend rally and the final Q4 cycle bottom. October is now pinned with two independent metrics: cycle-day count from prior lows (1,428 days from Nov 21, 2022 = Oct 19, 2026) and MicroStrategy's 98-week bottoming pattern (98 weeks from its current top = week of Oct 5, 2026). The behavioral rule is unchanged: the bottom is time-based, not price-based — start DCA-ing after the June low forms, target end-of-midterm-year accumulation, sell at end of post-halving year.

## Key Evidence

- BTC topped on cycle day 1,162 — within one week of the prior two cycles' tops (days 1,059 and 1,168) — making cycle-day timing the single strongest structural argument that the four-year cycle is intact; the same day-count methodology now pins the cycle bottom at Oct 19, 2026 (1,428 days from Nov 21, 2022). (7byCfkDQxq8, QMn7cm4nfYU)
- Two independent metrics converge on the same October 2026 bottom window: (a) cycle-day average of last two bottoms (days 1,424 and 1,432) lands on Oct 19, 2026; (b) MicroStrategy's 98-week prior-cycle bottoming pattern points to the week of Oct 5, 2026. (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- The 200-week exponential MA is confirmed as the June structural magnet: BTC touched ~$61.8K live during the June 4 stream and dipped to $61.3K; in 2018 the June low swept the February low, and in 2022 BTC fell below this MA in June — the June 13–18 window is identified as the likely low week based on exact prior-cycle dates. (icDF_qFXATM, QMn7cm4nfYU)
- The midterm-year classification formalizes structural bearishness: BTC dropped in H1 of every midterm year (2014, 2018, 2022, 2026), all three prior midterm cycles produced a significant June low, and the current cycle has replicated the 2018 stage sequence exactly (Feb low → late March/April higher low → May lower high → June capitulation). (dIa8HUYDNEY, QMn7cm4nfYU)
- On-chain metrics confirm the cycle low is not yet in: MVRV Z-score still above zero (historically cycle lows occur below it); composite on-chain risk indicator at 0.216 (needs to approach 0.1); running 1-year ROI at 0.589 (needs to drop below ~0.3–0.4). The June low is not the final low unless BTC crashes to ~$40K. (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- Cowen's distilled rule — buy BTC at the end of midterm years, sell at the end of post-halving years — is explicitly stated as the only indicator that ever worked; the bottom is time-based, not price-based. (slTIiS-Y65k, Tj7mvTHDe1U, dIa8HUYDNEY)
