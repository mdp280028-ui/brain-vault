## Current Thesis

Cowen's asymmetric tail curvature quantile model fits Bitcoin's lower and upper price tails with mathematically distinct curvature, superseding his earlier regression rainbow and non-rigorous quantile attempts. As of June 2026, BTC has crossed below Q1 (~$62K), placing it in the historical dislocation zone with conditional bear-case floors mapped at ~$58K, ~$52K, ~$48K, and ~$40K. The June 11 realized-price analysis is consistent with this framework: the realized price (~$53–54K) sits squarely within the Q1 dislocation floor sequence, and BTC has not yet breached it — meaning the model's deeper analog floors (~$52K, ~$48K) remain live targets. Cowen leans toward models indicating the cycle low is not yet in, and the realized-price condition (a sub-$53–54K dip historically required before every cycle bottom) converges with the asymmetric model's lower analog floors as the zone where capitulation may conclude.
