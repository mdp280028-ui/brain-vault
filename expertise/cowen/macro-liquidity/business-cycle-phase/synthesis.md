# Business Cycle Phase

The position of the US economy within its expansion-peak-contraction-trough business cycle, including Cowen's M2-normalized composite (SPX, unemployment, rates, inflation, M2). Cowen overlays the business cycle with Bitcoin's four-year cycle to time long-horizon allocation, treating cycle-bottoms as accumulation windows.

## Current Thesis

Cowen characterizes the US economy as in a late-cycle environment where inflation has remained above the 2% target continuously since 2021 — a structural failure of political will, not a temporary overshoot — and the Fed under Warsh has institutionalized restraint, with ~84.5% market-implied odds of at least one hike by December 2026 and a potential July 29 hike now priced at 60/40 to 65/35. M2 is trending up in 2026, providing a supportive macro floor even as tighter monetary conditions and real disposable income turning negative for the first time in ~2.5 years signal late-cycle stress. SPX is expected to rally into August/September after a shallow June correction before taking a larger drop in the back half of the midterm year — that correction is the catalyst Cowen identifies as the mechanism that rotates capital back into BTC and reopens the crypto bull market. BTC underperformance relative to major asset classes persists through mid-2026, with the accumulation window expected to open in Q4 2026 rather than 2027 as previously framed.

## Key Evidence

- Inflation has been above the 2% target every month since 2021 and never actually touched 2% even at its lowest; Cowen frames this as a choice — political will to allow asset prices to fall (the direct cure) is absent (zKPohkQdq_Q).
- Under Warsh, nine FOMC members project a rate hike before year-end; CME FedWatch prices ~84.5% implied probability of at least one hike by December, with a July 29 hike now separately priced at 60/40 to 65/35 (zKPohkQdq_Q, 5nIhiijBQt4).
- M2 money supply is trending up and to the right in 2026 — Cowen presents this as a supportive macro backdrop, contrasting it with the 2022 bear market when M2 was declining (5nIhiijBQt4).
- Real disposable income has turned negative for the first time in ~2.5 years, consistent with late-cycle consumer stress (5nIhiijBQt4).
- Headline CPI rose to 3.78% YoY in May 2026, driven by a supply-side energy shock — consistent with a late-cycle inflation impulse rather than an early-cycle recovery (Rf-gAQJk5-0).
- SPX is rising on AI-sector earnings rather than liquidity expansion — a late-cycle divergence that structurally advantages equities over crypto, which remains purely liquidity-dependent (dy3E7Jzte88).
