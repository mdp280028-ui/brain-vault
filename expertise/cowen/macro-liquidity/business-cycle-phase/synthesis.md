# Business Cycle Phase

The position of the US economy within its expansion-peak-contraction-trough business cycle, including Cowen's M2-normalized composite (SPX, unemployment, rates, inflation, M2). Cowen overlays the business cycle with Bitcoin's four-year cycle to time long-horizon allocation, treating cycle-bottoms as accumulation windows.

## Current Thesis

Cowen characterizes the US economy as in a late-cycle environment where inflation has remained above the 2% target continuously since 2021 — a structural failure of political will, not a temporary overshoot — and the Fed under Warsh has institutionalized restraint, with ~84.5% market-implied odds of at least one hike by December 2026 and a potential July 29 hike now priced at 60/40 to 65/35. M2 is trending up in 2026, providing a supportive macro floor even as tighter monetary conditions and real disposable income turning negative for the first time in ~2.5 years signal late-cycle stress. The late-cycle macro backdrop is now confirmed at the Bitcoin cycle level: BTC's first weekly close below the 200-week moving average this cycle — mirroring June 2022 and June 2018 — corroborates that the four-year cycle is tracking historical bear-market templates, with the accumulation window (cycle bottom) now expected in late Q3 or early Q4 2026. Cowen's base-case roadmap is a summer low, countertrend rally mid-to-late summer, then a final cycle-bottom drop; he recommends DCA in the second half of midterm years rather than trading the rallies, and explicitly flags that a price-based capitulation event (not yet observed) could form a premature bottom and would cause him to revise the lower-low expectation.

## Key Evidence

- Inflation has been above the 2% target every month since 2021 and never actually touched 2% even at its lowest; Cowen frames this as a choice — political will to allow asset prices to fall (the direct cure) is absent (zKPohkQdq_Q).
- Under Warsh, nine FOMC members project a rate hike before year-end; CME FedWatch prices ~84.5% implied probability of at least one hike by December, with a July 29 hike separately priced at 60/40 to 65/35 (zKPohkQdq_Q, 5nIhiijBQt4).
- M2 money supply is trending up in 2026 — a supportive macro backdrop contrasting with the 2022 bear when M2 was declining — while real disposable income has turned negative for the first time in ~2.5 years, consistent with late-cycle consumer stress (5nIhiijBQt4).
- BTC posted its first weekly close below the 200-week moving average this cycle on 2026-06-29, mirroring the June 2022 and June 2018 lows; every prior bear-market bottom has featured a massive volume capitulation spike not yet observed this cycle (1LI9wfqxOmc).
- The 2026 BTC price structure (low February, higher low late March/early April, lower low June) is a near-exact repeat of the 2018 analog, where the $6,000 level (corresponding to ~$60,000 in 2026 scaling) ultimately marked the cycle bottom (1LI9wfqxOmc).
- SPX is rising on AI-sector earnings rather than liquidity expansion — a late-cycle divergence that structurally advantages equities over crypto, which remains purely liquidity-dependent (dy3E7Jzte88).
