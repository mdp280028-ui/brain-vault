# BTC Cycle Theory

Cowen's framework that Bitcoin and broader risk assets follow an approximately four-year cycle of bull-top, bear-bottom, and accumulation phases. He frames the cycle as macro-driven rather than halving-driven, citing parallel equity-market cycle precedents going back to the 1950s.

## Current Thesis

The Q4 2026 cycle-low thesis is intact, with the June leg now framed around BTC's imminent "date with destiny" with the 200-week MA — expected mid-June — as the structural magnet for the local low, potentially wicking below the February 2026 low before holding. The full roadmap remains: June local low → brief July strength → volatility fade → final Q4 drop, an exact repeat of the 2018 and 2022 sequences. Rally duration is an added timing signal: the current bounce at ~17 weeks is shorter than the ~20–21 weeks that preceded new lows in prior cycles, indicating the low is not yet in. The distilled behavioral rule is unchanged: fade every H1 rally, buy at the end of midterm years, sell at the end of post-halving years — the "this time is different" narrative from the recent rally has been refuted by BTC continuing to track prior cycle weakness precisely.

## Key Evidence

- BTC topped on cycle day 1,162 — within one week of the prior two cycles' tops (days 1,059 and 1,168) — making cycle-day timing precision the single strongest structural argument that the four-year cycle is intact. (7byCfkDQxq8)
- The midterm-year classification formalizes why this year is structurally bearish: BTC dropped in H1 of every midterm year (2014, 2018, 2022, 2026), and all three prior midterm cycles produced a significant June low — making the June-low call a seasonality claim with 100% historical hit rate, caveated at ~70% accuracy overall. (dIa8HUYDNEY)
- The 200-week MA is the recurring June magnet: in 2018 BTC found a local low in June that swept the February low, and in 2022 BTC fell below the 200-week MA in June — confirming it is not always a hard floor but a key structural level BTC must reckon with before bouncing. The current cycle is tracking both instances at 10x the price level. (icDF_qFXATM)
- Rally duration metric adds a timing signal: the current bounce has lasted ~17 weeks, shorter than the ~20–21 weeks that preceded new lows in 2018 and 2022 — indicating the cycle low has not yet been registered. (icDF_qFXATM)
- The current cycle mirrors 2018 at every structural stage: February 2026 low = February 2018 low; higher low in late March/April 2026 = April 2018; early-May 2026 peak = early-May 2018 peak; 200-day MA rejection in 2026 matches 2018 and 2022 precisely. (7byCfkDQxq8, M5d5fbiSfhc, slTIiS-Y65k, dIa8HUYDNEY, icDF_qFXATM)
- Cowen's distilled rule — buy BTC at the end of midterm years, sell at the end of post-halving years — is explicitly stated as the only indicator that ever worked; the bottom is time-based, not price-based, with the behavioral corollary: "Usually, the way to be successful at Bitcoin is to ignore it for the first half of the midterm year. Pretend like it doesn't exist." [12:58] (slTIiS-Y65k, Tj7mvTHDe1U, dIa8HUYDNEY)
