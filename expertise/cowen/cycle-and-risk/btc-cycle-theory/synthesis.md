# BTC Cycle Theory

Cowen's framework that Bitcoin and broader risk assets follow an approximately four-year cycle of bull-top, bear-bottom, and accumulation phases. He frames the cycle as macro-driven rather than halving-driven, citing parallel equity-market cycle precedents going back to the 1950s.

## Current Thesis

BTC (~$64K) remains in the late stage of the 2026 bear market, with Cowen's July memo reinforcing the same multi-cycle day-count and seasonality comparison rather than shifting it: on the ROI-from-low chart BTC sits on day 1333 versus prior bottoms at day 1432/1436 (~100 days out), while the ROI-from-peak-to-low chart (day 283 vs. 364/377/406 for prior post-halving-year bears) suggests more time may still be needed. He continues to pattern-match 2026 to 2018/2022 (February low, higher low in March/April, May lower high, June/early-July lower low, then a July relief rally given back in August/September) and now adds a nuance to his broader framing: market structure most resembles 2019 (cycle top near the end of QT, more time left in the four-year cycle) while social interest most resembles 2018 (~0.25 risk metric, ~350K/day crypto YouTube views vs. ~1M/day in 2022). The price-based-capitulation window he'd flagged for June passed without a capitulation spike, but this was never his base case — time-based capitulation via the day-count models remains his primary framework, with a price-based event (like 2020) as the trigger that would shift his timing. He continues to reject macro-indicator-driven forecasting and top-timing in favor of the four-year cycle, reiterating that DCA'ing beats trying to time the exact bottom.

## Key Evidence

- ROI-from-low day-count: current cycle is on day 1333 (per the new brief; day 1331 per the prior brief), versus the last two cycles bottoming at day 1432 and 1436 — implying a bottom roughly 100 days out, i.e. late September/October, with Cowen flagging October as the strongest candidate and December possible if the bear runs as long as 2014's. (oj2n8nkTTTE, sy6AxBbgico, XQN1M-sIuGs)
- New: ROI-from-peak-to-low day-count shows BTC currently on day 283 since the cycle peak, while the prior three post-halving-year bear markets ended at day 364, 377, and 406 respectively — implying more time may still be needed before a low, a complementary (and slightly more patient) signal than the from-low chart. (XQN1M-sIuGs)
- 2026's price action pattern-matches 2018/2022 at scale: February low, higher low in March/April, lower high in May (2026: at the 200-day MA), lower low in June/early July, followed by a July relief rally historically given back in August/September. (sy6AxBbgico, 8TmOvGK235I, oj2n8nkTTTE, rxekpAAHwRs, XQN1M-sIuGs)
- New: framing split — market structure (liquidity, business cycle, QT timing) most resembles 2019, while social interest (risk metric ~0.25, YouTube views ~350K/day) most resembles 2018 rather than the more euphoric 2022; the price-based-capitulation window that would have hit in June passed without a capitulation, consistent with time-based capitulation remaining the base case. (XQN1M-sIuGs)
- Every prior bear market bottom (2014, 2018, 2022) featured a massive volume capitulation spike that reset all on-chain metrics; that spike has not yet occurred, which remains Cowen's primary reason for rejecting any current bottom call. (psz9FwqWGns, g4FiHz14W14, 1LI9wfqxOmc, 8TmOvGK235I, prior)
- Cowen's distilled rule — ignore BTC in the first half of midterm years, DCA in the second half, sell at the end of post-halving years — remains undefeated across three cycles; he reiterates DCA'ing over bottom-timing and will only abandon the framework if the four-year cycle itself eventually breaks.
