# Global M2 Liquidity

Global M2 money supply as a leading indicator for risk-asset performance. Cowen tracks year-over-year changes in global M2 as a 6-to-12-month lead for Bitcoin and SPX moves; M2 contractions precede risk-off regimes.

## Current Thesis

Global M2 is trending up and to the right in 2026, providing a supportive macro backdrop for risk assets. Cowen uses the 2022 bear market — which coincided with M2 declining — as the mirror-image proof: rising M2 is constructive, falling M2 is the danger signal. The current expansion supports his base case for a crypto bull market return in Q4 2026, though M2 tailwinds alone are not sufficient to override near-term headwinds like a potential Fed rate hike and negative real disposable income.

## Key Evidence

- M2 money supply is trending up and to the right in 2026, which Cowen presents as a supportive macro backdrop for risk assets including Bitcoin (5nIhiijBQt4).
- The 2022 bear market directly coincided with M2 declining — Cowen cites this as the reference case for how M2 contraction drives risk-off regimes (5nIhiijBQt4).
- Cowen connects rising M2 to the structural inflation argument: "going back to what you were saying about the M2 money supply constantly going up. I mean there you are like you know inflation is is is a constant thing." [16:06] (5nIhiijBQt4).
- The anti-cash thesis is framed as a direct consequence of perpetual M2 expansion — holding cash is a losing strategy because inflation continuously erodes purchasing power (5nIhiijBQt4).
