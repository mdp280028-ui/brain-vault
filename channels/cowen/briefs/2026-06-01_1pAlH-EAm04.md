## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin has just closed back below the bear market resistance band — the zone that Cowen had repeatedly flagged as a ceiling in midterm years. The thesis was that rallies through the band would fail to follow through, and that is now playing out.
2. The typical midterm-year pattern, as Cowen described it weeks ago: Bitcoin rallies through the band, tags the 200-day moving average, gets rejected, and falls back below. The sequence is up → tag → back down → tag → tag again → rejection.
3. Cowen explicitly maps the 2025 price structure onto the 2018 analog: February low → higher low in late March/early April → lower high in May → decline into late June. The 2018 version saw BTC bounce at $7,000, recover briefly for a week, then fall further into June before volatility dried up through Q3 and the bear market bottom arrived in Q4.
4. With the band now acting as resistance again, Cowen's short-term path guess: BTC tags $70K, bounces for a few days, then returns to the February lows. He caveats this is a guess, noting short-term price action is "akin to a random walk or geometric Brownian motion."
5. The 2022 pattern is also cited alongside 2018 as a precedent for this rejection structure — both midterm years featured the same bear market resistance band dynamic.
6. Cowen does not offer a year-level directional call in this video; the focus is entirely on the near-term path playing out consistent with the midterm-year cyclical framework he has been articulating.
