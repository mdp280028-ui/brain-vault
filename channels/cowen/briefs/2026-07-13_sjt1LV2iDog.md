## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Gold is trading around $4,000 and, measured as year-to-date ROI from the yearly open, 2026 is tracking almost identically to 2018 and 2022 — prior midterm years.
2. In 2018 and 2022 gold bottomed about 10-11% down from the yearly open; in 2026 gold is only down about 7-8% so far, suggesting limited further downside though a bit more weakness is possible.
3. Gold recently formed a death cross (50-day/200-day), but historically death crosses have preceded short-term rallies (as seen in 2022); gold hasn't even retested its 50-day MA since May 2026.
4. Averaging all midterm years back to the late 1960s/early 1970s (excluding 2018 and 2022), gold bottoms on average in early July of the midterm year; other years show wide variance (2014 bottomed in November, 2010 found weakness into late July, 2006 and 2002 into late June/July, 1998 into late August).
5. Cowen's overall guess is that gold's low forms sometime between July and October 2026.
6. Historical analogy: in 2007-2009, gold bottomed in October 2008 and returned to all-time highs by 2009 (~1.5 years after its top), while the S&P didn't bottom until mid-2009 and took until 2013 to recover — gold recovers from recession-driven drawdowns much faster than equities. A similar pattern held in the 2025 tariff-tantrum scare, when gold barely dropped and recovered quickly.
7. Cowen reiterates his stock market call: no major further equity drop expected until August/September, with a shallow correction in June, a rally in July, and a larger drop expected in the August-September window.
8. Gold's bull market support band (20-month SMA / 21-month EMA, a different band than the one used for Bitcoin) currently sits around $3,800 (3,824-3,841); gold hasn't tagged this band since 2023, and Cowen expects a low within the next few months followed by a bounce off it.
9. Even if the broader gold bull market were over, Cowen would still expect a bounce near the bull market support band, followed by a multi-month rally — meaning if he's "wrong," it would still look similar to being right for a few months.
10. Cowen frames gold's current pullback (~28% from highs) as comparable to the 2006 correction (~26% drop, non-recessionary), and sees gold's decade-long structure as: initial move up early in the decade, consolidation around 2022, further consolidation around 2026, then a final move higher into the end of the decade/early 2030s. He expects gold to come out stronger after the midterm-year struggle, likely with the low in by around the November ITC conference.

## Predictions made
- Gold likely to find its 2026 low sometime between July and October 2026, based on midterm-year seasonality averages.
- Gold's bull market support band (currently ~$3,800-3,841) likely to be tested/tagged within the next few months, followed by a bounce.
- Stock market (S&P) expected to have no major further drop until August/September 2026, following a shallow June correction and a July rally.
- Gold expected to resume strength later in 2026 and especially into 2027, with the low likely in by around the November 20-22, 2026 ITC conference dates.
