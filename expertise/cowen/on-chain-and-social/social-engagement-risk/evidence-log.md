# Evidence log: Social Engagement Risk

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-07-16 (XQN1M-sIuGs): Cowen's social interest risk metric reads ~0.25 as of July 2026, which he matches to July 2018's ~0.25 rather than July 2022's ~0.5, and corroborates with crypto YouTube view counts running around 350K/day now versus roughly 1 million/day in July 2022. He synthesizes this into a split framing: "more similar to 2018 in terms of social interest and in terms of market structure, more similar to 2019 in terms of like liquidity and and where we are in in the business cycle" [06:53].

- 2026-07-20 (OZAQDVkVuG8): Cowen reaffirms the social risk/participation reading at ~0.25, again matching July 2018 rather than July 2022's ~0.5, but this time applies it to Ethereum as a bearish factor: thin social participation means less buying support beneath ETH, making a deeper-than-2022 drawdown more likely, and he separately flags "drying" social interest (alongside a possible Fed rate hike) as a reason ETH/BTC could test its support band one more time. Quote: [10:38] "I don't think it's going to be 80%, but it might be a little bit more than 2022 just because social participation is so low right now."
