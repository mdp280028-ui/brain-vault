## Current Thesis

Midterm years are historically the worst year for equities and the typical "reset window" for Bitcoin, with BTC averaging −65% in midterm years and the post-midterm shift to Republican president + Democratic Congress representing the most bearish equity regime historically. The midterm cyclical framework continues to confirm in real time: BTC's performance under Trump's second term is tracking Biden's term almost tick-for-tick at the same cycle day (~−41% vs −43.8% at day 524), and Cowen now uses the Biden-term analog — itself a midterm-year bear market — as an additional cross-reference for the June low → counter-trend rally → Q4 final bottom sequence, with macro headwinds (not politics) driving both periods. The underlying mechanism is macro rather than partisan: inflation, unemployment trends, and a rising dollar suppressed risk assets in both the Biden midterm year and the current Trump second-term midterm year, reinforcing that the presidential cycle framework is a proxy for macro regime, not political outcomes. If the Biden analog holds, BTC bottoms later in 2026 and begins the next bull market into 2027 — consistent with prior midterm playbooks from 2018 and 2022.
