# Bull Market Support Band

A combined indicator (20-week SMA + 21-week EMA) Cowen treats as the bull/bear regime line for Bitcoin. Sustained closes below it signal bear-market continuation; reclaims signal regime shifts. Also applied to SPX and select altcoins.

## Current Thesis

For Bitcoin, the bear market resistance band (~$70–74K as of late June 2026) remains the defining ceiling of the current bear regime: BTC has repeatedly tested it but failed to sustain above it, and Cowen's base case is that it will eventually break lower rather than reclaim the band. BTC is now caught between the band trending down from above and the 200-week MA trending up from below — whichever gives first determines the next leg. Any countertrend rally (most likely in late July if the 200-week MA holds) is treated as a low-conviction within-bear bounce, not a regime shift. For gold (monthly timeframe, 20-month MA / 21-month EMA), the same band structure plays the opposite role — structural bull-market support expected to hold during the current midterm-year correction.

## Key Evidence

- Bitcoin rejected at the 20-week SMA / 21-week EMA band in the current midterm year, matching the pattern from 2014, 2018, 2019, and 2022 midterm bear cycles (dy3E7Jzte88)
- The BTC/SPX ratio was also rejected at the same 20-week SMA / 21-week EMA level, extending the bear-regime signal beyond spot BTC to relative performance (dy3E7Jzte88)
- BTC is now wedged between the bear market resistance band (~$70–74K) trending down and the 200-week MA trending up; a break in either direction is pending, with Cowen's base case favoring the downside (d71wYhwKpFY)
- Bitcoin has not yet traded below its realized price — a condition that historically occurs in bear markets — which Cowen cites as evidence a further leg lower remains likely in 2026 (d71wYhwKpFY)
- The recent BTC rally produced minimal follow-through before retesting the band — far less than 2023 — matching the classic midterm fake-out pattern (slTIiS-Y65k)
- For gold, the 20-month MA / 21-month EMA band held as support throughout the 1970s and 2000s bull markets and is expected to provide support again during the current ~28% midterm-year correction (DoteyQFNfnE)
