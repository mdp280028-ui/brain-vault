# Inflation Outlook

CPI, PPI, and forward inflation expectations. Cowen uses inflation trajectory as the primary constraint on Fed cuts and the key signal for whether monetary policy can ease.

## Current Thesis

Inflation has reaccelerated to 3.78% headline CPI — driven by a supply-side energy shock from Middle East geopolitical conflict pushing oil and energy prices higher — with core CPI at 2.74% and reversing its prior convergence toward 2%. Markets have fully repriced: all 2026–2027 cuts are gone and a 2027 hike is now more likely than a cut. The tighter monetary environment directly suppresses risk assets: Cowen explicitly links the repricing of rate-cut expectations (and the emerging hike risk) to capital rotation out of high-beta assets like ETH toward lower-risk assets like BTC. A potential Bank of Japan rate hike in June 2026 represents an additional tightening catalyst that could trigger a large liquidation event in risk markets.

## Key Evidence

- Headline CPI rose to 3.78% YoY, above the 3.6–3.7% consensus, with housing (3.37%→3.63%) and transportation (6.89%) as primary drivers (Rf-gAQJk5-0)
- Core CPI is at 2.74%, up from a February trough of 2.47%, suggesting inflation is reversing rather than converging to the 2% target (Rf-gAQJk5-0)
- PPI month-over-month spiked sharply (~6%), a level that independently forecloses near-term easing (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Markets have fully priced out cuts through 2026 and 2027 and now assign higher probability to a 2027 hike than a cut — a 180° reversal from the multi-cut path priced at the start of 2026 (Rf-gAQJk5-0, mwrgOz852wI)
- Oil prices are structurally higher through 2026 due to Middle East geopolitical conflict; late-cycle oil surges historically end business cycles by forcing the Fed's hand (FgxAe_NAh5c, Rf-gAQJk5-0, mwrgOz852wI)
- Bank of Japan potentially set to hike in June 2026 — historically coincident with major risk-asset capitulations, adding a second tightening vector beyond the Fed (mwrgOz852wI)
