## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bear markets make fools of both bulls and bears: bulls feel vindicated during multi-month counter-trend rallies and cash in credibility, then get proven wrong when the breakdown finally arrives; bears feel wrong for months even when their thesis is ultimately correct.
2. The current rally from $60K is not unprecedented — in 2018, the February low was not taken out until 19 weeks later (late June); in 2014, it took 25 weeks. As of this video, only 14 weeks have elapsed since the current cycle low, so a new low is not yet overdue.
3. Historical time-to-new-low after an initial bear-market bottom: ~15 weeks (2022), ~19 weeks (2018), ~21 weeks (two instances), ~25 weeks (2014) — which places the next potential low anywhere from mid-June to late July 2026.
4. June is historically a turning point in Bitcoin bear markets — either a significant low (2018, 2022) or a significant high (2014, 2019). Cowen expects one of those two outcomes in June, but states that regardless of which, prices will be lower by October.
5. BTC found support at $60K in 2026, mirroring $6K support in both 2018 and 2019. In both prior cases Bitcoin eventually fell below $4K — once driven by a second-half SPX correction (2018) and once by a pandemic recession (2019/2020). The analog is not exact but the structural pattern is the same.
6. On-chain levels give context for a Q4 target: realized price is ~$54K and the balanced price is ~$39K. In every historical bear market (2011, 2014, 2015, 2018, 2020, 2022), Bitcoin went below both levels. A Q4 print near $40K would be entirely within the historical range.
7. Resistance levels for the current counter-trend high: the 200-day moving average (near current price ~$78K) and the 0.382 Fibonacci retracement (~$85K). The 200-day MA held as resistance in the last two cycles; 2014 and 2019 briefly exceeded it before rolling over. Cowen cannot specify which level caps the move.
8. BTC/Gold ratio shows a well-defined high and a potentially well-defined low, implying Bitcoin falls another ~45% against gold into Q4 while gold continues higher.
9. Bear market price structure is the inverse of bull market structure: in the bull run, Bitcoin trended down for weeks then broke up sharply; in a bear market it breaks down quickly then grinds higher for months — the asymmetry is what causes most participants to feel perpetually wrong and leads to over-confidence in counter-trend positions.
10. Midterm years are historically when Bitcoin underperforms other assets most severely (gold, SPX, international/emerging markets, silver all outperforming BTC year-to-date in 2026), but they are also when the best long-term buying opportunities appear — the buyers who made the most money over Bitcoin's history bought at the end of every midterm year.
