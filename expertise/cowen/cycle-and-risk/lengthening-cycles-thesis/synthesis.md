# Lengthening Cycles Thesis

Cowen's thesis that each Bitcoin cycle takes longer to complete than the prior one, pushing the cycle-top further out and prolonging bear-market drawdowns. Frequently discussed in tension with the diminishing-returns thesis.

## Current Thesis

Cowen holds that the current bear market resolves on a time-based rather than price-based schedule, with the bull market not resuming until late 2026 or early 2027 — placing the recovery roughly 2.5 years after the April 2024 halving and consistent with each successive cycle running longer than the last. The bottoming process itself spans quarters: a June 2026 local low is followed by a July–August counter-trend rally, with the final cycle bottom targeted in October 2026 — a three-phase structure that directly mirrors 2018 and 2022. Two independent day-count metrics (days from prior cycle low; MicroStrategy 98-week bottoming pattern) converge on October 5–19, 2026 as the specific cycle-bottom window, providing quantitative grounding for the time-based thesis. The June low is not considered sufficient to end the bear market unless Bitcoin crashes to ~$40K, reinforcing that time, not price, is the primary gating factor.

## Key Evidence

- The projected bull market resumption of "late 2026 or early 2027" places the next sustained upleg ~2.5 years after the April 2024 halving, longer than post-halving lead times in prior cycles (slTIiS-Y65k)
- Two independent four-year-cycle timing metrics converge on October 2026: the last two cycle bottoms occurred at days 1,424 and 1,432 from the prior low (splitting the difference at 1,428 days from November 21, 2022 = October 19, 2026), and MicroStrategy's 98-week bottoming pattern from its current top points to the week of October 5, 2026 (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- The 2026 bear market sequence has replicated the 2018 analog exactly — February low → late March/April higher low → May lower high → June capitulation — demonstrating the lengthening-cycle playbook is tracking on schedule (QMn7cm4nfYU)
- BTC measured against gold has been declining since December 2024, demonstrating the bear market is already longer than the USD price chart alone suggests (slTIiS-Y65k)
- The midterm-year capitulation pattern (June low → multi-month bounce → Q4 final low) has repeated in 2018 and 2022, showing the bottoming process itself spans quarters rather than weeks (slTIiS-Y65k)
- Cowen's core rule — "buy at the end of midterm years, sell at the end of post-halving years" — structurally encodes a lengthening cycle: the optimal sell window drifts further from each halving as the cycle extends (slTIiS-Y65k)
