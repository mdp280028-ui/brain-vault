# Evidence log: Real Estate Long Rates

Append-only. Newest entries at bottom.

- 2026-05-21 (8PQee-onXrU): Cowen observes that 30-year Treasury yields are approaching or exceeding their October 2023 highs and entertains a secular regime-change thesis: "what if we're just going into a period where rates just are going higher, you know? I mean we were in a period for 30 or 40 years where they went lower. Maybe that the tide has turned on that" [25:11]. The context is oil-shortage inflation driven by depleted strategic petroleum reserves, which he and co-host Guy expect will force rate hikes under incoming Fed Chair Kevin Warsh — reinforcing the higher-for-longer long-rate environment that weighs on real-estate valuations.
