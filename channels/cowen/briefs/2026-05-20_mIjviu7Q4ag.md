## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Stablecoin dominance (USDT + USDC combined) formed a massive 2+ year base and broke out above it — Cowen's core argument is that bases this large almost never produce false breakouts, making the trend structurally durable.
2. The breakout is following an A-B-C-D pattern: (A) sweep the multi-year highs; (B) backtest the breakout zone near the 20-week SMA / 21-week EMA; (C) rally back up; (D) pull back again to retest — stablecoin dominance is currently at point D.
3. The same A-B-C-D pattern already played out on the Bitcoin dominance chart in prior years — dominance repeatedly faked out alt-season bulls on pullbacks but never durably broke down, ultimately trending far higher and longer than almost anyone expected. Cowen treats this as the direct precedent for where stablecoin dominance is headed.
4. Bitcoin dominance excluding stablecoins is still making higher highs; the headline dominance figure is only being suppressed by stablecoin dominance rising so sharply. When the next bull market arrives, Cowen expects capital to rotate out of stablecoins into BTC first, causing headline BTC dominance to surge even higher.
5. In the 2022 bear market, stablecoin dominance repeatedly dipped just below the 21-week EMA / 20-week SMA before bouncing sharply higher — this occurred in March, August, and October 2022. The current dip below those averages and recovery mirrors that behavior exactly.
6. BTC/USD was rejected from the 200-day moving average, consistent with the same level capping countertrend rallies in 2018 and 2022 — Cowen treats this confluence with the stablecoin dominance signal as confirmation of ongoing bear market structure.
7. The 200-day rejection coincided with stablecoin dominance sweeping just below the 21-week EMA before rallying back — Cowen frames the confluence of these two signals as structural, not coincidental.
8. 2018 seasonal analog: BTC put in a February low, a higher low in April, topped at the 200-day MA in May, then made a June low that swept below the February low. Cowen sees the identical sequence unfolding in 2026 and flags it as the most relevant near-term setup.
9. Cowen explicitly states he does not believe the bear market is over, directly contrasting the widespread bullish sentiment and calls for new all-time highs; he raises the rhetorical possibility that the current price action may already be the early stages of another breakdown that most participants have not recognized yet.

## Predictions made
- BTC to form a June 2026 low that sweeps below the February 2026 low (2018 seasonal analog)
- Stablecoin dominance to continue trending higher after completing the current backtest of its breakout zone
