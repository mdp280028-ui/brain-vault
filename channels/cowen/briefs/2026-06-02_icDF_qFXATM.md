## Key points (the distilled video)
1. Bitcoin has a recurring "date with destiny" with the 200-week moving average every few years — Cowen expects the next such test to arrive in mid-June 2026.
2. The 2018 cycle analog is central to his thesis: Bitcoin found a February low (~$6K), rallied to a lower high in May, then put in the cycle low in June. The same pattern is playing out in 2026 at 10x the price level (February low ~$60K, lower high in May 2026).
3. In 2022, Bitcoin also fell below the 200-week MA in June — demonstrating that this indicator is not always a hard floor, just a key magnet.
4. The rally duration metric supports his bearish lean: the current bounce has lasted ~17 weeks, shorter than the ~20–21 weeks it took to put in new lows in 2018 and 2022 — suggesting the low may not be in yet.
5. His base case path: Bitcoin comes down to the 200-week MA in June, potentially wicks below it to take out the February 2026 low, then attempts to hold the 200-week MA going into summer.
6. After the June low, he expects a brief period of strength in July — consistent with July 2018 and July 2022 behavior.
7. Post-July rally, his expectation is that volatility fades before a final leg down in Q4 2026 — the same sequence that played out in both prior cycles.
8. He pushes back on the "this time is different" narrative that prevailed during the recent rally, noting that Bitcoin has continued to track prior cycle weakness despite widespread bullish sentiment.

## Predictions made
- BTC finds a local low sometime in June 2026 (base case: mid-June), potentially wicking below the February 2026 low near the 200-week MA
- Brief BTC strength expected in July 2026
- Final BTC drop anticipated in Q4 2026 after the July rally fades
