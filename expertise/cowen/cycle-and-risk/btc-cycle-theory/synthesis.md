# BTC Cycle Theory

Cowen's framework that Bitcoin and broader risk assets follow an approximately four-year cycle of bull-top, bear-bottom, and accumulation phases. He frames the cycle as macro-driven rather than halving-driven, citing parallel equity-market cycle precedents going back to the 1950s.

## Current Thesis

Cowen maintains a bearish BTC thesis targeting a cycle low in Q4 2026, with the four-year cycle driven by macro conditions — not the halving. He argues the current counter-trend rally (targeting mid-80s / the 38.2% Fibonacci retracement) does not negate the bear market, citing the identical rally-then-lower pattern seen in 2018, 2020, and 2022. The Fed transition to Warsh reinforces the bear case: resurging CPI and spiking PPI make rate cuts politically pressured but practically impossible, with hikes a real risk in 2027. BTC is expected to underperform energy, metals, and international equities throughout the bear phase — a feature, not a bug, of the four-year cycle framework.

## Key Evidence

- The S&P 500 has bottomed approximately every four years going back to the 1950s (1958, 1962, 1966, 1970… 2014, 2018, 2022), demonstrating the cycle predates Bitcoin and is independent of the halving. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- BTC counter-trend rallies in prior cycles (2018, 2020, 2022) each reached approximately the 38.2% Fibonacci retracement before rolling over to new lows — current rally to mid-80s fits the same pattern. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- The 2018 analog is explicitly invoked: September SPX top → December crash coincided with BTC collapsing to ~$3,100; Cowen sees Q3/Q4 2026 SPX second correction as a structural parallel. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Resurging inflation (CPI trending up YoY, PPI MoM spiking ~6%) forecloses a Fed pivot, keeping macro pressure on risk assets into the cycle low. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Cowen's own portfolio — energy, metals, manufacturing, international stocks, emerging markets — is outperforming BTC in real time, consistent with the bear-market phase of the cycle framework. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
- Oil's 21-week EMA has already provided support once; further oil surges later in 2026 would further close the door on rate cuts, extending macro headwinds. (FgxAe_NAh5c)
